# Sensitivity of Isolated Photon Production at TeV Hadron Colliders to the Gluon Distribution in the Proton

## Abstract

The single inclusive spectra of isolated photons measured at high transverse energy in proton-antiproton collisions at sqrt(s) = 1.96 TeV are compared with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD predictions with various parametrizations of the parton distribution functions (PDFs). Within the experimental and theoretical uncertainties, the Tevatron data can be reproduced equally well by the recent CTEQ6.6, MSTW08 and NNPDF1.2 PDF sets. Predictions for isolated photon spectra in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV for central (y = 0) and forward (y = 4) rapidities relevant for LHC experiments are presented. Different proton PDFs result in maximum variations of order +/-30% in the expected ET-differential isolated photon cross sections. The inclusion of the isolated photon data in global PDF fits will place extra independent constraints on the gluon density.

## Key Results

- **All three PDF sets (CTEQ6.6, MSTW08, NNPDF1.2) reproduce Tevatron data** within experimental uncertainties; chi^2/ndf < 1 for all three at mu = ET. MSTW08 gives slightly better agreement overall.
- **PDF sensitivity at the LHC**: Up to +/-30% variation in isolated photon cross sections at forward rapidities (y = 4, ET ~ 5-20 GeV) between different PDFs, much larger than at central rapidity (~+/-10%).
- **Subprocess decomposition (isolated photons)**:
  - At Tevatron (y = 0): qg Compton dominates up to ET ~ 120 GeV; q-qbar annihilation takes over at higher ET (valence quarks).
  - At LHC (y = 0): Compton dominates for all ET > 45 GeV, contributing ~3/4 of isolated photon production.
  - At LHC (y = 4): Fragmentation is 50-80% for all accessible ET (even after isolation cuts), making forward photons less clean for direct gluon constraints.
- **Standard vs Frixione isolation**: At Tevatron kinematics, the smooth-cone (Frixione) isolation gives ~5% lower cross sections than standard cone at low ET, converging at high ET. At LHC forward rapidities, the difference reaches 10-15% at low ET due to larger fragmentation contribution.
- **Scale dependence**: +/-10% variation when mu varies from ET/2 to 2ET, similar for all PDF sets.
- **BFG-I vs BFG-II**: Isolated photon spectra are virtually identical for the two FF sets (the ~60-80% fragmentation suppression by isolation makes the gluon-to-photon FF difference irrelevant).
- **Kinematic reach**: Isolated photon measurements at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC collectively probe x from ~10^{-5} (LHC forward) to ~0.5 (fixed target), providing independent constraints on g(x, Q^2) complementary to DIS and jet data.

## Methodology / Formalism

### Cross section decomposition

The NLO differential cross section (Eq. 1 of paper):

    d sigma = d sigma^dir + d sigma^frag = sum_{a,b} int dx_a dx_b F_a(x_a; mu_F) F_b(x_b; mu_F)
        x [ d hat{sigma}^gamma_ab(p_gamma, x_a, x_b; mu_R, mu_F, mu_ff)
          + sum_c int_{z_min}^1 (dz/z^2) d hat{sigma}^c_ab(p_gamma, x_a, x_b, z; mu_R, mu_F, mu_ff) D^gamma_c(z; mu_ff) ]

Three subprocesses at LO:
1. **Compton**: qg -> gamma q (gluon-sensitive, dominant at RHIC/Tevatron/LHC midrapidity)
2. **Annihilation**: q qbar -> gamma g
3. **Fragmentation**: qq -> qq -> gamma X (suppressed by ~60-80% with isolation)

### JETPHOX settings

- JETPHOX version 1.1
- alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118, N_f = 5 active flavors
- Box diagram gg -> g gamma switched off (few-percent contribution)
- PDFs interfaced via LHAPDF (version 5.7.1)
- Common scale: mu = mu_R = mu_F = mu_ff = ET^gamma (default), varied to ET/2 and 2ET
- BFG-II fragmentation functions (default); BFG-I for comparison

### Isolation criteria studied

**Standard cone** (experimental):
    E_T^had <= E_T^max = epsilon_h * E_T^gamma   inside   R = sqrt((y - y_gamma)^2 + (phi - phi_gamma)^2)

- D0: R = 0.4, epsilon_h = 0.1, |y| < 0.9, ET = 20-300 GeV
- CDF: R = 0.4, E_T^had < 2 GeV, |y| < 1.0, ET = 30-400 GeV

**Smooth cone** (Frixione, Eq. 4):
    E_T^had(r) <= H(r) = epsilon E_T^gamma [ (1 - cos r) / (1 - cos R) ]^n   for all r <= R

with epsilon = 1, n = 1 (completely eliminates fragmentation). The paper compares both prescriptions.

### PDF error propagation

- CTEQ6.6: 44 error sets (22 eigenvector pairs), T = 10 (90% C.L.)
- MSTW08: 40 error sets (20 eigenvector pairs), dynamical tolerance
- NNPDF1.2: 100 replicas, statistical fluctuations

PDF uncertainty bands computed via master formula:

    Delta X = (1/2) sqrt( sum_i [ X(S_i^+) - X(S_i^-) ]^2 )

### Kinematic coverage

At y = 0, the dominant x values probed are x ~ 2 ET / sqrt(s):
- RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV): x ~ 0.05-0.2 for ET = 5-20 GeV
- Tevatron (sqrt(s) = 1.96 TeV): x ~ 0.02-0.3 for ET = 20-300 GeV
- LHC (sqrt(s) = 14 TeV): x ~ 7e-4 to 0.14 for ET = 5-1000 GeV

At forward rapidity y = 4 at LHC: x can reach down to ~10^{-5}.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Validates the JETPHOX framework** for isolated photon predictions and demonstrates its ability to reproduce Tevatron data with multiple modern PDF sets.
- **Demonstrates Compton dominance**: At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and midrapidity (PPG12 kinematics), the Compton process qg -> gamma q dominates the isolated photon yield, making the measurement directly sensitive to g(x, Q^2). This is a key physics motivation for the PPG12 measurement.
- **Fragmentation is small after isolation**: Fig. 3 shows that with standard isolation (R = 0.4, epsilon_h = 0.1), fragmentation is ~10-20% of the total at midrapidity. PPG12 uses tighter isolation (R = 0.3, parametric cut), further suppressing fragmentation.
- **Standard vs smooth isolation**: The ~5% difference at Tevatron high ET validates that the choice of cone vs Frixione isolation has small impact at the PPG12 kinematics. The JETPHOX NLO calculation with standard cone isolation (matching the experimental procedure) is appropriate.
- **BFG-I vs BFG-II indistinguishable**: Confirms that the PPG12 choice of BFG-II fragmentation functions does not introduce significant systematic uncertainty.
- **Scale variation as theory uncertainty**: The +/-10% scale band at Tevatron is consistent with the PPG12 practice of running JETPHOX at mu = 0.5ET, ET, 2ET.
- **PDF uncertainty complementary to scale**: At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (x ~ 0.05-0.35 for PPG12 pT range), different PDF sets show ~5-10% variations, smaller than at LHC forward rapidity but still relevant for precision comparisons.

# Polarized and Unpolarized Prompt Photon Production Beyond the Leading Order

## Summary

Gordon and Vogelsang presented a complete next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD calculation of prompt photon production in hadronic collisions, covering both the unpolarized and polarized cases. The calculation includes the direct (Compton and annihilation) and fragmentation contributions to photon production at O(alpha_s^2). This was one of the key early NLO calculations that established the theoretical framework for comparing prompt photon cross-section measurements to perturbative QCD predictions, and it remains a standard reference for NLO photon production theory at RHIC energies.

## Key Points

- Complete NLO (O(alpha_s^2)) calculation of inclusive prompt photon production in pp and ppbar collisions
- Includes both direct photon production (quark-gluon Compton scattering qg -> q gamma, quark-antiquark annihilation qqbar -> g gamma) and fragmentation contributions (parton -> gamma via photon fragmentation functions)
- Treats the polarized case as well, enabling predictions for double longitudinal spin asymmetry A_LL measurements
- Demonstrates that NLO corrections are significant (K-factors of order 1.3-2 depending on kinematics) and reduce the scale dependence compared to LO
- The fragmentation contribution is non-negligible at moderate pT and must be included for accurate predictions; isolation cuts suppress but do not eliminate it
- Report number DO-TH-93-10, published as PRD 48 (1993) 3136
- Pre-arXiv era publication -- no electronic preprint available

## Relevance to PPG12

- The Gordon-Vogelsang NLO calculation is one of the theory predictions compared to the PPG12 measured isolated photon cross-section at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Already cited in the PPG12 conference note (references.bib key: Gordon:1993qc) alongside JETPHOX (Aurenche et al.)
- Provides the theoretical baseline for understanding the relative contributions of direct and fragmentation photons at RHIC kinematics
- The treatment of fragmentation contributions is particularly relevant since PPG12 uses fixed-cone isolation, which does not fully eliminate the fragmentation component (unlike Frixione smooth-cone isolation)

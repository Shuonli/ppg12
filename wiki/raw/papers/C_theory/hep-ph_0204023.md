# Cross Section of Isolated Prompt Photons in Hadron-Hadron Collisions

## Abstract

The production of isolated prompt photons in hadronic collisions is considered. A general discussion in QCD perturbation theory of the isolation criterion used by hadron collider experiments is presented. The isolation criterion is implemented in a computer programme of the Monte Carlo type, which evaluates the production cross section at next-to-leading order accuracy in perturbative QCD. The calculation includes both the direct and the fragmentation components of the cross section, without any approximation of the dependence on the radius R of the isolation cone. The scale dependence of the isolated cross section, the sensitivity of the cross section to the values of the isolation parameters, and a quantitative comparison between the full R dependence and its small-R approximation are examined.

## Key Results

- **Factorization proof**: Proves to all orders in QCD perturbation theory that the factorization theorem holds for isolated prompt photon production in hadron-hadron collisions using the standard cone criterion (E_T^had <= E_T^max inside R).
- **Complete NLO calculation**: First calculation including both direct (D) and fragmentation (F) contributions at NLO without any small-cone approximation, retaining the full R dependence.
- **Scale dependence**: The isolated cross section decreases by ~16% when the common scale mu = M = M_F varies from pT/2 to 2pT, comparable to the non-isolated case. Isolation does not induce extra scale sensitivity.
- **Small-cone approximation validity**: The small-R approximation overestimates the fragmentation component by ~8% at R = 1.0, but is adequate for R <= 0.4. Below R ~ 0.1, the NLO calculation becomes unreliable (isolated > non-isolated), signaling the need for log(R) resummation.
- **Sensitivity to isolation parameters**: The total cross section (D + F) varies by less than 10% when epsilon_h ranges from 0.067 to 0.333 or when R varies from 0.4 to 0.7 (Tables 1-2).
- **D0 annular isolation**: Also treats the D0 variant where E_T^max is applied to an annular region (R - Delta R to R), showing it satisfies factorization with a modified kinematic structure.

## Methodology / Formalism

### Inclusive cross section decomposition

    sigma(p_gamma) = sum_a int_0^1 (dz/z) hat{sigma}_a(p_gamma/z; mu, M, M_F) D^gamma_a(z; M_F)
                     + hat{sigma}_gamma(p_gamma; mu, M, M_F)

where:
- hat{sigma}_a: partonic cross section for producing parton a (fragmentation mechanism)
- hat{sigma}_gamma: partonic cross section for direct photon (point-like coupling)
- D^gamma_a: parton-to-photon fragmentation functions

### NLO components

Direct at NLO:

    hat{sigma}_gamma = (alpha_s/pi) [ sigma^gamma_Born(M) + (alpha_s/pi)^2 sigma^gamma_HO(mu, M, M_F) ]

Fragmentation at NLO:

    hat{sigma}_a = (alpha_s/pi)^2 [ sigma^a_Born(M) + (alpha_s/pi)^3 sigma^a_HO(mu, M, M_F) ]

### Isolation criterion and factorization

Standard cone isolation: E_T^had <= E_T^max inside (y - y_gamma)^2 + (phi - phi_gamma)^2 <= R^2.

The isolated cross section factorizes as:

    sigma^is(p_gamma; z_c, R) = sum_a int_{z_c}^1 (dz/z) hat{sigma}^{a,is}(p_gamma/z; z_c, R; mu, M, M_F) D^gamma_a(z; M_F)
                                + hat{sigma}^{gamma,is}(p_gamma; z_c, R; mu, M, M_F)

where z_c = 1/(1 + epsilon_h) is the minimum fragmentation variable surviving isolation.

**Key property**: The isolation constraint z > z_c on the fragmentation variable and the additional constraint zx > z_c on the splitting variable (when a gluon is inside the cone) are the only modifications to the inclusive calculation.

### Factorization proof (all orders)

The proof exploits the correspondence between singularities at parton level and non-perturbative effects. The isolation function F^{(n+1)} must satisfy four properties:
1. It is a measurement function on the final state
2. It is infrared safe (soft partons do not change isolation status)
3. It is collinear safe for initial-state collinear singularities
4. Final-state collinear splitting inside the cone: the fragmentation structure is preserved with modified z range

These ensure that the factorized structure of the inclusive cross section carries over to the isolated case, with modified short-distance coefficients.

### Direct contribution with isolation (small-R approximation)

The subtracted contribution for z < z_c inside the cone:

    d sigma^{direct,sub} = (alpha_s/pi)^2 sum_b int_{z_min}^{z_c} (alpha/(2 pi)) e_a^2
        [ 2 ln(R pT_gamma (1-z) / M_F) P_{gamma q}(z) + z ] dz
        x (Ea / d vec{p}_a d eta_b) d hat{sigma}_Born deta_b deta_a pTa dpTa

The choice M_F = R pT_gamma eliminates R-dependence from this expression but transfers it to the fragmentation function.

### Code implementation (DIPHOX-based)

- Combined phase-space slicing (parameters pTm, RTh) and subtraction method
- Phase space divided into: Part I (soft cylinder), Parts II.a/II.b (final-state collinear cones), Part II.c (finite remainder)
- Dimensional regularization (n = 4 - 2 epsilon) for singular parts
- Exact R dependence via subtraction in Parts II.a/II.b: difference between exact and collinear-approximate matrix elements integrated numerically
- Results independent of unphysical parameters pTm and RTh

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Theoretical foundation of JETPHOX**: This paper provides the NLO calculation of isolated photon production implemented in the JETPHOX code that PPG12 uses. The code is based on DIPHOX and extended to single-photon isolation.
- **Validates R = 0.3 cone size**: PPG12 uses R = 0.3 isolation cone. Table 1 shows the NLO calculation is reliable at R = 0.4 (small-cone approximation adequate) and the full R dependence matters little at R >= 0.3, though R = 0.1 is problematic. The reference [31] in the JETPHOX paper (which is this paper) warns that NLO may not be reliable for R <= 0.3, making the PPG12 choice of R = 0.3 borderline -- an important caveat for systematic uncertainty assessment.
- **Fragmentation contribution**: At Tevatron kinematics with isolation, fragmentation is ~12% of total (Table 1 at R = 0.7). At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (lower energy), the fragmentation fraction is somewhat different, and understanding this decomposition helps interpret the JETPHOX theory band.
- **Scale variation prescription**: The ~16% cross-section variation from pT/2 to 2pT motivates the PPG12 practice of running JETPHOX at three scales (mu = 0.5 ET, ET, 2 ET) to define the theory uncertainty band.

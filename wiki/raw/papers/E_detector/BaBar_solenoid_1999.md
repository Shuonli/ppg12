# Design and Testing of the 1.5 T Superconducting Solenoid for the BaBar Detector

## Summary

This paper describes the design, construction, and testing of the 1.5 T superconducting solenoid magnet originally built for the BaBar detector at the PEP-II asymmetric e+e- collider at SLAC. The solenoid is an aluminum-stabilized, indirectly cooled superconducting coil providing a uniform 1.5 T field over a large bore volume. After BaBar completed its physics program, this magnet was transported to BNL and recycled for use in the sPHENIX detector, where it provides the magnetic field for charged particle tracking and serves as the structural backbone around which the calorimeter system is arranged.

## Key Points

- Superconducting solenoid with 1.5 T central field strength
- Aluminum-stabilized NbTi/Cu conductor, indirectly cooled by liquid helium
- Large bore diameter accommodating the inner tracking systems
- Stored energy of ~27 MJ with passive quench protection system
- Designed for continuous operation with high field uniformity (< 5% variation over the tracking volume)
- Report number: SLAC-REPRINT-1998-006
- Original application: BaBar detector for CP violation studies in B meson decays at SLAC PEP-II
- Second life: recycled for sPHENIX at BNL RHIC, saving significant cost compared to building a new magnet

## Relevance to PPG12

- The 1.5 T magnetic field from the BaBar solenoid determines the bending of charged particles in the sPHENIX tracking volume, which affects:
  - Charged particle momentum reconstruction used in track-based isolation
  - The material budget traversed by photons before reaching the EMCal (cryostat wall)
  - The overall detector geometry (calorimeters arranged inside and outside the solenoid cryostat)
- The inner HCal sits inside the solenoid bore, while the outer HCal doubles as the magnetic flux return

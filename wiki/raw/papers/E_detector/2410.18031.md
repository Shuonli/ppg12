# sPHENIX Highlights: First Results from sPHENIX at RHIC

## Abstract

First results from the sPHENIX experiment on the pi0 v2 and dET/deta in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using detector commissioning data during the RHIC 2023 Run are presented. These results are shown across a large centrality range and compared to previous PHENIX and STAR results. These measurements demonstrate the sPHENIX capabilities towards accomplishing the sPHENIX jet physics program. From the ongoing RHIC 2024 Run, p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, a first glimpse of K0_S and Lambda0 obtained by the sPHENIX TPC detector are shown.

## Key Results

### pi0 v2 in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC 2023 Run)
- Measured using EmCal and MBD detectors during commissioning
- EmCal readout region: -0.9 < eta < 1.1 (partially instrumented during 2023 commissioning)
- pi0 reconstructed from diphoton invariant mass M_gamma_gamma
- Selection cuts: chi^2 < 4, E_cluster >= 1 GeV (noise rejection), 2 <= pT <= 5 GeV
- Signal extracted via combined Gaussian + 2nd-order polynomial fit over 0.1 < M_gamma_gamma < 0.35 GeV
- Signal bounds: mu_Gauss +/- 2*sigma_Gauss
- Centrality classes: 0-60% in 10% bins
- Good agreement with PHENIX measurement (PRL 105, 142301, 2010) across all centralities
- MBD used for: minimum-bias triggering, centrality determination, flow reaction plane calculation

### dET/deta in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC 2023 Run)
- Uses all three calorimeters: EmCal, IHCal, OHCal
- MBD used for collision vertex and centrality determination
- EmCal energy calibration: iterative eta-dependent pi0 mass calibration (same as v2 analysis)
- IHCal and OHCal calibration: cosmic ray muon MIP calibrations (early 2024 data), with temperature-dependent corrections for collision conditions
- dET/deta calculated as sum of calorimeter tower ET vs eta per centrality class
- Correction factors fairly constant with centrality
- EmCal-only and HCal-only dET/deta results in agreement (despite seeing different fractions of collision energy)
- Positive eta and negative eta compatible within uncertainties for all calorimeters
- sPHENIX results consistently higher than PHENIX for all centrality bins; agree within uncertainties for mid-central (30-60%)
- sPHENIX results above STAR for 0-10% but agree for other centralities
- Note: preliminary centrality calculation for Run 2023, expected to be updated

### K0_S and Lambda0 in p+p at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (RHIC 2024 Run)
- Measured using TPC-only reconstructed tracks
- ~210k events analyzed (as of 05/28/2024)
- Loose selection cuts, no corrections to measured track trajectory
- Displaced vertex requirement: r(SV) >= 3 cm
- Clear K0_S peak at expected mass (~498 MeV) in pi+ pi- invariant mass
- Clear Lambda0 peak at expected mass (~1116 MeV) in p+/- pi-/+ invariant mass
- Demonstrates TPC tracking capability with uncalibrated data

## Detector Description

### Overview
- Cylindrical spectrometer covering |eta| <= 1.1 and full 2pi azimuth
- Uses former BaBar 1.4 T superconducting solenoid magnet
- Commissioned with first Au+Au data in RHIC 2023 Run

### Tracking System (inside-out radially)

#### MVTX (MAPS Vertex Tracker)
- **Technology**: Monolithic Active Pixel Sensors (MAPS), copy of inner 3 layers of ALICE ITS
- **Pixel pitch**: 27 micron
- **Layer radii**: 2.5, 3.2, 4.0 cm
- **Location**: just outside beryllium beam-pipe
- **Purpose**: collision vertex, displaced vertex of charged tracks, tracking resolution

#### INTT (Silicon Strip Intermediate Tracker)
- **Technology**: silicon strip detector, strip pitch 78 micron in phi
- **Configuration**: two hermetic cylindrical barrels
- **Functions**: (1) track seeding and resolution enhancement, (2) 60 ns timing for single bunch crossing readout -> suppresses pileup background

#### TPC (Time Projection Chamber)
- **Dimensions**: 2.1 m length, 80 cm outer radius, ~4 m^3 gas volume
- **Gas mixture**: Ar-CF4-Isobutane 75/20/5
- **Field cage**: carbon fiber/honeycomb construction, 18 layers of 3 mil kapton for HV insulation (45 kV)
- **Readout**: quad-GEM modules (4 layers of GEM foils + cathode pad plane), 36 modules per end
- **Space point resolution**: < 200 micron
- **Purpose**: high precision momentum measurement of all charged tracks at mid-rapidity

#### TPOT (Time Projection Outer Tracker)
- **Technology**: 8 identical Micromegas modules in 3 sectors
- **Location**: mounted on EmCal at bottom, facing TPC
- **Purpose**: TPC tracking distortion correction

### Electromagnetic Calorimeter (EmCal)
- **Coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi in phi
- **Depth**: 20.1 radiation lengths
- **Hadronic interaction lengths**: 0.83 lambda_I
- **Technology**: tungsten powder absorber with embedded scintillating fibers
- **Tower size**: delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.024 x 0.024
- **Readout**: scintillating fiber light collected by light guide onto four gain-matched SiPMs per tower
- **Purpose**: measure photons, electrons, positrons via electromagnetic showers
- **2023 commissioning readout**: -0.9 < eta < 1.1 (partial)

### Hadronic Calorimeter (HCal)
- **Total hadronic interaction lengths**: 4.9 lambda_I
- **Configuration**: IHCal (inside magnet cryostat, after EmCal) + OHCal (outside magnet), both cylindrical
- **Technology**: sampling calorimeters with aluminum (IHCal) / steel (OHCal) absorber plates + scintillating tiles
- **Tower size**: delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.1 x 0.1
- **Tile angle**: offset from transverse direction to reduce channeling
- **Readout**: scintillating tile light collected into SiPMs

### Global Detectors

#### MBD (Minimum Bias Trigger Detector)
- **Channels**: 128 PMTs (64 per side), arranged in 3 concentric rings, 2pi azimuth
- **Coverage**: 3.61 < |eta| < 4.51
- **Timing resolution**: 50 ps
- **Radiator**: quartz
- **Functions**: MB triggering, centrality selection, event vertex determination

#### sEPD (sPHENIX Event Plane Detector)
- **Configuration**: pair of disks of scintillating tiles, finely segmented in phi
- **Readout**: SiPMs
- **Coverage**: 2 < |eta| < 4.9
- **Purpose**: high-resolution event plane determination

#### ZDC (Zero Degree Calorimeters)
- **Location**: ~18 m from IP, downstream of DX dipole magnets
- **Technology**: interleaved tungsten + PMMA optical fibers, PMT readout
- **Acceptance**: neutral energy within 2 mrad cone about beam direction
- **Purpose**: neutral energy measurement

### EMCal Calibration Procedure (Used in These Analyses)
1. Tower-by-tower energy calibration for uniform phi response
2. Cluster pair finding and diphoton invariant mass reconstruction
3. Iterative eta-dependent calibration: shift pi0 mass peak to expected value based on eta of most energetic tower in higher-energy cluster
4. Iterated until calibration stable for all eta rings
5. HCal: cosmic muon MIP calibration + temperature-dependent corrections

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal calibration chain**: PPG12 uses the same iterative eta-dependent pi0 mass calibration procedure to establish the EMCal energy scale for photon clusters. The calibration quality demonstrated here with 2023 commissioning data is the foundation for the Run 2024 pp data used in PPG12
- **Calorimeter tower ET**: The dET/deta measurement validates the calorimeter response uniformity in eta, which is directly relevant to the PPG12 cluster ET and isolation cone ET measurements
- **EmCal+HCal consistency**: The agreement between EmCal-only and HCal-only dET/deta confirms that both subsystems see the expected fraction of collision energy, validating the combined isolation cone energy sum used in PPG12
- **MBD triggering and vertex**: PPG12 uses the same MBD system for minimum-bias triggering in pp and collision vertex determination, validated here in Au+Au
- **TPC tracking**: The K0_S and Lambda0 reconstruction with the TPC demonstrates the tracking capability that underpins charged-particle track isolation in PPG12's photon isolation procedure
- **INTT pileup suppression**: The INTT's 60 ns timing resolution for single bunch-crossing readout is the same capability used to suppress pileup in PPG12 pp data (relevant to double-interaction studies)
- **pi0 reconstruction**: The pi0 invariant mass reconstruction procedure establishes the cluster-level performance (energy resolution, position resolution) that determines how well isolated single photons are reconstructed in PPG12
- **RHIC 2024 Run**: The paper shows first glimpses of p+p data from the same RHIC 2024 Run that provides PPG12's dataset

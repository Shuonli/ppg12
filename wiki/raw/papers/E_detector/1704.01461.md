# Design and Beam Test Results for the sPHENIX Electromagnetic and Hadronic Calorimeter Prototypes

## Abstract

A prototype of the sPHENIX calorimeter system was tested at the Fermilab Test Beam Facility as experiment T-1044 in the spring of 2016. The EMCal prototype is composed of scintillating fibers embedded in a mixture of tungsten powder and epoxy. The HCal prototype is composed of tilted steel plates alternating with plastic scintillator. The energy resolution for electrons in the EMCal is 2.8% + 15.5%/sqrt(E) and the energy resolution for hadrons in the combined EMCal plus HCal system is 13.5% + 64.9%/sqrt(E). These results demonstrate that the proposed calorimeter system satisfies the sPHENIX specifications.

### EMCal MIP Response
- MIP calibration performed with 120 GeV proton beam traversing towers perpendicularly
- Effective scintillation light attenuation length in blocks: L_eff = 125 +/- 11 cm (much longer than block length)
- Light guide collection efficiency: 71% relative to direct PMT coupling
- Light guide position non-uniformity: ~30% variation across input cross section -> ~20% position-dependent energy response variation
- Cherenkov background in light guides: < 11% of MIP energy (90% CL), upper limit; expected much smaller in nominal orientation

### EMCal Hadron Rejection
- Measured at 8 GeV/c beam momentum with 2.5 x 2.5 cm^2 hodoscope selection
- Rejection factor depends on cluster energy cut value
- Data consistent with pi^- simulation; K^- component expected ~1% at higher momenta
- Muon contribution subtracted using simulation (systematic still under investigation)

### e/pi Response Ratio in HCal
- <E_e>/<E_pi> > 1 for all energies (HCal standalone)
- Energy dependent: increases toward lower energies

### sPHENIX Physics Requirements (Design Targets)
- EMCal resolution: better than 15%/sqrt(E) + 5% (driven by Upsilon -> e+e- separation)
- HCal single-hadron resolution: better than 100%/sqrt(E) (driven by jet energy resolution)
- Jet energy resolution: sigma/E < 120%/sqrt(E) (dominated by underlying event in central Au+Au, not intrinsic resolution)
- EMCal background fluctuation in central Au+Au 0-10%: ~340 MeV per tower cluster

## Detector Description

### EMCal Prototype (1D Projective, 2016)
- **Type**: W/SciFi SPACAL (Spaghetti Calorimeter)
- **Prototype size**: 32 two-tower blocks = 64 towers, delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.2 x 0.2 at mid-rapidity (eta = 0)
- **Block dimensions**: length 13.9 cm (~18 X0), back height 2.39 cm, front height 2.07 cm
- **Projectivity**: 1D (tapered in phi only); 2D version tested in subsequent 2018 beam test
- **Fibers per block**: 1560 (Kuraray SCSF78, single cladding, blue), 30 x 52 triangular lattice, ~1.0 mm center-to-center
- **Fiber outer diameter**: 0.47 mm
- **Absorber**: tungsten powder (THP Technon 100 mesh, >= 95.4% W purity) + EPO-TEK 301 epoxy
- **Block density**: approximately 10 g/cm^3
- **Sampling fraction**: approximately 2.3% (EM showers)
- **Radiation length**: X0 ~ 0.7-0.8 cm
- **Absorber skin**: ~0.5 mm tungsten-epoxy on each side (protects outer fibers)
- **Production sites**: THP (San Diego) and UIUC, both tested in beam
- **Full sPHENIX coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi azimuth

### EMCal Light Collection
- **Light guides**: machined acrylic trapezoidal prism, input 2.64 x 2.36 cm^2 -> output 1.4 x 1.4 cm^2 over 2.54 cm height
- **Light guide efficiency**: 71% relative to direct PMT coupling
- **Light guide uniformity**: ~30% variation across input area
- **Back reflector**: Vikuiti ESR reflective film (two layers, epoxied to back)
- **SiPMs**: Hamamatsu S12572-33-015P, 4 per tower (2x2 array), 40K pixels (15 micron), PDE ~25%, operated at ~4V above breakdown, gain ~2.3x10^5
- **Optical coupling**: GE Silicones RTV615
- **Temperature dependence**: (-3.68 +/- 0.29)%/deg C uncorrected; (0.33 +/- 0.30)%/deg C after offline compensation (effectively zero)

### Inner HCal Prototype
- **Inner radius**: 116 cm (envelope)
- **Outer radius**: 137 cm (envelope)
- **Material**: ASTM A36 Steel absorber plates
- **Absorber plate thickness**: 1.02 cm (inner radius) to 1.47 cm (outer radius)
- **Tilt angle**: 32 deg relative to radius
- **Total absorber plates**: 21
- **Scintillator**: extruded polystyrene tiles, 0.7 cm thick, with embedded 1 mm Kuraray Y11 WLS fiber (single clad)
- **Scintillator dopant**: 1.5% PTP + 0.01% POPOP
- **Reflective coating**: proprietary surface exposure to aromatic solvents (~50 micron)
- **Tile wrapping**: 100 micron Al foil + 30 micron cling-wrap + 100 micron black vinyl tape
- **Gap thickness**: 0.85 cm
- **Sampling fraction**: 0.078 (inner radius) to 0.060 (outer radius)
- **Towers**: 16 total (4 in phi x 4 in eta), 5 tiles summed per tower
- **Fiber-SiPM gap**: 0.75 mm air gap (reduces optical saturation, < 5% variation with 0.2 mm misalignment, < 20% light loss)

### Outer HCal Prototype
- **Inner radius**: 182 cm (envelope)
- **Outer radius**: 269 cm (envelope)
- **Material**: ASTM A36 Steel absorber plates
- **Absorber plate thickness**: 2.62 cm (inner radius) to 4.25 cm (outer radius)
- **Tilt angle**: 12 deg relative to radius (smaller than IHCal to achieve minimum 5.5 lambda_I with four-tile crossing)
- **Total absorber plates**: 21
- **Sampling fraction**: 0.037 (inner radius) to 0.028 (outer radius)
- **Towers**: 16 total (4 in phi x 4 in eta)
- **Tile lateral attenuation length**: approximately 2-2.5 m

### Mock Cryostat
- Three vertical aluminum plates between inner and outer HCal
- Provides ~1.4 X0 material equivalent to sPHENIX solenoid

### Combined System
- **Total hadronic interaction length**: EMCal (~0.83 lambda_I from TDR) + IHCal + OHCal = ~5.5 lambda_I (HCal alone ~4.9 lambda_I from 2410.18031)
- **Hadron shower categories**: FULL (all), HCAL (starts in IHCal or OHCal), HCALOUT (starts in OHCal)
- **Energy balancing**: separate gain factors for EMCal and HCal, optimized to minimize energy-asymmetry dependence

### Electronics and DAQ
- **SiPM signal chain**: passive sum -> preamplifier (common-base transresistance) -> gain selection -> differential driver -> digitizer
- **Gain ranges**: normal and high gain (16x for EMCal/OHCal, 32x for IHCal); both recorded for HCal
- **Digitization**: 60 MHz sampling, 12-bit flash ADC, 24 time slices per event, 4 microsecond pipeline
- **DAQ**: RCDAQ, client-server, best-effort online monitoring, embeds ancillary data (temperature, voltage)
- **Slow control**: interface board (on detector) + controller board (external crate), Ethernet-based, provides SiPM bias control, temperature/leakage compensation, LED/pulse control
- **LED monitoring**: 405 nm LEDs per tower for calibration/monitoring

### Test Beam Setup (Fermilab FTBF, T-1044, Spring 2016)
- **Beam energies**: 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32 GeV (secondary), 120 GeV (primary protons)
- **Beam composition**: mainly electrons, pions, muons (energy dependent); K ~1% at 20-32 GeV
- **Beam momentum spread**: delta_p/p ~ 2% for 2-16 GeV range
- **Beam spot size**: 0.6 cm to several cm (energy dependent)
- **Particle ID**: two differential gaseous Cherenkov counters
- **Position measurement**: hodoscope (8x8 array of 0.5 cm scintillator fingers, SiPM readout)
- **Veto counters**: 4 scintillator counters around hodoscope
- **Reference**: Pb-glass calorimeter (SF-5, 45x15x15 cm^3), resolution 2.0%(delta_p/p) + 1.4% + 5.0%/sqrt(E)
- **EMCal angles tested**: 10 deg (nominal, prevents channeling) and 45 deg

### Simulation
- GEANT4 v4.10.02-patch-01, physics list QGSP_BERT_HP
- Birks' constant kB = 0.0794 mm/MeV
- Full digitization chain: energy deposition -> Birks' law -> visible energy -> SiPM active pixels (Poisson) -> ADC
- EMCal simulation includes fiber attenuation and light guide non-uniformity
- Timing window: 0-60 ns for visible energy summation
- Integrated into sPHENIX analysis framework

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal energy resolution baseline**: The 2.8% + 15.5%/sqrt(E) operational resolution (full tower, 10 deg) is the foundational measurement for the sPHENIX EMCal performance. This directly feeds into the PPG12 energy resolution systematic and the unfolding response matrix for the isolated photon cross-section
- **Position-dependent energy correction**: The ~20% energy variation across a tower from light guide non-uniformity motivates the cluster-level recalibration procedure used in the sPHENIX reconstruction chain. The residual non-uniformity affects the energy scale systematic in PPG12
- **Hadron rejection**: The EMCal's intrinsic ability to reject hadrons via E/p cuts is complementary to the BDT-based shower-shape photon identification used in PPG12. Understanding the hadronic background contamination is important for the ABCD background subtraction
- **HCal for isolation cone**: The combined EMCal+HCal hadronic resolution (13.5% + 64.9%/sqrt(E)) determines how well hadronic energy in the isolation cone is measured. The IHCal is included in PPG12's isolation cone energy sum
- **MIP calibration methodology**: The tower-by-tower MIP calibration technique established here is used for the full sPHENIX EMCal/HCal calibration, directly affecting the photon energy scale in PPG12
- **SiPM temperature compensation**: The offline temperature correction procedure ((0.33 +/- 0.30)%/deg C after compensation) is applied in the full detector operation to maintain energy scale stability
- **Light attenuation**: L_eff = 125 +/- 11 cm in EMCal blocks sets the longitudinal uniformity of shower sampling, relevant to the depth-dependent shower-shape variables used in the BDT

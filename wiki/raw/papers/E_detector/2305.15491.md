# Predictions for the sPHENIX physics program

## Abstract

sPHENIX is a next-generation detector experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, designed for a broad set of jet and heavy-flavor probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma created in heavy ion collisions. In anticipation of the commissioning and first data-taking of the detector in 2023, a RIKEN-BNL Research Center (RBRC) workshop was organized to collect theoretical input and identify compelling aspects of the physics program. This paper compiles theoretical predictions from the workshop participants for jet quenching, heavy flavor and quarkonia, cold QCD, and bulk physics measurements at sPHENIX.

## Key Results

- Projected yields: 1 million jets above 30 GeV and 50,000 direct photons above 20 GeV in 0-10% central Au+Au events over three years of running
- Jet R_AA measurable out to 70 GeV in Au+Au
- Comprehensive theory predictions from JetMed, JETSCAPE, CoLBT-hydro, SCETG, DREENA-A, LIDO, and KSU/QTraj models
- Baseline jet and hadron nuclear modification factors computed with three nPDF sets (EPPS21, nNNPDF3.0, TUJU21): nPDF uncertainties of order 10% at pT = 10 GeV, growing at higher momenta
- Jet substructure (Soft Drop groomed radius, fragmentation functions) predictions for dijet and gamma-jet channels
- JETSCAPE predictions for inclusive jet and charged-jet R_AA at R = 0.4, 0.6, 0.8 in 0-10% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

### Direct Photon and Gamma-Jet Predictions (Sections 3, 6)
- Isolated photon-tagged jet asymmetry z_Jgamma distributions predicted for Au+Au at 200 GeV with R=0.3 jets opposite pT_gamma > 30 GeV photons (Fig. 12 left)
- Steeper falling cross sections at RHIC lead to narrower z_Jgamma in pp and larger broadening/shift in AA compared to LHC
- Jet R_AA opposite 35 GeV direct photon: factor of 2 enhancement predicted near 20 GeV in Au+Au, qualitatively different from LHC (Fig. in Section 3)
- RHIC jet composition: dominated by quark jets (narrower than gluon jets at LHC), leading to larger enhancement of jet shape toward periphery
- Photon-tagged jets provide calibrated initial parton energy, reducing surface bias
- Energy correlator (EEC) predictions: sensitive to QGP resolution scale, computed for E = 30-100 GeV quark jets
- Bayesian inference with JETSCAPE: sensitivity study framework for quantifying impact of future sPHENIX data on qhat extraction

### Cold QCD and pp Physics (Section 6)
- pp running provides vacuum QCD baseline for all AA/pA measurements
- Hadron-in-jet production measurements sensitive to TMD fragmentation functions
- Azimuthal angular correlations in dijets probe Sudakov effects and parton kT
- Natural connection to future EIC physics program at BNL

## Detector Description

### EMCal (Electromagnetic Calorimeter)
- **Technology**: Tungsten absorber + scintillating fiber (W/SciFi, SPACAL design)
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.025 x 0.025
- **Depth**: ~20 radiation lengths (X0)
- **Coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi azimuth
- **Design**: 2-D projective towers

### HCal (Hadronic Calorimeter)
- **Inner HCal (IHCal)**: Aluminum plates + scintillating tiles, inside solenoid bore
- **Outer HCal (OHCal)**: Steel plates + scintillating tiles, doubles as magnetic flux return
- **Combined depth**: ~5 nuclear interaction lengths (lambda_int); EMCal + HCal total ~6 lambda_int
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.1 x 0.1
- **Coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi azimuth

### Tracking
- **MVTX**: MAPS vertex detector, 3 layers, precision vertex identification
- **INTT**: Intermediate silicon tracker, 4 layers, fast timing for bunch crossing association
- **TPC**: Compact Time Projection Chamber, gateless design, large lever arm for momentum
- **TPOT**: TPC Outer Tracker, 8 rectangular panels below TPC for space-charge distortion corrections
- **Combined tracking acceptance**: |eta| < 1.1 for |z_vtx| < 10 cm, full 2pi azimuth
- **Readout**: Continuous streaming mode for all tracking detectors

### Magnet
- **Type**: Superconducting solenoid (BaBar magnet from SLAC)
- **Central field**: 1.4 Tesla (operational; design maximum 1.5 T)
- **Cryostat inner radius**: 140 cm

### Forward Detectors
- **MBD (Minimum Bias Detector)**: Quartz radiator tubes, 3.51 < |eta| < 4.61, minimum-bias triggering and centrality
- **sEPD**: Scintillating plastic tiles, 2.0 < |eta| < 4.9, event plane determination
- **ZDC**: Situated 18 m from IP on each side, neutron measurement for event selection
- **SMD**: Scintillating tiles behind first ZDC module, spatial information, first-order event plane (Psi_1)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal specifications directly govern PPG12 photon measurement**: the tower granularity (0.025 x 0.025) determines cluster shape variables (weta_cogx, wphi_cogx) used in the BDT, and the ~20 X0 depth defines shower containment for the e11/e33, et1-4 tower energy ratios
- **Isolation cone construction**: The hermetic EMCal + HCal coverage at |eta| < 1.1 provides the calorimetric isolation sum used in PPG12's parametric isolation cut (R = 0.3 cone)
- **Projected direct photon yield**: 50,000 photons above 20 GeV in central Au+Au sets the scale for statistical precision; PPG12 measures the pp reference cross-section at similar kinematics
- **Vertex resolution and tracking**: The |z_vtx| < 10 cm acceptance cut and MBD vertex reconstruction directly impact PPG12's cluster kinematics (ET, eta recalculation from vertex)
- **Streaming readout + unbiased triggering in pp**: Enables the high-statistics pp photon sample (16.6 pb^-1 in Run 24) used in PPG12

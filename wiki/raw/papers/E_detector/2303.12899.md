# Disentangling centrality bias and final-state effects in the production of high-pT neutral pions using direct photons in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

PHENIX presents a simultaneous measurement of direct gamma and pi0 production in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV over pT = 7.5 to 18 GeV/c for different event activity classes. Direct photon yields are used to empirically estimate the hard-scattering contribution (N_coll^EXP) without relying on the Glauber model. For minimum-bias d+Au, R_dAu,EXP^pi0 = 0.925 +/- 0.023(stat) +/- 0.15(scale), consistent with unity. The previously observed enhancement of high-pT pi0 production in low-activity events is shown to be a centrality bias in the Glauber framework. For the top 5% highest-activity events, a 20% suppression is observed with 4.5-sigma significance, potentially due to final-state effects.

## Method: N_coll^EXP from Direct Photons

The key innovation is using direct photon yields to define an experimental N_coll that is independent of the Glauber model:

```
N_coll^EXP(pT) = Y_dAu^gamma_dir(pT) / Y_pp^gamma_dir(pT)
```

This assumes:
1. Direct photons do not experience final-state interactions (validated by R_AA^gamma_dir ~ 1 in Au+Au)
2. Cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects on gamma_dir in d+Au are similar or smaller than in Au+Au
3. CNM effects (isospin, shadowing, EMC effect) predicted to reduce high-pT gamma_dir by up to 10%, with similar reduction for pions; the ratio cancels within 5%

The nuclear modification factor is then:

```
R_dAu,EXP^pi0(pT) = Y_dAu^pi0(pT) / (N_coll^EXP * Y_pp^pi0(pT))
```

## Experimental Details

- **Data**: 2016 d+Au run at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, triggered sample of 12.6 x 10^6 events (~50 nb^-1 integrated luminosity), plus 65 x 10^6 minimum-bias events
- **MB trigger**: Coincidence of at least one hit in upstream and downstream Beam-Beam Counters (3.0 < |eta| < 3.9), recording 88 +/- 4% of inelastic cross section
- **Event trigger**: Local energy deposit > 2.4 GeV in EMCal (|eta| < 0.35)
- **Event activity classes**: Based on charged-particle multiplicity in Au-going direction (0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-88%)

### pi0 Reconstruction

- pi0 -> gamma-gamma decay in the EMCal
- Photon candidates identified by cluster shape and timing
- Combinatorial background subtracted from invariant mass distribution
- Raw yield corrected via iterative unfolding to obtain true pi0 yield in |y| < 0.5

### Direct Photon Extraction

- Statistical subtraction method: gamma_dir = gamma_inclusive - gamma_hadron_decay
- Decay photon contribution estimated from measured pi0 yield and known decay kinematics
- gamma_dir/pi0 ratio measured as function of pT for each event activity class

## Key Results

### gamma_dir/pi0 Ratio
- d+Au (0-100%) ratio consistent with pp for all pT
- Low-to-moderate event activity classes: also consistent with pp
- High activity (0-5%): ratio visibly larger than pp, indicating pi0 suppression relative to gamma_dir

### N_coll^EXP vs. N_coll^GL

| Event Class | N_coll^GL | N_coll^EXP |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| 0-100% (MB) | 7.6 +/- 0.4 | 7.6 +/- 0.2 +/- 1.2 |
| 0-5% (highest) | 18.1 +/- 1.2 | 17.3 +/- 0.7 +/- 2.8 |
| 60-88% (lowest) | 3.2 +/- 0.2 | 3.7 +/- 0.2 +/- 0.6 |

- N_coll^EXP/N_coll^GL ratio shows clear trend with event activity: larger than 1 for low activity, decreasing with increasing activity
- This confirms a systematic bias in the Glauber model mapping of event activity to N_coll

### R_dAu,EXP^pi0

| Event Class | <R_dAu,EXP^pi0> |
|-------------|-----------------|
| 0-100% (MB) | 0.92 +/- 0.02 +/- 0.15 |
| 0-5% (highest) | 0.75 +/- 0.03 +/- 0.13 |
| 60-88% (lowest) | 0.94 +/- 0.05 +/- 0.16 |

- **MB**: Consistent with unity (scale uncertainty dominated by pp reference)
- **Low/moderate activity**: Consistent with MB within 5% -- eliminates the previously puzzling enhancement
- **0-5% highest activity**: Suppression quantified by double ratio:
  - R_dAu,EXP^pi0(0-5%) / R_dAu,EXP^pi0(0-100%) = **0.806 +/- 0.042** (4.5-sigma from unity)
  - Corresponding to pT shift delta_pT = 0.213 +/- 0.055 GeV/c at 9 GeV/c

### Comparison with LHC

- The observed suppression (delta_pT ~ 0.2 GeV/c) is smaller than current LHC upper limits on jet energy loss in p+Pb (0.4 GeV/c for 15 GeV/c jets outside R=0.4 cone)
- PHENIX has enhanced sensitivity due to: (i) model-independent method using double ratios, (ii) softer pT spectrum at RHIC, (iii) measuring leading particles rather than partial jet energies

## Systematic Uncertainties

- 16% common scale uncertainty on N_coll^EXP (dominated by pp reference uncertainties)
- Statistical uncertainties: 2-5% on R_dAu,EXP^pi0 depending on event class
- Cancellation in double ratios: systematic uncertainties largely cancel in R_dAu,EXP(i)/R_dAu,EXP(0-100%)
- Potential biases: different Bjorken-x ranges sampled by gamma_dir vs. pi0 at same pT could contribute to apparent suppression

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Direct photon as standard candle**: This paper validates that direct photons are unmodified by nuclear effects (R_AA^gamma_dir ~ 1) and can serve as calibrated probes of hard scattering. PPG12's isolated photon cross-section measurement provides exactly this baseline.
- **gamma_dir/pi0 ratio**: The measured gamma_dir/pi0 ratio in pp at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV provides a cross-check for PPG12's signal-to-background ratio expectations. A signal purity that deviates significantly from the expected gamma/pi0 ratio would indicate issues with the BDT or isolation cuts.
- **Photon identification**: PHENIX's EMCal-based photon identification (cluster shape and timing) at |eta| < 0.35 uses similar physical principles to sPHENIX's CEMC-based approach at |eta| < 0.7, though with different granularity and BDT methodology.
- **pT range overlap**: PHENIX measures 7.5-18 GeV/c, overlapping with PPG12's 8-35 GeV range.
- **Cold nuclear matter effects**: The finding that CNM effects cancel to within 5% in the gamma_dir/pi0 ratio supports using pp photon cross-sections as baseline for future nuclear modification studies at sPHENIX.
- **Centrality bias warning**: The demonstrated Glauber model bias is a cautionary result for any analysis interpreting event activity in terms of N_coll, relevant for future sPHENIX p+Au or d+Au measurements.

# Measurement of medium-induced acoplanarity in central Au-Au and pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using direct-photon+jet and pi0+jet correlations

## Abstract

STAR reports measurements of acoplanarity using semi-inclusive distributions of charged-particle jets recoiling from direct photon (gamma_dir) and pi0 triggers in central Au-Au and pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Significant medium-induced acoplanarity broadening is observed for large but not small recoil jet resolution parameter, corresponding to recoil jet yield enhancement up to a factor of ~20 for trigger-recoil azimuthal separation far from pi. This phenomenology is indicative of the response of the Quark-Gluon Plasma to excitation, but not the scattering of jets off of its quasiparticles. The measurements are not well described by current theoretical models which incorporate jet quenching.

## Experimental Setup

- **Datasets**: pp (2009 RHIC run, 23 pb^-1) and Au-Au (2014 RHIC run, 3.9 nb^-1) at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- **Centrality**: 0-15% highest multiplicity Au-Au collisions selected via charged-particle multiplicity at |eta| < 0.5
- **Trigger particles**: gamma_dir and pi0 candidates with 11 < E_T^trig < 15 GeV
- **Recoil jets**: Charged-particle jets with 10 < p_T,jet^ch < 20 GeV/c, R = 0.2 and 0.5
- **Online trigger**: High-energy single shower in the Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter (BEMC)

### Photon Identification

- **Shower shape discrimination**: EM shower shape measured in the BEMC and its Shower Maximum Detector (BSMD) used to discriminate gamma and pi0-induced showers
- **gamma-rich population**: Contains admixture of pi0; labeled "gamma-rich" (gamma_rich)
- **Direct photon fraction**: 40-80% of gamma_rich population, depending on collision system and E_T^trig
- **Purity determination**: gamma_dir fraction determined using measured rate of nearby correlated charged hadrons. Correction accounts for hadronic decay component and much (but not all) of fragmentation photon contribution.
- **pi0 purity**: Estimated from simulation to be > 95%

### Jet Reconstruction

- Charged-particle tracks in |eta_track| < 1
- Anti-kT algorithm with R = 0.2 and 0.5
- Area-based underlying event subtraction for Au-Au: p_T,jet^reco,ch = p_T,jet^raw - rho * A_jet
- Mixed-event (ME) correction for combinatorial jet background in central Au-Au (not needed for pp)

## Key Results

### Acoplanarity Distribution

- Corrected Y_pT(Delta_phi) distributions measured for gamma_dir and pi0 triggers in both pp and central Au-Au
- Distributions fall steeply away from Delta_phi = pi, with greater yield for R = 0.5 than R = 0.2
- pp baseline described by PYTHIA-6 STAR tune and analytic QCD calculations

### Nuclear Modification Factor I_AA(Delta_phi)

- I_AA = Y_Au-Au / Y_pp (ratio of corrected yields in Au-Au to pp)
- **R = 0.2**: Suppression (I_AA < 1) at large acoplanarity (Delta_phi far from pi)
- **R = 0.5**: Significant enhancement (I_AA >> 1) at large acoplanarity
- At Delta_phi ~ 2.65 rad:
  - pi0 triggers, 15 < p_T,jet^ch < 20 GeV/c: I_AA differs by factor **20 +/- 2** (sys) between R = 0.2 and R = 0.5
  - gamma_dir triggers, 10 < p_T,jet^ch < 15 GeV/c: Factor **3.8 +/- 1.7** difference

### Physical Interpretation

- **R-dependent broadening**: Selective enhancement for R = 0.5 but not R = 0.2 is **inconsistent with** jet scattering off QGP quasiparticles (which would affect all R values equally)
- **Also inconsistent with MPI effects**: MPI would broaden acoplanarity for all R values
- **Consistent with medium response / jet wake**: Diffuse QGP wake or medium response to recoil jet propagation. The large-R jets at large acoplanarity represent the diffuse wake rather than scattered jet fragments.
- Similar marked R-dependent broadening observed by ALICE for h+jet in central Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

### Theory Comparison

- **Analytic QCD + <qL>**: Predicts broadening that varies with path length. Does not fully describe data.
- **Hybrid Model (with/without wake)**: Predicts medium-induced narrowing for both R = 0.2 and R = 0.5, in disagreement with data. Calculations for lower p_T,jet (5-10 GeV/c) with wake show qualitative R-dependent broadening features similar to data.
- **JEWEL with Recoils**: Includes medium-response recoils. Does not fully describe observed R-dependence.
- **Key conclusion**: Current jet quenching models require modification of their underlying physics description to improve agreement with the data.

### Systematic Uncertainties

- Dominant systematic: unfolding procedure
- ME normalization: negligible effect on acoplanarity distributions
- Delta_phi smearing correction: weight matrix w(Delta_phi_true, Delta_phi_meas) from PYTHIA embedding; systematic uncertainty from varying jet fragmentation model is negligible
- Residual correlated uncertainties between Delta_phi bins from unfolding

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Photon identification techniques**: STAR's shower-shape discrimination in the BEMC/BSMD is analogous to PPG12's BDT-based shower shape analysis in the sPHENIX CEMC. Both analyses face the challenge of separating direct photons from pi0-decay photon backgrounds.
- **Purity estimation**: STAR's correlated-hadron method for determining gamma_dir fraction complements PPG12's ABCD method. Both must account for fragmentation photon contamination.
- **Direct photon as calibrated probe**: This measurement uses direct photons as calibrated probes of the QGP, exploiting the fact that photons do not interact with the colored medium. PPG12's cross-section measurement provides the pp baseline for such gamma+jet analyses.
- **pp baseline at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV**: The pp data from this analysis (23 pb^-1) provides a cross-check for photon production rates at the same sqrt(s) as PPG12.
- **PYTHIA-6 STAR tune**: The pp baseline uses the PYTHIA-6 STAR tune rather than PYTHIA-8 Detroit tune. This highlights the importance of tune choice for photon+jet predictions.
- **Isolation**: While this analysis uses a shower-shape-based photon tag rather than isolation, the underlying physics of UE contamination in photon identification is shared with PPG12.

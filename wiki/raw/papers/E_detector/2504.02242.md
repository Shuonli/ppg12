# Measurement of the transverse energy density in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with the sPHENIX detector

## Abstract

This paper reports measurements of the transverse energy per unit pseudorapidity (dET/deta) produced in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, performed with the sPHENIX detector at RHIC. The results cover |eta| < 1.1 and constitute the first such measurement performed using a hadronic calorimeter at RHIC. Measurements are presented for centrality intervals within 0-70%, and the average dET/deta as a function of Npart is compared to HIJING, AMPT, and EPOS4 generators. Results agree with previous PHENIX and STAR measurements, with improved eta granularity and precision in peripheral events.

## Key Results

- dET/deta measured using three independent methods: EMCal-only, HCal-only, and full calorimeter, all yielding consistent results
- First measurement of dET/deta at RHIC using a hadronic calorimeter
- dET/deta values have strong centrality dependence, varying by factor of >10 between 60-70% and 0-5%
- No significant eta dependence observed within |eta| < 1.1
- dET/deta/(0.5 Npart) increases from ~2.5 to ~3.5 GeV per participant pair over reported Npart range
- Compatible with previous PHENIX and STAR measurements; AMPT best describes both magnitude and Npart dependence
- Dataset: 518,000 events within 0-70% centrality range from low-luminosity Au+Au running in RHIC Run 2024
- Magnetic field at nominal 1.4 T operating point

## Detector Description

### EMCal (Electromagnetic Calorimeter)
- **Technology**: Tungsten powder absorber + scintillating fiber (W/SciFi) sampling calorimeter
- **Depth**: ~20 radiation lengths (~0.8 hadronic interaction lengths)
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.025 x 0.025
- **Readout**: Scintillating fiber light collected by light guides, measured with SiPMs
- **Energy resolution (beam test)**: sigma_E/E = 2.8% (constant term) + 15.5%/sqrt(E) (stochastic term) for electrons
- **Additional cluster energy smearing in simulation**: 11.9% needed to match pi0 mass peak width in data
- **Calibration**: In situ pi0 -> gamma-gamma mass peak; relative phi-symmetry calibration + absolute eta-dependent calibration
- **Cluster formation**: Contiguous towers with E > 70 MeV
- **Coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi azimuth

### HCal (Hadronic Calorimeter)
- **IHCal**: Aluminum absorber plates + scintillating tiles, inside 1.4 T solenoid bore
- **OHCal**: Steel absorber plates + scintillating tiles, outside solenoid (also serves as flux return)
- **Combined depth**: EMCal + HCal total nearly 5 hadronic interaction lengths
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.1 x 0.1 (projective in eta)
- **Tile orientation**: Offset angle to reduce channeling of particles through active volume only
- **Readout**: Wavelength-shifting fibers embedded in tiles, detected by SiPMs
- **Energy resolution (beam test, combined EMCal+HCal)**: sigma_E/E = 13.5% (constant term) + 64.9%/sqrt(E) (stochastic term) for hadrons
- **Calibration**: Cosmic ray muon MIP energy deposition (single-tower trigger, offline cuts for azimuthal-direction muon selection); scintillator MIP corrected to full absorber+scintillator response using single-hadron simulation validated by test beam

### Magnet
- **Type**: Superconducting solenoid (BaBar)
- **Nominal operating field**: 1.4 T
- **Location**: Between IHCal and OHCal

### MBD (Minimum Bias Detector)
- **Location**: Forward rapidity, 3.51 < |eta| < 4.61, both sides of IP
- **Channels**: 128 PMTs (64 per side)
- **Functions**: MB triggering (>= 2 PMTs per side), centrality, z-vertex reconstruction
- **Vertex resolution**: Better than 1 cm in central Au+Au
- **Charge calibration**: MIP units
- **Previously used in PHENIX** at different position (3.0 < |eta| < 3.9), now with new readout/trigger electronics

### ZDC (Zero Degree Calorimeters)
- **Location**: 18 m from IP on both sides
- **Technology**: Tungsten alloy + optical fiber (Cherenkov), sampling hadronic calorimeter
- **Calibration**: Single-neutron peak at 100 GeV
- **Use**: Beam-background rejection in MB selection

### Electronics
- **Common readout**: EMCal, HCal SiPMs and MBD, ZDC PMTs share common calorimeter electronics
- **Digitization**: 14-bit precision at 56.4 MHz (6x RHIC bunch crossing rate)
- **Waveform**: 12-sample waveforms for signals above 2-sigma pedestal noise threshold; 2 summary samples otherwise
- **Zero suppression**: Hardware-level at 2-sigma pedestal noise from start of Run 2024; additional offline peak-minus-pedestal algorithm with subsystem-dependent thresholds

### Calorimeter Tower Projective Geometry
- Towers are projective in eta from z = 0 (nominal detector center)
- Events required |z_vtx| < 10 cm from MBD reconstruction
- ET per tower: E_tower * sin(theta_tower), where theta and eta determined w.r.t. vertex position

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal energy resolution (2.8% + 15.5%/sqrt(E))**: This is the authoritative measurement from beam test data for the as-built detector. PPG12 uses this resolution (and the 11.9% additional smearing) to define the energy resolution systematic variations (eres_up/eres_down)
- **EMCal calibration via pi0 mass**: The same pi0-based calibration chain is used for PPG12's EMCal cluster energy. The relative phi-symmetry + absolute eta-dependent calibration ensures uniform response across the barrel
- **HCal calibration via cosmic MIPs**: The HCal energy scale enters PPG12 through the isolation cone sum (EMCal + IHCal + OHCal towers within R=0.3 of the cluster)
- **Combined hadronic resolution (13.5% + 64.9%/sqrt(E))**: Affects the hadronic contribution to the isolation cone energy and the energy scale systematic
- **14-bit digitization at 56.4 MHz**: Sets the dynamic range and time resolution for tower energy reconstruction in the anatreemaker
- **Tower projective geometry from z=0**: PPG12's cluster kinematics (ET = E/cosh(eta)) depend on the vertex-corrected tower positions; the projective assumption is only exact for z_vtx = 0, leading to the vertex-dependent systematic studied in DoubleInteractionCheck.C
- **Correction factors (EMCal ~0.7, HCal ~0.2)**: The relative fractions of EM vs hadronic energy absorbed by each calorimeter subsystem are important for understanding how photon vs jet energy is distributed across the isolation cone
- **Additional EMCal cluster smearing of 11.9%**: Quantifies the data-MC mismatch in resolution, directly relevant to PPG12's simulation of photon cluster energies
- **Zero-suppression thresholds**: The 2-sigma pedestal noise threshold and offline ADC thresholds affect which towers are included in cluster finding and isolation cone sums; noise towers can bias isolation measurements in peripheral events

# Design and Beam Test Results for the 2D Projective sPHENIX Electromagnetic Calorimeter Prototype

## Abstract

A prototype of the sPHENIX electromagnetic calorimeter (EMCal) was tested at the Fermilab Test Beam Facility in Spring 2018 as experiment T-1044. The EMCal prototype corresponds to a solid angle of delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.2 x 0.2 centered at pseudorapidity eta = 1. The prototype consists of scintillating fibers embedded in a mix of tungsten powder and epoxy, with fibers projecting back approximately to the center of the sPHENIX detector, giving 2D projectivity. The energy resolution was found to be sigma(E)/<E> = 3.5(0.1)% + 13.3(0.2)%/sqrt(E) (hodoscope correction) and sigma(E)/<E> = 3.0(0.1)% + 15.4(0.3)%/sqrt(E) (cluster correction), meeting sPHENIX physics requirements.

- Better agreement between data and simulation with tighter position cut (center of tower)

### Key Improvements over 2016 Prototype (1704.01461)

- 2D projective geometry (both eta and phi) vs 1D projective (phi only) in 2016
- Prototype centered at eta = 1 (vs eta = 0 in 2016), testing high-rapidity performance
- 1/sqrt(E) resolution term improved by ~2.5%, constant term increased by ~0.7%
- Linearity improved by ~1%
- Final sPHENIX EMCal design closely follows this 2018 prototype

## Detector Description

### EMCal Prototype Specifications
- **Prototype size**: 4x4 blocks = 8x8 towers, delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.2 x 0.2 at eta = 1
- **Tower segmentation**: delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.024 x 0.024
- **Tower volume**: approximately 2.5 x 2.5 x 14 cm^3
- **Tower area**: approximately (1.1 R_M)^2 where R_M ~ 2.3 cm (Moliere radius)
- **Tower depth**: approximately 20 X0 (radiation length X0 ~ 7 mm)
- **Fibers per block**: 2668 (Saint Gobain BCF-12, diameter 0.47 mm, single cladding)
- **Fiber emission peak**: 435 nm, decay time 3.2 ns, attenuation length >= 1.6 m
- **Absorber**: tungsten powder (THP Technon 100 mesh, 25-150 micron, >= 99% W purity) + EPO-TEK 301 epoxy
- **Block density**: 9.2-9.8 g/cm^3 (approximately 9.5 g/cm^3)
- **Sampling fraction**: approximately 2.1%
- **Geometry**: 2D projective -- blocks tapered in both eta and phi, tilted so fibers do not project directly at IP (reduces channeling)

### Light Collection
- **Light guides**: UV transmitting acrylic, trapezoidal shape, epoxied to front end of blocks
- **Back reflectors**: aluminum, epoxied to back end of blocks
- **SiPMs**: Hamamatsu S12572-015P, 4 per tower (2x2 array), 3x3 mm^2 active area, 40K pixels (15 micron), PDE = 25%
- **SiPM operation**: 4 V above breakdown, gain approximately 2.3x10^5
- **Gain modes**: nominal and high gain (16x)
- **Temperature stability**: held constant within ~0.5 deg C; gain dependence ~1.5%/deg C (negligible contribution to resolution)

### Electronics and DAQ
- **Digitization**: 60 MHz sampling, 12-bit ADC (PHENIX digitizer system)
- **Signal chain**: 4 SiPMs summed per tower -> preamplifier -> shaper -> ADC
- **LED monitoring**: 405 nm LEDs per tower for calibration
- **DAQ**: RCDAQ system

### Test Beam Setup (Fermilab FTBF, T-1044, Spring 2018)
- **Beam energies**: 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28 GeV electrons
- **Beam profile**: Gaussian, energy-dependent (sigma ~2.5 cm for energy scans, ~8 cm for position scans)
- **Beam momentum spread**: delta_p/p ~ 2%
- **Beam rate**: 1 spill/min, up to ~10^5 particles per 4 s spill
- **Particle ID**: two gaseous Cherenkov counters (electrons vs MIPs/hadrons)
- **Position measurement**: hodoscope (16 x 0.5 cm scintillator fingers, 8 vertical + 8 horizontal, SiPM readout)
- **Veto counters**: 4 scintillator+PMT detectors surrounding EMCal acceptance
- **Reference calorimeter**: Pb-glass, 45x15x15 cm^3, resolution ~1.4% + 5.0%/sqrt(E)

### Calibration Procedures
1. **Shower calibration**: 5x5 tower cluster energy normalized to input energy at center of each tower using 8 GeV data
2. **Position-dependent correction**: Two methods -- hodoscope-based (8x8 bins from external hodoscope) and cluster-based (8x8 bins from EMCal cluster position). Improves resolution by 2-3%
3. **Beam profile correction**: Reweighting to make beam position distribution uniform. Changes resolution by 0.1-0.5%

### Simulation
- GEANT4 v4.10.02-patch-02, physics list QGSP_BERT
- Birks' constant kB = 0.0794 mm/MeV
- Includes SiPM saturation (Poisson model), light guide collection efficiency, ADC conversion
- Integrated into sPHENIX analysis framework

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal energy resolution**: The 13-15%/sqrt(E) + 3-3.5% constant term directly governs the energy resolution systematic in PPG12's isolated photon cross-section measurement. The energy-dependent resolution feeds into the unfolding response matrix
- **Tower granularity**: The delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.024 x 0.024 segmentation defines the cluster reconstruction and shower-shape variables (weta_cogx, wphi_cogx, e-ratios, et1-et4) used in the PPG12 BDT
- **2D projectivity**: The 2D projective design ensures electromagnetic showers at |eta| < 0.7 (PPG12 barrel acceptance) are well-contained in a small number of towers, maintaining good signal-to-noise for isolated photon identification
- **Position-dependent energy response**: The ~20% energy variation across a tower (light guide non-uniformity) motivates the position-dependent calibration procedure applied in the full sPHENIX reconstruction. Residual non-uniformity contributes to the energy scale systematic
- **Sampling fraction**: The 2.1% sampling fraction and ~20 X0 depth determine the shower containment, back-leakage correction, and hadronic interaction length (~0.83 lambda_I) relevant for the isolation cone energy measurement
- **SiPM saturation**: Energy-dependent SiPM saturation causes the quadratic nonlinearity term (c ~ -10^-3 GeV^-1), affecting the energy scale at the highest PPG12 pT bins (>25 GeV)

# RHIC Project Overview

## Summary

This paper provides the definitive overview of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory, published as part of the NIM A 499 special volume dedicated to RHIC and its detectors. RHIC is a versatile collider capable of accelerating and colliding heavy ions (up to gold) and polarized protons at center-of-mass energies up to 200 GeV per nucleon pair for heavy ions and 510 GeV for polarized pp. The paper covers the accelerator complex (AGS injector chain, two superconducting rings, six interaction points), the magnet system, RF systems, beam instrumentation, and the achieved luminosity performance. RHIC began operations in 2000 and has supported a rich physics program across four major experiments (BRAHMS, PHOBOS, PHENIX, STAR) and later sPHENIX.

## Key Points

- RHIC design: two independent superconducting storage rings (Blue and Yellow) sharing a common tunnel of 3.83 km circumference at BNL
- Capable of colliding Au+Au, Cu+Cu, d+Au, p+p, and other species; pp collisions can be polarized
- Maximum center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au, sqrt(s) = 510 GeV for pp
- Six interaction regions, four instrumented with experiments during initial running
- Superconducting magnets: 1,740 magnets operating at 4.6 K
- Luminosity for pp at 200 GeV has improved over the years, reaching levels suitable for rare-probe measurements
- Beam crossing angles configurable (0 mrad and 1.5 mrad used in Run 24, relevant to PPG12 pileup studies)
- The AGS serves as the injector, with the Tandem Van de Graaff or EBIS as ion sources and OPPIS/LINAC for polarized protons

## Relevance to PPG12

- PPG12 analyzes pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV delivered by RHIC during Run 24 (2024)
- The integrated luminosity of 16.6 pb^-1 (1.5 mrad crossing angle period) or ~50 pb^-1 (full run) is determined by RHIC beam parameters and MBD trigger efficiency
- Two crossing angle periods (0 mrad: runs 47289-51274; 1.5 mrad: runs 51274-54000) produce different pileup rates (18.7% vs 7.2% double interaction fraction), directly traceable to RHIC beam parameters
- Already cited in both the conference note and analysis note (bib key: HARRISON2003235)

# sPHENIX: An Upgrade Proposal from the PHENIX Collaboration

## Abstract

The PHENIX collaboration proposes a major upgrade to the PHENIX detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. This upgrade, sPHENIX, enables a jet and beauty quarkonia physics program addressing fundamental questions about the nature of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma. The design consists of a 1.5 T superconducting solenoid (BaBar magnet) with silicon tracking, electromagnetic calorimetry, and hadronic calorimetry providing uniform coverage for |eta| < 1.1. The physics goals include jet quenching measurements (dijets, gamma-jet, fragmentation functions), Upsilon family separation, and heavy-flavor tagged jets.

## Key Results

### Physics Requirements (Chapter 2)
- **Jets**: Energy resolution < 120%/sqrt(E_jet) in pp for R = 0.2-0.4; < 150%/sqrt(E_jet) in central Au+Au for R = 0.2
- **Jet energy scale**: Uncertainty < 3%
- **Direct photon**: EMCal resolution < 15%/sqrt(E) required for photon ID
- **EMCal trigger**: Cluster trigger with rejection > 100 for E_gamma > 10 GeV in pp and pA
- **Upsilon**: Track momentum resolution ~1.2% for 4-10 GeV/c, invariant mass resolution ~100 MeV
- **Tracking**: dp/p < 0.2% x p for high-z fragmentation functions
- **DAQ rate**: 15 kHz for Au+Au, capability to sample > 2/3 trillion Au+Au collisions in 22-week run

### Physics Projections (Chapter 1)
- 10 million dijet events with ET > 20 GeV
- Large gamma-jet sample for photon-tagged jet correlations
- Direct photon measurements with preshower option (Appendix A)
- Jet R_AA measurable to 70 GeV

## Detector Description

### Magnet (Section 3.1)
- **Type**: BaBar superconducting solenoid from SLAC
- **Central field**: 1.5 T (design specification)
- **Operating current**: 4596 A
- **Cryostat inner radius**: 140 cm
- **Cryostat outer radius**: 173 cm
- **Cryostat length**: 385 cm
- **Mean radius of windings**: 153 cm
- **Coil length**: 351 cm
- **Material thickness at normal incidence**: ~126 mm Al equivalent
- **Manufacturer**: Ansaldo Energia (now ASG)

### EMCal (Section 3.2)
- **Technology**: Tungsten powder absorber + scintillating fiber (SPACAL, W/SciFi)
- **Fiber diameter**: 0.47 mm on 1 mm centers
- **Final density**: 10.2 g/cm3
- **Radiation length (X0)**: 7 mm
- **Moliere radius**: ~2.3 cm (~15 mm quoted as design target)
- **Depth**: 18 radiation lengths = 12.6 cm radial thickness; total with readout ~25 cm
- **Hadronic interaction length**: ~1 lambda_int
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.024 x 0.024
- **Total channels**: ~25,000 (256 in phi x 96 in eta)
- **Tower size**: ~2 x 2 cm
- **Energy resolution**: < 12%/sqrt(E) demonstrated in beam tests (prototype)
- **Light yield**: ~500 photoelectrons/GeV with SiPM readout
- **Projective design**: Projective in azimuth; non-projective in eta in reference design
- **Readout**: Silicon photomultipliers (SiPMs) or APDs
- **Location**: Inside solenoid bore

### Inner HCal (Section 3.3)
- **Technology**: Non-magnetic metal (aluminum) plates + scintillating tiles
- **Location**: Inside solenoid bore, between EMCal and cryostat
- **Depth**: ~1 nuclear interaction length
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.1 x 0.1
- **Tile orientation**: Tapered plates tilted ~+10 degrees from radial
- **Scintillator**: 7 mm thick tiles with embedded wavelength-shifting fiber
- **Gap width**: 8 mm between absorber plates
- **Number of plates**: 384 tapered plates

### Outer HCal (Section 3.3)
- **Technology**: Steel (iron) plates + scintillating tiles
- **Location**: Outside solenoid cryostat, doubles as magnetic flux return
- **Radial extent**: ~180 cm to 264.5 cm
- **Depth**: ~4 nuclear interaction lengths
- **Segmentation**: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.1 x 0.1; 64 azimuthal wedges, 22 eta slices per segment
- **Total channels**: 1408 x 2 (inner + outer)
- **Tile orientation**: Tapered plates tilted ~-10 degrees (opposite to IHCal), staggered by half-fin thickness
- **Tower size**: ~10 x 10 cm at inner surface
- **Scintillator**: 7 mm thick injection-molded polystyrene tiles with serpentine WLS fiber
- **Light yield**: ~400 photoelectrons/GeV (estimated from T2K tile design, 12 p.e./MIP)
- **Punch-through probability**: 1% for particles > 2 GeV/c at normal incidence

### Combined Calorimeter Performance
- **Total depth**: ~6 nuclear interaction lengths (EMCal ~1 + IHCal ~1 + OHCal ~4)
- **Energy containment**: ~97% for hadrons below 50 GeV/c
- **Single hadron resolution (GEANT4)**: ~100%/sqrt(E) for HCal alone; better than requirements for combined system
- **Jet energy resolution (pp, GEANT4)**: substantially better than 120%/sqrt(E) for R=0.2 and R=0.4 jets (Fig 4.3); consistent with ~1.2x single-particle HCal resolution (CMS-like ratio)
- **No significant quark vs gluon jet resolution difference** despite different fragmentation functions (Fig 4.4)
- **Mean UE energy per EMCal tower**: ~53 MeV in 0-10% central Au+Au (HIJING)
- **Mean UE energy per EMCal 3x3 array**: ~390 MeV in 0-10% central Au+Au
- **UE fluctuation RMS**: ~3.5 GeV in R=0.2 cone, ~7 GeV in R=0.4 cone for central Au+Au
- **Punch-through probability**: 0.6% for protons traversing full calorimeter depth
- **Electronics**: Common SiPM readout, 14-bit ADC at 65 MSPS, temperature-compensated bias
- **SiPM specs**: Hamamatsu S10362-33-25C MPPC, 14,400 pixels (25 um x 25 um), PDE ~36%, gain up to 2.75 x 10^5, temperature coefficient 56 mV/degC on V_br

### Tracking (Section 3.6)
- **Reference design**: 7 layers of silicon tracking detectors
- **Momentum resolution goal**: dp/p ~ 0.2% x p (high-z fragmentation functions)
- **Upsilon resolution**: 1.2% at 4-10 GeV/c
- **DCA resolution**: < 100 microns for electron pT > 4 GeV/c
- **Track reconstruction efficiency**: > 90% for pT > 3 GeV/c
- **Track purity**: > 90% in central Au+Au for pT > 3 GeV/c
- **Coverage**: |eta| < 1.1, full 2pi azimuth

### Forward Detectors
- **Acceptance coverage**: |eta| < 1.1 for all barrel systems (calorimeters and tracking)
- **Calorimeters extend slightly beyond |eta| = 1 for fiducial volume containment**

### Preshower Option (Appendix A.2)
- **Purpose**: Enhance direct photon signal-to-background for pT ~ 10-20 GeV/c by discriminating single photons from close-by pi0 decay pairs
- **Location**: In front of EMCal at r ~ 88 cm
- **Design**: 2 X0 tungsten converter + single-layer silicon pad detector
- **Pad sizes studied**: 5 mm x 5 mm and 10 mm x 10 mm
- **Coverage**: ~25% of full azimuth (cost-driven)
- **Occupancy**: ~3% or less in central Au+Au at 200 GeV
- **Note**: This option was not included in the final sPHENIX build; PPG12 relies on shower-shape BDT and isolation cuts instead

### Physics Projections (Chapter 4 Performance Summary)
- **Inclusive jets**: Statistics to measure R_AA out to 70-80 GeV/c in 0-20% Au+Au
- **Direct photons**: R_AA measurable to ~50 GeV/c (assuming R_AA = 1)
- **b-jets**: R_AA measurable to ~40 GeV/c (assuming 50% tagging efficiency)
- **Charged hadrons**: R_AA measurable to ~50 GeV/c
- **Projected luminosity**: 10 weeks pp (L = 630 pb^-1) + 22 weeks Au+Au (0.6 x 10^12 sampled MB events)
- **Jet unfolding**: Iterative Bayes method demonstrated to recover truth spectrum to better than 10% accuracy

## Relevance to PPG12

- **EMCal energy resolution (12%/sqrt(E))**: Directly determines photon energy measurement precision. PPG12 uses this in the energy scale systematic uncertainty and the energy resolution systematic variation (`eres_up`/`eres_down` configs)
- **EMCal segmentation (0.024 x 0.024)**: Sets the granularity of shower-shape variables used in the BDT (weta_cogx, wphi_cogx, e11/e33, et1-4, e32/e35, tower energy ratios). The ~2 cm tower size and 2.3 cm Moliere radius mean most photon showers are contained in a 3x3 tower cluster
- **EMCal depth (18 X0)**: Ensures full electromagnetic shower containment for photons up to 35 GeV (PPG12 pT range)
- **HCal segmentation (0.1 x 0.1)**: Used for the hadronic component of the isolation cone energy sum
- **Combined calorimeter isolation**: The hermetic EMCal + HCal system provides the calorimetric isolation sum in the R = 0.3 cone; hadronic leakage beyond the calorimeter is negligible at ~97% containment
- **Underlying event energy**: The 53 MeV/tower mean UE in Au+Au is relevant for isolation corrections; in pp (PPG12) the UE is much smaller
- **Direct photon requirement (sigma_E/E < 15%/sqrt(E))**: Explicitly stated as a physics-driven design requirement; PPG12 operates well within this envelope

# An Introduction to PYTHIA 8.2

## Abstract

The PYTHIA program is a standard tool for the generation of events in high-energy collisions, comprising a coherent set of physics models for the evolution from a few-body hard process to a complex multiparticle final state. It contains a library of hard processes, models for initial- and final-state parton showers, matching and merging methods between hard processes and parton showers, multiparton interactions, beam remnants, string fragmentation and particle decays. PYTHIA 8.2 is the second main release after the complete rewrite from Fortran to C++, and has reached maturity to offer a complete replacement for most applications, notably for LHC physics studies.

## Key Physics Models Relevant to Photon Production

### Hard Processes (Section 2.2)

- **QCD processes**: Standard 2->2 hard-QCD processes including open charm/bottom production, plus new 2->3 processes for parton-shower comparisons.
- **Electroweak processes**: Includes **prompt photon production** (gamma+jet and gamma-gamma), single gamma*/Z and W+/- production. The prompt photon processes are the primary signal channel for PPG12.
- **MPI process mix**: In PYTHIA 8, the MPI process list is enlarged beyond QCD 2->2 to include multiple gamma+jet and gamma-gamma processes, colour-singlet and -octet quarkonium production, and electroweak exchanges.
- **Implementation**: Focuses on 2->1 and 2->2 processes; higher multiplicities accessible through resonance decays or external ME generators (MadGraph 5 interface available).

### Parton Distribution Functions (Section 2.4)

- Sixteen built-in proton PDF sets, plus pion, Pomeron, and lepton PDFs.
- Interfaces to LHAPDF5 (Fortran) and LHAPDF6 (C++).
- Default: LO PDF sets preferred (PYTHIA machinery is LO-based). NLO PDFs can be used but require retuning of MPI parameters.
- **Two-PDF option**: Separate PDFs for the hard interaction and for showers/MPI, allowing NLO PDFs for the hard process with LO PDFs for showering.
- The Monash 2013 tune uses NNPDF2.3 LO; the Detroit tune updates to NNPDF3.1 LO.

### Parton Showers: ISR and FSR (Section 2.5)

- Based on dipole-style pT-ordered evolution first introduced in PYTHIA 6.3.
- **Evolution variable**: Transverse momentum, p_perp_evol, defined differently for ISR (spacelike) and FSR (timelike):
  - FSR: p_perp^2 = z(1-z) Q^2 (forward in time, mother -> two daughters)
  - ISR: p_perp^2 = (1-z) Q^2 (backward evolution, evolving parton unresolved into new mother + sister)
- **Splitting kernels**: Standard LO DGLAP: q->qg, g->gg, g->qqbar (QCD), f->f-gamma, gamma->f-fbar (QED).
- **alpha_s**: Effective alpha_s(MZ) specified separately for ISR and FSR. Default renormalization scale is p_perp_evol. Effective PYTHIA value ~0.139 (larger than MSbar 0.1185 due to CMW scheme translation and missing NLO corrections).
- **New in PYTHIA 8**: gamma->qqbar and gamma->l+l- branchings in FSR; optional weak boson (Z0, W+-) emission in ISR and FSR.
- **Matrix element corrections**: Automated first-emission ME corrections for many production and decay processes. For ISR, internal ME corrections for Z/W/H + jet; for other processes, an approximate improved-shower description ensures reasonable behavior at high pT.
- **Gluon polarization effects**: Approximately included via non-isotropic azimuthal angle selection.

### Multiparton Interactions (Section 2.6)

- Natural consequence of composite structure of colliding hadrons.
- **Divergence regulation**: QCD 2->2 cross section diverges as 1/pT^4 at low pT. Regulated by replacement: alpha_s^2/pT^4 -> alpha_s^2(pT^2 + pT0^2) / (pT^2 + pT0^2)^2.
- **Key tunable parameters**: (1) effective pT0, (2) alpha_s(MZ) for MPI, (3) PDF set for MPI parton luminosities, (4) hadron mass distribution in impact-parameter space, (5) colour reconnection strength.
- **Interleaved evolution** (master equation): MPI, ISR, and FSR combined into one common pT-ordered sequence. The next step in event generation is determined by which mechanism (MPI, ISR, or FSR) produces the highest pT.
- **Correlation effects**: Energy/momentum conservation among beam partons; PDFs "squeezed" into remaining available x-range after each MPI.
- **Impact parameter**: More central collisions -> more overlap -> more MPIs. Multiple proton shape profiles available (Gaussian, double-Gaussian, etc.).
- **pT0 energy scaling**: pT0 = pT0_Ref * (sqrt(s) / sqrt(s_Ref))^ecmPow. This parameter and its energy dependence are the primary targets of the Detroit tune.

### String Fragmentation / Hadronisation (Section 2.8)

- Based solely on the Lund string fragmentation framework.
- **Physical picture**: Colour flux tube between q and qbar modeled as massless relativistic string with tension kappa ~ 1 GeV/fm. String breaks by production of new qqbar pairs via quantum tunneling.
- **Flavour suppression**: u:d:s:c ~ 1:1:0.3:10^-11 (charm and bottom production negligible in hadronisation step).
- **Transverse momentum**: Flavour-independent Gaussian spectrum for pT of new qqbar pairs from tunneling.
- **Gluon kinks**: Gluons are kinks on strings carrying energy/momentum with two string pieces attached. Ratio of gluon/quark string forces = 2.
- **Baryon production**: Via diquark mechanism (popcorn model) -- still poorly understood.
- **Junction topologies**: Y-shaped string configurations associated with net baryon number.
- **Fragmentation function**: f(z) shape constrained by left-right symmetry; free parameters determined from data.

### Beam Remnants and Colour Reconnection (Section 2.7)

- **Primordial kT**: Fermi motion of initiator partons inside the hadron, ~few hundred MeV. Width depends on hard process scale.
- **Colour reconnection**: Three models implemented -- MPI-based (default), QCD-based, gluon-move. All aim to minimize total string length by rearranging colour flow between different MPIs.

### Matching and Merging (Section 2.10)

- **Process-specific**: First-order ME corrections, POWHEG matching, MC@NLO matching.
- **Process-independent**: CKKW-L multi-leg merging, MLM jet matching, UMEPS unitarised merging, NL3 and UNLOPS NLO merging, FxFx NLO jet matching.
- These provide consistent combination of matrix-element descriptions at high momentum-transfer scales and parton-shower descriptions at low scales.

### Tunes (Section 2.12)

- Monash 2013 tune is the default since PYTHIA 8.200; covers LEP, Tevatron, and LHC data.
- Previous default: 4C tune (2010, including early 7 TeV LHC data).
- Key tunable parameters: effective alpha_s values (perturbative domain), fragmentation-function parameters, MPI parameters (non-perturbative domain).
- Automated tuning via Professor toolkit.

## Technical Details

- **Language**: C++ (complete rewrite from Fortran 77)
- **License**: GPL v2
- **Performance**: 10-1000 events/second depending on process
- **Energy range**: CM energies > 10 GeV (below this, enters hadronic resonance region)
- **Beam types**: hadron-hadron (p, n, pion, Pomeron) and lepton-lepton. No lepton-hadron or nucleus-nucleus (use external frameworks like Angantyr for heavy-ion).
- **Interfaces**: HepMC, LHAPDF, FastJet, MadGraph 5, Powheg-Box, aMC@NLO

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Signal simulation**: Prompt photon MC samples (photon5, photon10, photon20) generated with PYTHIA 8 hard QCD processes (Compton qg->q-gamma, annihilation qqbar->g-gamma).
- **Background simulation**: Jet MC samples (jet10/15/20/30/50) use QCD 2->2 processes. Fragmentation photons from pi0/eta decay in jet fragmentation are the primary background.
- **Shower shapes**: ISR/FSR determine the spatial extent and substructure of electromagnetic showers in the CEMC, directly affecting the 25 BDT training features (e-ratios, tower fractions, cluster widths).
- **Isolation energy**: MPI and ISR produce underlying event activity that leaks into the isolation cone (R=0.3), affecting the parametric isolation cut. The Detroit tune's improved MPI description at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is critical for realistic isolation modeling.
- **Cluster multiplicity**: String fragmentation determines the particle-level composition of jets, affecting how pi0/eta decays overlap to form merged clusters that mimic photon shower shapes.
- **pT spectrum**: The pT distribution of signal and background determines the cross-section weights and ET-dependent BDT thresholds used in the ABCD method.
- **Detroit tune**: PPG12 MC production uses the Detroit tune (see 2110.09447.md), which retuned the MPI parameters specifically for sqrt(s) = 200 GeV RHIC energies.

# Measurement of charged hadron multiplicity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with the sPHENIX detector

## Abstract

The pseudorapidity distribution of charged hadrons produced in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is measured using data collected by the sPHENIX detector. Charged hadron yields are extracted by counting cluster pairs in the inner and outer layers of the Intermediate Silicon Tracker (INTT), with corrections applied for detector acceptance, reconstruction efficiency, combinatorial pairs, and contributions from secondary decays. The measured distributions cover |eta| < 1.1 across various centralities, and the average pseudorapidity density of charged hadrons at mid-rapidity is compared to predictions from Monte Carlo heavy-ion event generators. This result, featuring full azimuthal coverage at mid-rapidity, is consistent with previous experimental measurements at RHIC.

## Key Results

- dNch/deta measured at mid-rapidity for Au+Au at 200 GeV across centrality classes (0-3% to 70-80%)
- Results consistent with previous PHOBOS, BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR measurements
- Data collected October 2024 with zero magnetic field, 2 mrad beam crossing angle
- Total analyzed: 4.38 x 10^6 minimum-bias events with |z_vtx| < 10 cm
- MB trigger efficiency: 92.0% +3.4/-3.1% (syst.) for inelastic Au+Au events
- z-vertex reconstruction resolution: 0.17 cm (from INTT tracklets)
- Two independent analysis methods (combinatoric and closest-match) yield consistent results

### Glauber Model Parameters (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV)
- sigma_NN = 42 +/- 3 mb
- Nuclear radius = 6.38 (+0.27/-0.13) fm
- Skin depth = 0.535 (+0.020/-0.010) fm

## Detector Description

### INTT (Intermediate Silicon Tracker)
- **Configuration**: Two-layer barrel strip tracker with clamshell geometry
- **Inner layer radii**: 7.2 cm and 7.8 cm (two sensor rings, offset in phi)
- **Outer layer radii**: 9.7 cm and 10.3 cm (two sensor rings)
- **Number of ladders**: 56 total (24 inner, 32 outer)
- **Active area per ladder**: 45.6 cm x 2 cm (4 silicon sensors per ladder)
- **Strip pitch**: 78 micron
- **Strip length**: 16 mm (within +/-13.0 cm of IP) or 20 mm (elsewhere)
- **Channels per ladder**: 6,656
- **Radiation length per ladder**: 1.14% X/X0
- **Coverage**: 2pi in azimuth, |eta| < 1.1 for |z_vtx| < 10 cm
- **Single-hit efficiency**: > 99% (test beam)
- **Purpose in sPHENIX**: Connects MVTX and TPC tracks, provides fast timing for bunch crossing association

### MBD (Minimum Bias Detector)
- **Location**: +/-250 cm from IP along beam axis
- **Coverage**: 3.51 < |eta| < 4.61
- **Channels**: 128 PMTs (64 per side), 3 concentric rings
- **Functions**: MB triggering, centrality determination, z-vertex reconstruction
- **Vertex resolution**: ~0.17 cm (INTT-based) for z; MBD provides coarser initial vertex
- **Calibration**: Charge in MIP units

### ZDC (Zero Degree Calorimeters)
- **Location**: ~18 m from IP on both sides
- **Technology**: Tungsten alloy + optical fiber sampling hadronic calorimeter (Cherenkov light)
- **Calibration**: Single-neutron peak at 100 GeV
- **Purpose**: MB event selection, beam-background rejection

### Material Budget Before INTT
- Beam pipe: 1.8% X/X0
- MVTX: 2.0% X/X0
- Total before INTT: ~3.8% X/X0

### Monte Carlo Generators Used
- HIJING (nominal): Dual Parton Model + Lund string + jet quenching + nuclear shadowing
- AMPT: HIJING initial state + ZPC parton cascade + ART hadron transport
- EPOS4: Gribov-Regge theory, Cronin broadening, parton saturation, collective hadronization

## Relevance to PPG12

- **MBD vertex and triggering**: PPG12 uses the same MBD system for minimum-bias triggering in pp collisions and z-vertex determination. The vertex resolution impacts cluster ET and eta calculation via the CEMC cylinder geometry (radius = 93.5 cm)
- **Glauber centrality framework**: While PPG12 measures pp, the same Glauber + NBD framework validates the MBD charge calibration used for luminosity determination
- **INTT z-vertex**: PPG12's |z_vtx| < 10 cm cut follows the same INTT-based vertex reconstruction validated here
- **ZDC background rejection**: The ZDC criteria for separating hadronic interactions from beam backgrounds are used across all sPHENIX analyses
- **First publication validation**: As sPHENIX's first physics result, this paper validates the detector commissioning, data quality, and MC simulation chain that underpins all subsequent analyses including PPG12

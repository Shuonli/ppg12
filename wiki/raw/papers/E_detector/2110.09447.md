# PYTHIA 8 underlying event tune for RHIC energies (Detroit Tune)

## Abstract

An underlying event (UE) tune for the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator applicable for hadron collisions primarily at sqrt(s) ranges available at RHIC. The new "Detroit" tune shows significant improvements over the default Monash tune in describing experimental data from STAR, PHENIX, and CDF. Both tunes are unable to sufficiently describe forward rapidity pion data at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. The tune is advocated for PYTHIA 8 studies at current and future RHIC experiments (STAR, sPHENIX).

## Tuning Methodology

- **Toolkit**: Professor v2.3.3 parameterization-based approach
- **Starting point**: Default Monash tune with PYTHIA 8.303
- **PDF update**: NNPDF2.3 (Monash) replaced by **NNPDF3.1 LO** with alpha_s(MZ) = 0.130
- **Procedure**: N PYTHIA parameters sampled across provided ranges; each sampling generates MC prediction compared to data. Results parameterized by 3rd-order polynomial in each bin. Chi2 fit using Minuit determines best parameter values.
- **Reference energy changed**: From 7 TeV (Monash) to **200 GeV** (Detroit) to control power-law extrapolation at low sqrt(s) where the functional form varies rapidly.
- **Proton shape**: Changed from default (bProfile=3) to **double Gaussian matter profile** (bProfile=2). Using the default profile produced inadequate description of sqrt(s) = 300 GeV UE data and a global chi2 more than 2x larger.

## Tuned Parameters

| Parameter | Monash (Default) | Detroit |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| MultipartonInteractions:pT0Ref | 2.28 GeV | **1.40 GeV** |
| MultipartonInteractions:ecmPow | 0.215 | **0.135** |
| MultipartonInteractions:coreRadius | 0.4 | **0.56** |
| MultipartonInteractions:coreFraction | 0.5 | **0.78** |
| ColourReconnection:range | 1.8 | **5.4** |

### Physical Meaning of Changes

- **pT0Ref = 1.40 GeV** (at sqrt(s)_Ref = 200 GeV): About 30% larger than the Monash-extrapolated pT0 at 200 GeV. This is the key regularization parameter for the low-pT MPI cross section divergence.
- **ecmPow = 0.135**: ~40% reduction vs. Monash (0.215). The energy scaling pT0 = pT0Ref * (sqrt(s)/sqrt(s)_Ref)^ecmPow now varies much more slowly with sqrt(s), preventing overshoot at higher energies.
- **coreRadius = 0.56, coreFraction = 0.78**: Double-Gaussian proton matter profile parameters. Slightly increased vs. defaults, giving a more concentrated central core with a broader outer component.
- **ColourReconnection:range = 5.4**: 3x larger than Monash default (1.8). Allows more extensive colour reconnection between MPI systems, reducing string lengths and lowering particle multiplicity.

## Eigentunes

A set of 10 eigentunes (5 principal directions, +/- variations) provided for systematic uncertainty studies. These are obtained by diagonalizing the covariance matrix at the best-fit point with tolerance Delta_chi2 = n.d.f./2. Eigentune parameter ranges:

| Parameter | Range across eigentunes |
|-----------|------------------------|
| pT0Ref | 1.37 -- 1.44 GeV |
| ecmPow | 0.119 -- 0.150 |
| coreRadius | 0.41 -- 0.77 |
| coreFraction | 0.60 -- 0.90 |
| CR:range | 3.61 -- 7.50 |

## Input Data

| Experiment | sqrt(s) (GeV) | Observable |
|------------|---------------|------------|
| STAR | 200 | pi+/- cross sections vs. pT (mid-rapidity) |
| PHENIX | 200 | Drell-Yan di-muon pairs vs. pT |
| STAR | 200 | Average charged particle multiplicities and pT vs. leading jet pT (toward, transverse, away) |
| CDF | 300, 900, 1960 | Charged particle density and sum-pT vs. leading hadron pT (transverse region) |
| STAR | 200 | SoftDrop groomed jet substructure (zg, Rg) |
| STAR | 200 | Inclusive and groomed jet mass |

- Exclusions: pi+/- below pT < 1 GeV/c (potential feed-down effects); data bins where PYTHIA prediction envelope shows extreme variation.
- RIVET analyses prepared for all measurements; publicly available at github.com/star-bnl/star-pythia8-tune.

## Key Results

### Mid-rapidity Performance (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV)
- **pi+/- spectra**: Detroit tune well describes data across full measured pT range. pi+ slightly overpredicted by ~10% above pT = 5 GeV/c.
- **UE multiplicity**: Charged particle multiplicities in toward, away, and transverse regions well described across all leading jet pT. Significant improvement over Monash.
- **Jet substructure**: Improved agreement for SoftDrop groomed observables (zg, Rg) and jet mass distributions. Persistent discrepancies remain in distribution tails.
- **Drell-Yan**: Well described by Detroit; Monash slightly underpredicts.

### Energy Extrapolation
- **CDF sqrt(s) = 300, 900, 1960 GeV**: Detroit tune describes charged particle multiplicities at all energies. Slight underprediction of pT-sum at sqrt(s) = 300 and 900 GeV in 4-8 GeV/c leading particle pT range (<10%).
- **CMS sqrt(s) = 7, 13 TeV**: Detroit tune consistent with UE data above leading track/jet pT of 5/10 GeV. At low pT, deviations driven by different proton shape function used.
- **CMS CP1 comparison**: Detroit tune comparable or slightly better than CMS CP1 tune at LHC energies.

### Forward Rapidity Limitations
- **BRAHMS pi+/- at y = 2.95, 3.3**: Both Monash and Detroit tunes undershoot data at low and mid pT, are consistent or exceed at high pT.
- **STAR pi0 at 3.4 < eta < 4.0**: Similar discrepancy. Detroit tune does worse than Monash at forward rapidity.
- Unable to simultaneously tune mid- and forward-rapidity data. Forward data favors larger pT0Ref. ISR radiation parameters (SpaceShower:alphaSvalue) explored but insufficient.
- This is a known limitation important for the Electron-Ion Collider program.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Primary MC tune**: All PPG12 PYTHIA 8 simulation at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV uses the Detroit tune. This directly affects:
  - **Isolation cone energy**: MPI and UE activity that falls in the R=0.3 isolation cone. The parametric isolation cut (reco_iso_max = b + s * ET) was calibrated against Detroit-tuned MC.
  - **Jet fragmentation patterns**: Colour reconnection (range=5.4) and MPI (pT0=1.40 GeV) control how background jet events fragment, determining shower shape distributions (e11/e33, et1-4, weta, wphi) used in BDT training.
  - **Prompt photon isolation**: The UE contribution to the isolation cone is an irreducible background. Detroit tune's improved UE description reduces systematic bias in isolation efficiency.
  - **Signal/background yields**: Cross-section weights in MergeSim.C and CalculatePhotonYield.C depend on PYTHIA's total cross sections at 200 GeV.
- **Eigentunes for systematics**: The 10 eigentune variations could in principle be used for generator-level systematic uncertainties, though PPG12 currently focuses on cut-based systematics.
- **Forward rapidity caveat**: The Detroit tune's failure to describe forward rapidity data is not a concern for PPG12, which operates at mid-rapidity (|eta| < 0.7).

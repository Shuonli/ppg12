# Measurement of the inclusive isolated prompt photon cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV with the ATLAS detector

## Abstract

A measurement of the cross section for the inclusive production of isolated prompt photons in proton-proton collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of sqrt(s) = 8 TeV is presented. The measurement covers the pseudorapidity ranges |eta^gamma| < 1.37 and 1.56 <= |eta^gamma| < 2.37 in the transverse energy range 25 < E_T^gamma < 1500 GeV. The results are based on an integrated luminosity of 20.2 fb^-1, recorded by the ATLAS detector at the LHC. Photon candidates are identified by combining information from the calorimeters and the inner tracker. The background is subtracted using a data-driven technique, based on the observed calorimeter shower-shape variables and the deposition of hadronic energy in a narrow cone around the photon candidate. The measured cross sections are compared with leading-order and next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and are found to be in a good agreement over ten orders of magnitude.

## Key Results

- Cross-section measured from 25 GeV to 1500 GeV in four eta bins: |eta| < 0.6, 0.6 <= |eta| < 1.37, 1.56 <= |eta| < 1.81, 1.81 <= |eta| < 2.37
- Luminosity: 20.2 fb^-1 (uncertainty 1.9%)
- First ATLAS measurement at 8 TeV; extends down to 25 GeV (previously 45 GeV) and up to 1500 GeV
- First use of ET-dependent isolation requirement in ATLAS photon measurements
- Signal purity rises from ~60% at 25 GeV to ~100% at ~300 GeV
- NLO JETPHOX with CT10 PDF: data ~20% higher than theory at low ET but consistent within theoretical uncertainties; discrepancy decreases at high ET
- Total integrated cross-sections: 15.6 nb (|eta| < 0.6), 20.2 nb (0.6-1.37), 6.7 nb (1.56-1.81), 14.3 nb (1.81-2.37)
- Good agreement over ten orders of magnitude in cross-section
- PeTeR (NLO+NNNLL resummation, approximately NNLO) and MCFM also compared

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Nine shower-shape variables exploited, tuned separately for converted and unconverted photons and adjusted as function of eta
- MC shower-shape variables shifted to match data; additional correction factors (few percent from unity) for identification efficiency
- Identification efficiency (epsilon_id^MC): high and approximately constant for unconverted photons; drops at high ET (> 150 GeV) for converted photons due to difficulty separating close-by tracks
- Track-based classification used to minimize electrons reconstructed as photons

### Isolation Definition (ET-Dependent -- Key Innovation)
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 using topological clusters of calorimeter cells
- ET-dependent isolation cut: **E_T^iso < 4.8 GeV + 4.2 x 10^-3 x E_T^gamma**
- Non-isolated boundary: E_T^iso > 7.8 GeV + 4.2 x 10^-3 x E_T^gamma (same slope, 3 GeV gap from signal boundary)
- This replaces the fixed cuts (3 or 7 GeV) used previously; optimized to retain high identification efficiency at high ET while maintaining good signal-to-background ratio
- Corrections for photon shower leakage (few hundred MeV to 3-4 GeV, consistent for Pythia and Sherpa)
- Underlying event and pileup correction via jet-area method (event-by-event, typically 1-3 GeV)

### Background Estimation (2D Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Four regions defined in (E_T^iso, identification) plane:
  - A (signal): tight AND isolated
  - B: tight AND non-isolated (E_T^iso > 7.8 + 0.0042*ET)
  - C: orthogonal identification AND isolated
  - D: orthogonal identification AND non-isolated
- 3 GeV separation between isolated and non-isolated boundaries limits signal leakage
- Orthogonal identification: four first-layer shower-shape variables inverted (maximizes hadronic background)
- Signal leakage fractions f_K = N_K^signal / N_A^signal computed in MC (Pythia nominal, Sherpa cross-check)
- R_bkg = N_A^bkg * N_D^bkg / (N_B^bkg * N_C^bkg); nominal = 1 (validated); 10% deviation found in control region test, used as systematic
- Signal yield solved via: N_A^signal = N_A - R_bkg * (N_B - f_B * N_A^signal)(N_C - f_C * N_A^signal) / (N_D - f_D * N_A^signal)
- Signal purity P_signal = N_A^signal / N_A; rises from 60% at 25 GeV to 100% at ~300 GeV
- At highest ET bins with insufficient background events, purity from previous bin used
- Electron fake background (e -> gamma from W/Z): < 0.2% for most ET range, max 0.7%

### Cross-Section and Unfolding
- Cross-section formula: dsigma/dET = (1/L*DeltaET) * (N_gamma * P_signal - N_e->gamma) * (1/epsilon_trig) * (1/epsilon_corr)
- epsilon_corr = bin-by-bin unfolding factor from Pythia (N_signal^MC / N_particle^MC), values 0.8-0.9
- Cross-checked with iterative Bayesian unfolding (4 iterations, convergent)
- Sherpa used as alternative for systematic on unfolding and signal leakage

### Systematic Uncertainties
- **Photon energy scale**: ~1% (dominant at high ET due to steeply falling spectrum), larger in 1.56-1.81 region
- **Direct/fragmentation admixture**: fit of ET distribution replaces arbitrary doubling/removal of fragmentation; significant at low ET
- **R_bkg variation**: 10% deviation applied; dominates at low ET
- **Photon identification efficiency**: 0.5% most of range, up to 2% at low ET and 1-4% above 550 GeV
- **Photon energy resolution**: 0.5%, rising to 1% at highest ET
- **Unfolding (MC generator choice)**: Sherpa vs Pythia comparison, 2% at low ET, falling to ~0 with increasing ET
- **Bootstrap technique**: used for evaluating statistical influence on systematic uncertainties with Gaussian kernel smoothing
- **Luminosity**: 1.9%
- **Negligible**: trigger efficiency (< 0.1%), pileup (validated by splitting dataset), MC isolation shift

### NLO Theory
- JETPHOX 1.2.2 with CT10 PDF (baseline), also MSTW2008NLO, NNPDF2.3, HERAPDF1.5
- BFG set II fragmentation functions
- Scales: mu_R = mu_F = mu_f = ET; varied by factor 2 simultaneously and independently
- Scale uncertainty: 12-20%; PDF uncertainty: 5% at 100 GeV to 15% at 900 GeV
- alpha_s uncertainty: evaluated by +/- 0.002 variation around 0.118
- PeTeR (NLO + NNNLL threshold resummation + EW Sudakov logarithms): approximately NNLO, normalized to JETPHOX for isolation
- MCFM: found to be consistently below JETPHOX even at high ET (under investigation)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ET-dependent isolation**: This paper introduces E_T^iso < 4.8 + 0.0042*ET, the exact functional form that PPG12 adapts for its parametric isolation cut (reco_iso_max = reco_iso_max_b + reco_iso_max_s * ET). The slope+intercept parametrization is borrowed directly.
- **Non-isolated boundary with gap**: The 3 GeV gap between isolated and non-isolated regions (signal boundary at 4.8+0.0042*ET, non-isolated at 7.8+0.0042*ET) is designed to limit signal leakage into background control regions. PPG12 uses an analogous gap structure.
- **Low-ET purity**: The 60% purity at 25 GeV demonstrates the challenge PPG12 faces at its own low-ET boundary (8 GeV), where purity is even lower.
- **R_bkg systematic**: The 10% deviation test for background correlation is directly relevant to PPG12's treatment of ABCD correlation systematics.
- **Bootstrap smoothing**: The technique for reducing statistical fluctuations in systematic uncertainties is a methodological reference for PPG12.
- **Direct/fragmentation admixture fit**: This improved treatment of the fragmentation systematic (vs arbitrary 0-100% variation in the 35 pb^-1 paper) may inform PPG12's approach.

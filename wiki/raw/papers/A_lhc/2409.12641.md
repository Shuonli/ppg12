# Measurement of the inclusive isolated-photon production cross section in pp and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Abstract

The ALICE Collaboration at the CERN LHC has measured the inclusive production cross section of isolated photons at midrapidity as a function of the photon transverse momentum (pT_gamma), in Pb-Pb collisions in different centrality intervals, and in pp collisions, at centre-of-momentum energy per nucleon pair of sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. The photon transverse momentum range is between 10-14 and 40-140 GeV/c, depending on the collision system and on the Pb-Pb centrality class. The result extends to lower pT_gamma than previously published results by the ATLAS and CMS experiments at the same collision energy. The covered pseudorapidity range is |eta_gamma| < 0.67. The isolation selection is based on a charged particle isolation momentum threshold p_T^{iso,ch} = 1.5 GeV/c within a cone of radii R = 0.2 and 0.4. The nuclear modification factor is calculated and found to be consistent with unity in all centrality classes, and also consistent with the HG-PYTHIA model, which describes the event selection and geometry biases that affect the centrality determination in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions. The measurement is compared to next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and to the measurements of isolated photons and Z0 bosons from the CMS experiment, which are all found to be in agreement.

## Key Results

- Isolated photon cross-section measured in pp and five Pb-Pb centrality classes (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-70%, 70-90%) at |eta_gamma| < 0.67
- pT range: 10-14 to 40-140 GeV/c (system/centrality dependent) -- extends to lower pT than ATLAS and CMS measurements at same energy
- Cross-sections agree with JETPHOX NLO pQCD calculations (NNPDF4.0 / nNNPDF3.0 + BFG II FF) in all systems and centrality classes for both R = 0.2 and R = 0.4
- R_AA consistent with unity for 0-70% centrality, confirming photons are unaffected by QGP
- R_AA in 70-90% peripheral centrality tends below unity (~0.8-0.9), consistent with HG-PYTHIA centrality bias model (R_AA = 0.82) and CMS Z0 boson observation
- 50-90% combined centrality class: R_AA below unity at ~2 sigma in 18 < pT < 30 GeV/c range
- Ratio of cross-sections with R = 0.4 over R = 0.2 measured for first time at low pT at LHC: ranges between 0.8 and 1, consistent with JETPHOX NLO (~0.9); no centrality dependence observed
- Cross-section ratios at different sqrt(s) (7/5.02 TeV and 13/5.02 TeV) agree with JETPHOX NLO predictions
- Sharp contrast between isolated photon R_AA ~ 1 and charged particle/pion R_AA ~ 0.1-0.2 in central Pb-Pb demonstrates electroweak probe transparency to QGP

### Purity
- Low pT (10-12 GeV/c): 20-30% in Pb-Pb (due to pi0 contamination), ~10% in pp
- 18-40 GeV/c plateau: ~50-60% in Pb-Pb, ~40% in pp
- Above 40 GeV/c: rising due to merged pi0 rejection; reaching ~80% above 80 GeV/c in central Pb-Pb
- R = 0.2 gives ~10% higher purity than R = 0.4 in central Pb-Pb; reversed ordering in pp

### Efficiency
- Total efficiency (reco x ID x isolation / kappa_iso): 20-40% for 0-10% central Pb-Pb; 50-60% for peripheral and pp
- Large efficiency loss from shower-shape selection in central events (~40% reduction below 40 GeV/c) due to UE contamination broadening cluster shapes
- R = 0.4 gives ~10-15% lower efficiency than R = 0.2 in Pb-Pb due to larger UE fluctuation in cone

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- EMCal-based clustering: seed threshold 500 MeV, aggregation threshold 100 MeV, minimum 2 cells
- Cell aggregation limited to 5 x 5 cells around highest-energy cell for shower-shape calculation
- Shower shape variable sigma^2_{long,5x5}: larger eigenvalue of cluster cell energy distribution in eta-phi plane
  - Signal (single photon): sigma^2_{long,5x5} ~ 0.25
  - Merged pi0 decays: pT-dependent band at sigma^2_{long,5x5} = 0.3-0.9 for 8-20 GeV/c
  - Selection: 0.1 < sigma^2_{long,5x5} < 0.3 for pT > 18 GeV/c; looser pT-dependent cut sigma^2_max = 0.6 - 0.016 x pT below 18 GeV/c in Pb-Pb
  - Lower limit 0.1 suppresses spurious neutron-induced signals
- Charged particle veto (CPV) in Pb-Pb only: cluster-track matching with pT-dependent residual cuts in eta and phi, E_cluster/p_track < 1.7
- Abnormal signal removal: F+ < 0.95

### Isolation Definition
- Charged-particle based isolation: sum of track pT in cone around photon direction
- Two cone radii: R = 0.2 and R = 0.4 (first time both used for same measurement)
- Isolation threshold: p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c (same for all centrality and both radii)
- UE estimation via eta-band method: band at same eta as photon, excluding isolation cone region with gap Delta R_{UE gap} = 0.1
- Median UE density: < 1 GeV/(c rad) in pp, up to > 100 GeV/(c rad) in 0-10% Pb-Pb
- UE subtracted event-by-event; after subtraction, p_T^{iso,ch} distributions centred near zero
- Geometrical correction for clusters with |eta| > 0.5 where cone extends beyond tracking acceptance

### Background Estimation (ABCD Method)
- Four regions defined by shower shape (narrow = 0.1 < sigma^2_{long,5x5} < 0.3 vs wide = sigma^2_{long,5x5} > 0.4) and isolation (isolated: p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c vs anti-isolated: 4 < p_T^{iso,ch} < 25 GeV/c)
- Purity: P = 1 - (N_n^iso / N_n^{anti-iso}) / (N_w^iso / N_w^{anti-iso})_{data} x (B_n^iso / N_n^iso) / (N_w^iso / N_w^{anti-iso})_{MC}
- MC correction factor accounts for correlation between shower shape and isolation
- Correction for jet quenching: jet-jet MC scaled by charged-particle R_AA (ALICE + CMS combined) in Pb-Pb
- Purity fitted with one or two sigmoid functions to reduce point-to-point statistical fluctuations

### Trigger and Luminosity
- MB trigger: V0A AND V0C coincidence
- EMCal L1-gamma triggers: 4 GeV (pp), 5 GeV (peripheral Pb-Pb), 10 GeV (central Pb-Pb)
- Trigger efficiency: 85-97% depending on pT and threshold; ~2% lower for isolated narrow clusters
- Rejection factors: ~45 (0-10% Pb-Pb) to ~1000 (pp)
- Integrated luminosity via EMCal trigger rejection factors and MB cross-sections

### MC Simulation
- PYTHIA 8 (v8.210, Monash 2013 tune): gamma-jet (signal) and jet-jet (background)
- GEANT3 detector simulation
- Pb-Pb: pp simulated events embedded into real minimum-bias Pb-Pb data at cell level (calorimeter) and track level (tracking)
- Cross-talk modelling between EMCal cells included (updated parameterization for Pb-Pb)

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Purity: dominant source overall (10-30%); driven by statistical uncertainties in pp/peripheral, isolation probability correlation in central
- Cross talk / MC tuning: dominant at intermediate pT in central Pb-Pb (up to 10%)
- UE area estimation: 2-5% from method comparison (eta-band vs perpendicular cone vs FastJet)
- UE gap variation: additional systematic from Delta R_UE gap choice
- Shower shape signal range: 1-3% (up to 15% at low pT in pp)
- Supermodule dependence: 3.5%
- Energy scale: 2.1% (from 0.5% EMCal scale uncertainty on steep spectrum)
- Material budget: 2.1%
- CPV: ~2% in central Pb-Pb
- Total cross-section: 10-30% depending on pT and centrality
- R_AA: correlated sources (energy scale, material, SM) cancel; total 5-15%
- Cone radius ratio: 3-5% (strong cancellation of correlated sources)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Low-pT reach**: This measurement extends isolated photon coverage down to 10-14 GeV/c, overlapping with PPG12's lowest bins (8-10 GeV). The purity challenges at low pT (20-30% due to pi0 contamination) and the analysis strategies to handle them are directly informative for PPG12.
- **ABCD method with sigma^2_long**: ALICE uses the sigma^2_{long,5x5} shower shape (larger eigenvalue of energy distribution) analogous to how PPG12 uses BDT score for tight/non-tight classification. The signal region (0.1 < sigma^2_long < 0.3) vs wide region (0.4 < sigma^2_long < 2.0) maps conceptually to PPG12's BDT threshold split.
- **Charged-particle isolation**: ALICE uses charged-track isolation with p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c, which is more robust in high-multiplicity than calorimetric isolation. PPG12 uses calorimetric isolation (R = 0.3) with a parametric ET-dependent cut.
- **Cone radius dependence**: The R = 0.4 / R = 0.2 cross-section ratio (~0.9) demonstrates sensitivity to fragmentation and cone-size choice. PPG12 uses R = 0.3 and should be aware that cone-size effects are at the ~10% level.
- **Centrality bias in peripheral events**: The R_AA suppression in 70-90% Pb-Pb (R_AA ~ 0.8) is NOT physics suppression but a centrality determination bias modelled by HG-PYTHIA. This is important context for any future sPHENIX A+A measurements.
- **Purity sigmoid fitting**: ALICE fits purity with sigmoid functions to smooth statistical fluctuations, similar to PPG12's approach of using parametric fits rather than bin-by-bin purity corrections.
- **UE estimation methods**: Multiple UE methods (eta-band, perpendicular cone, FastJet median) compared and used for systematics; relevant methodology for PPG12's pileup studies.

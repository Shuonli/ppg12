# Measurement of the inclusive isolated prompt photon cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV with the ATLAS detector using 4.6 fb^-1

## Abstract

A measurement of the cross section for the production of isolated prompt photons in pp collisions at a center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is presented. The results are based on an integrated luminosity of 4.6 fb^-1 collected with the ATLAS detector at the LHC. The cross section is measured as a function of photon pseudorapidity eta^gamma and transverse energy E_T^gamma in the kinematic range 100 <= E_T^gamma < 1000 GeV and in the regions |eta^gamma| < 1.37 and 1.52 <= |eta^gamma| < 2.37. The results are compared to leading-order parton-shower Monte Carlo models and next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations. Next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations agree well with the measured cross sections as a function of E_T^gamma and eta^gamma.

## Key Results

- Cross-section measured from 100 GeV to 1 TeV, extending the 35 pb^-1 result by a factor of >100 in integrated luminosity
- Two eta regions: barrel (|eta| < 1.37) and endcap (1.52 <= |eta| < 2.37)
- Total inclusive cross-section for |eta| < 1.37, E_T > 100 GeV: sigma = 236 +/- 2 (stat) +13/-9 (syst) +/- 4 (lumi) pb
- PYTHIA predicts 224 pb; HERWIG predicts 187 pb
- NLO JETPHOX with CT10 PDF: data-to-theory ratio consistent within uncertainties; theory ~5-10% lower at low ET but within theoretical uncertainty band
- Signal purity > 93%, increasing with ET
- PYTHIA describes data well; HERWIG falls below data by 10-20%
- Fragmentation contribution significant at low ET (~20%), decreasing to negligible above 500 GeV

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Tight selection criteria using shower-shape variables in the EM calorimeter
- Separate treatment of converted and unconverted photon candidates
- Shower shapes in MC corrected to match data, with corrections estimated separately for converted/unconverted photons
- Identification efficiency above 93% in both barrel and endcap, determined using data-driven techniques

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 around the photon candidate
- Calorimeter-based: sum of energy in cone from both ECAL and HCAL, excluding 5x7 second-layer ECAL cells around shower barycenter
- Photon shower leakage correction subtracted (function of ET, from single-photon MC)
- Underlying event and pileup correction via jet-area method (Refs. [29, 30])
- Isolation cut: E_T^iso <= 7 GeV (looser than the 3 GeV used in the 35 pb^-1 paper, optimized for signal purity and reconstruction efficiency at high ET)
- Non-isolated defined as E_T^iso > 7 GeV
- Residual mild pileup dependence well modeled in simulation

### Background Estimation (2D Sideband / ABCD Method)
- ABCD equation with signal leakage (Eq. 2):
  N_A^S = N_A - R_BKG * (N_B - c_B * N_A^S)(N_C - c_C * N_A^S) / (N_D - c_D * N_A^S)
  where c_K = N_K^S / N_A^S are the signal leakage fractions
- R_BKG = N_A^BKG * N_D^BKG / (N_B^BKG * N_C^BKG) characterizes isolation-identification correlation in background; nominal R_BKG = 1
- Signal purity > 93%, increasing with ET; determined using the 2D sideband approach
- For endcap 500-600 GeV bin, purity from preceding bin used (too few events)
- Leakage fractions computed from MC; results neglecting leakage or with R_BKG != 1 used for systematic variations

### Cross-Section Extraction
- Bin-by-bin correction factor C_i(gamma) = N_reco / N_particle-level from PYTHIA
- C_i(gamma) accounts for acceptance, smearing, reconstruction efficiency, identification efficiency, and event selection
- Key components: acceptance+smearing (95% barrel, 87% endcap), identification efficiency (>93%), trigger efficiency (~100% above 100 GeV)
- Alternative unfolding cross-check with topological-cluster-based isolation shows < 3% difference

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Isolation cut variation (+/- 700 MeV): < 1% effect on cross-section
- Photon identification efficiency: <= 2% for all ET
- Photon energy scale: 2% at low ET to 6% at large ET (dominant high-ET systematic)
- Photon energy resolution: typically 2%
- MC generator choice (HERWIG vs PYTHIA): 2% at low ET to 4% above 800 GeV
- Background subtraction (alternative techniques, R_BKG variation): studied systematically
- Pileup: cross-sections consistent between low-pileup and high-pileup runs
- Luminosity: 1.8%

### NLO Theory (JETPHOX 1.3)
- CT10 and MSTW2008NLO PDFs via LHAPDF
- BFG set II fragmentation functions
- Scales: mu_R = mu_F = mu_f = ET
- Scale uncertainty: 12-20% (dominant theoretical uncertainty)
- PDF uncertainty (CT10 52 eigenvectors, 68% CL): 5% at 100 GeV to 15% at 900 GeV
- alpha_s uncertainty: ~2%
- Parton-level isolation: E_T < 7 GeV within cone R = 0.4

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD equation**: This paper presents the explicit ABCD signal-leakage equation (Eq. 2) that PPG12 directly implements. The c_B, c_C, c_D leakage fractions and R_BKG correlation parameter are the same mathematical framework.
- **Isolation**: The looser 7 GeV cut (vs 3 GeV in the earlier paper) demonstrates that isolation thresholds must be optimized for the specific ET range. PPG12 uses a parametric ET-dependent cut that generalizes this concept.
- **Purity behavior**: Purity rising from ~93% to near 100% with ET is qualitatively similar to PPG12, though PPG12 operates at much lower ET (8-35 GeV) where purities are lower.
- **Correction factor approach**: The bin-by-bin C_i(gamma) unfolding is analogous to PPG12's efficiency correction, combining reconstruction, identification, and isolation efficiencies.
- **Fragmentation contribution**: The 20% effect at low ET from fragmentation photons is an important consideration for PPG12, where JETPHOX comparisons must account for the same effect at lower sqrt(s).

# Measurement of the Differential Cross Section for Isolated Prompt Photon Production in pp Collisions at 7 TeV

## Abstract

A measurement of the differential cross section for the inclusive production of isolated prompt photons in proton-proton collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of 7 TeV is presented. The data sample corresponds to an integrated luminosity of 36 pb^-1 recorded by the CMS detector at the LHC. The measurement covers the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 2.5 and the transverse energy range 25 < ET < 400 GeV, corresponding to the kinematic region 0.007 < xT < 0.114. Photon candidates are identified with two complementary methods, one based on photon conversions in the silicon tracker and the other on isolated energy deposits in the electromagnetic calorimeter. The measured cross section is presented as a function of ET in four pseudorapidity regions. The next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations are consistent with the measured cross section.

## Key Results

- Differential isolated photon cross section measured in four eta regions: |eta| < 0.9, 0.9 < |eta| < 1.44, 1.57 < |eta| < 2.1, 2.1 < |eta| < 2.5
- ET range: 25-400 GeV (conversion method limited to ET < 200 GeV due to sample size)
- Weighted average of conversion and isolation methods reported as final cross section
- NLO pQCD calculations (JETPHOX 1.3.1_2 with CT10 PDFs, BFG set II FF, scales mu_R = mu_F = mu_f = ET) are consistent with data
- Purity increases with ET: from ~50-60% at 25 GeV to ~95-100% above 100 GeV (isolation method)
- Data extends the previous CMS measurement from ET = 21-300 GeV to 25-400 GeV and from |eta| < 1.45 to |eta| < 2.5

## Methodology

### Photon identification
- ECAL: PbWO4 crystals, 76000 total, granularity 0.0174 x 0.0174 (barrel)
- Energy resolution: better than 0.5% for unconverted photons above 100 GeV; better than 1% in barrel for ET > 20 GeV
- Preselection variables: H/E (HCAL/ECAL ratio < 0.05), sigma_eta_eta (shower shape second moment), Iso_TRK, Iso_ECAL, Iso_HCAL
- Generator-level isolation: sum of ET of all particles in cone R = 0.4 around photon < 5 GeV
- Tight shower shape cuts: barrel sigma_eta_eta < 0.010, endcap sigma_eta_eta < 0.028-0.030

### Two complementary purity extraction methods

**Photon conversion method:**
- Uses ET/pT ratio (calorimeter energy / tracker momentum) for converted photons
- Signal peaks at ET/pT ~ 1; background (pi0/eta decays) extends above 1
- Binned extended maximum likelihood fit with signal and background templates from simulation
- Limited to ET < 200 GeV due to conversion sample statistics
- Conversion probability: 20-70% for |eta| < 1.44, 65-70% for 1.57 < |eta| < 2.5

**Isolation method:**
- Uses ISO = Iso_TRK + Iso_ECAL + Iso_HCAL as discriminating variable
- Signal parametrized by convolution of exponential and Gaussian; background by threshold function
- Unbinned extended maximum likelihood fit in ISO range -1 to 20 GeV
- Signal yields quoted for ISO < 5 GeV
- Data-to-simulation scaling factors derived from Z->e+e- (signal shape) and sigma_eta_eta sideband (background shape)
- Pixel veto applied to suppress electron contamination

### Efficiency
- Total efficiency factorized: epsilon = epsilon_reco * epsilon_id1 * epsilon_trig * epsilon_id2
- Tag-and-probe technique with Z->e+e- events for data-to-MC scale factors
- Trigger efficiency: 99.8% (barrel), 99.0% (endcaps)
- Conversion method efficiency: ~2-14% depending on eta (limited by conversion probability)
- Isolation method efficiency: ~70-95% depending on ET and eta

### Unfolding
- Bin-by-bin correction factors applied to account for energy resolution and migrations
- Verified with modified spectrum in simulation; 2-4% systematic uncertainty

### Cross section formula
- d^2sigma / (dET deta) = U(N_gamma) / (Delta_ET * Delta_eta * epsilon * SF * L)
- Weighted average of the two methods with chi2-based combination

## Relevance to PPG12

- CMS uses two independent purity extraction methods and demonstrates that signal/background template fitting is the primary approach for isolated photon yield extraction. PPG12 uses the analogous ABCD method with shower-shape (BDT score) vs isolation as the two axes, similar in concept to CMS's sigma_eta_eta sideband approach.
- The sigma_eta_eta shower shape variable used by CMS is a second moment of the energy distribution in the eta direction, analogous to the sigma_long^2 eigenvalue used by ALICE and the BDT shower shape score used in PPG12.
- CMS's isolation cone R = 0.4 with 5 GeV threshold at generator level is similar in spirit to PPG12's R = 0.3 parametric isolation cut.
- The JETPHOX NLO comparison framework (CT10/CT14 PDFs, BFG II FF, scale variations) is directly inherited by PPG12.
- The data-to-simulation scaling factor approach via Z->e+e- tag-and-probe is a standard technique that PPG12 cannot directly replicate (no Z production at RHIC), but the concept of calibrating efficiency from a clean control sample is universal.

# Centrality, rapidity and transverse momentum dependence of isolated prompt photon production in lead-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV measured with the ATLAS detector

## Abstract

Prompt photon production in sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions has been measured by the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider using data collected in 2011 with an integrated luminosity of 0.14 nb^-1. Inclusive photon yields, scaled by the mean nuclear thickness function <T_AA>, are presented as a function of collision centrality and transverse momentum in two pseudorapidity intervals, |eta| < 1.37 and 1.52 <= |eta| < 2.37. The scaled yields in the two pseudorapidity intervals, as well as the ratios of the forward yields to those at midrapidity, are compared to the expectations from next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations. The measured cross sections agree well with the predictions for proton-proton collisions within statistical and systematic uncertainties. Both the yields and ratios are also compared to two other pQCD calculations, one which uses the isospin content appropriate to colliding lead nuclei, and another which includes nuclear modifications to the nucleon parton distribution functions.

## Key Results

- Isolated prompt photon yields measured in four centrality classes (0-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-80%) for 22 <= pT < 280 GeV in |eta| < 1.37 and 1.52 <= |eta| < 2.37
- <T_AA>-scaled yields agree with JETPHOX pp NLO calculations within statistical and systematic uncertainties in all centrality and eta intervals -- photon yields scale with nuclear thickness as expected
- Ratios of data to JETPHOX pp prediction are consistent with unity across all centrality bins
- Forward-to-central yield ratio R_FC shows slight preference for calculations including isospin effects (neutron content of Pb), though data are consistent with all three scenarios (pp, Pb+Pb isospin, EPS09 nPDF) within uncertainties
- Purity increases from ~50% at pT = 22 GeV to ~90% at highest pT for all centrality intervals
- No evidence for modification of photon production due to hot nuclear medium

### Centrality-Dependent Details
- 0-10% central: <N_coll> = 1500.6 +/- 7.6%, <T_AA> = 23.4 +/- 1.6% mb^-1
- 40-80% peripheral: <N_coll> = 77.8 +/- 9.1%, <T_AA> = 1.2 +/- 8.1% mb^-1
- Total events in 0-80% centrality: N_evt = 7.93 x 10^8

## Methodology

### Underlying Event Subtraction
- Critical for heavy-ion photon measurements: UE can add up to several GeV to photon energy and ~60 GeV to isolation cone at R = 0.3 in central events
- Iterative procedure estimates average energy density in each calorimeter layer in Delta eta = 0.1 intervals, after jet exclusion
- Accounts for elliptic modulation (v2) in energy flow
- Residual fluctuations remain after subtraction and affect both shower shapes and isolation

### Photon Identification
- Nine shower-shape variables used across three electromagnetic calorimeter layers
- Primary variables: R_eta, R_phi, w_eta,2 (second layer); w_s,tot, w_s,3, F_side, E_ratio, Delta E (first strip layer); R_had (hadronic leakage)
- Tight criteria defined to contain 97% of isolated reconstructed photons matched to truth in 40-60 GeV pT range in 0-10% centrality
- Non-tight selection: fails at least one of w_s,3, F_side, E_ratio, Delta E (enhances neutral hadron decays)
- Shower-shape corrections from pp data applied to match simulation to data
- Validated with Z -> e+e- decays in heavy-ion data
- Photon conversion rate: ~30% for |eta| < 1.37, ~60% for 1.52 <= |eta| < 2.37

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: R_iso = 0.3 (smaller than pp measurements to reduce UE sensitivity)
- Isolation cut: E_T^iso < 6 GeV (after UE subtraction and shower leakage correction)
- Shower leakage correction parameterized by photon energy and eta

### Background Estimation (Double Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Four regions: A (tight, isolated = signal), B (tight, non-isolated), C (non-tight, isolated), D (non-tight, non-isolated)
- Background correlation R_bkg = N_A^bkg x N_D^bkg / (N_B^bkg x N_C^bkg) assumed = 1; validated with inclusive jet MC
- Signal leakage factors c_i = N_i^sig / N_A^sig computed from PYTHIA simulation:
  - 40-80%: c_B < 0.01, c_C = 0.09-0.02, c_D < 0.003
  - 0-10%: c_B = 0.08-0.11, c_C = 0.13-0.04, c_D ~ O(1%)
  - c_B larger in central events due to UE contamination of isolation cone
- Statistical uncertainties evaluated via 5000 pseudo-experiments
- For low-statistics high-pT sidebands, extrapolation from lower-pT sideband ratios used

### Yield Extraction
- Per-event yield: (1/N_evt) dN/dpT = N_sig^A x U x W / (N_evt x epsilon_tot x Delta_pT)
- U: bin-by-bin unfolding correction for energy scale/resolution (fit to smooth function; deviates from unity by 6-13% at lowest pT in 0-10%, approaches unity rapidly)
- W: electron contamination correction (from W/Z decays, 1-4% effect)
- epsilon_tot: combined reconstruction + identification + isolation efficiency
- Trigger efficiency > 99.5% for all triggers

### Efficiency
- Reconstruction efficiency: ~90-95% near eta = 0, decreasing to ~85-90% at forward angles; losses from converted photons reconstructed as electrons
- Identification efficiency: dependent on pT and centrality; systematic variations from removing shower-shape corrections (~10% cumulative effect at low pT forward region)
- Isolation efficiency: reduced in central events due to UE fluctuations
- Total efficiency: 40-60% in 0-10% central; higher in peripheral events

### Systematic Uncertainties
- R_bkg correlation: dominant at low pT (up to 21% in 40-80% central); varies 10-20%
- Leakage factors: up to 15% variation at low pT
- Non-tight definition: 3-7%
- Isolation criteria: 5-10%
- Shower-shape corrections: 2-9% (larger at low pT in forward region)
- Energy scale: 5% overall (7% in forward region above 88 GeV)
- Electron contamination: up to 4% in one pT bin
- <T_AA> uncertainty: 1.6% (0-10%) to 8.1% (40-80%)
- Total: 12-26% depending on pT, eta, and centrality

### Theory Comparison
- JETPHOX 1.3 NLO pQCD with CTEQ6.6 PDF, BFG II fragmentation functions
- Isolation: E_T < 6 GeV in R = 0.3 cone
- Three configurations: (1) pp, (2) Pb+Pb with isospin reweighting, (3) Pb+Pb with EPS09 nPDF
- Scale uncertainty: envelope of correlated and independent factor-2 variations of mu_R, mu_F, mu_f (~12-18%)
- PDF uncertainty: ~3% below 100 GeV, up to 6% above 140 GeV

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD method in heavy-ion collisions**: This paper is the definitive demonstration that the double-sideband (ABCD) method works in heavy-ion environments with large underlying event fluctuations. PPG12 uses the same ABCD framework. Key finding: signal leakage factor c_B increases significantly in central events (from < 1% to 8-11%) due to UE contamination of isolation cone -- directly relevant to PPG12's pileup/double-interaction studies.
- **UE subtraction**: The iterative layer-by-layer UE subtraction technique, accounting for elliptic flow modulation, is the state-of-the-art for calorimetric isolation in heavy ions. While PPG12 operates in pp, its pileup studies (DoubleInteractionCheck.C) face analogous challenges where additional collision vertices contaminate isolation cones.
- **TAA scaling confirmation**: Photon yields scale with nuclear thickness as expected from binary collision scaling (no jet quenching for photons). This confirms the electroweak probe baseline used in all heavy-ion jet quenching analyses, including sPHENIX program goals.
- **Isolation cone size**: ATLAS uses R = 0.3 in Pb+Pb (smaller than R = 0.4 in pp) to reduce UE sensitivity. PPG12 also uses R = 0.3, making this a directly relevant validation.
- **Shower-shape variables**: The nine ATLAS shower-shape variables (R_eta, R_phi, w_eta,2, w_s,3, F_side, E_ratio, Delta E, R_had, w_s,tot) are conceptually similar to the EMCal shower-shape features in PPG12's BDT (e11/e33, et1-et4, weta, wphi, etc.).
- **Purity dependence on centrality**: Purity is similar (~50% to 90%) across centrality classes, with c_B dramatically increasing in central events. PPG12 should be aware that pileup effects in sPHENIX similarly increase signal leakage into non-isolated sidebands.

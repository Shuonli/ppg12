# Measurement of the inclusive isolated-photon cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using 36 fb^-1 of ATLAS data

## Abstract

The differential cross section for isolated-photon production in pp collisions is measured at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV with the ATLAS detector at the LHC using an integrated luminosity of 36.1 fb^-1. The differential cross section is presented as a function of the photon transverse energy in different regions of photon pseudorapidity. The differential cross section as a function of the absolute value of the photon pseudorapidity is also presented in different regions of photon transverse energy. Next-to-leading-order QCD calculations from Jetphox and Sherpa as well as next-to-next-to-leading-order QCD calculations from Nnlojet are compared with the measurement, using several parameterisations of the proton parton distribution functions. The predictions provide a good description of the data within the experimental and theoretical uncertainties.

## Key Results

- Differential cross-section measured as a function of ET in four eta bins: |eta| < 0.6, 0.6 < |eta| < 1.37, 1.56 < |eta| < 1.81, 1.81 < |eta| < 2.37
- Also measured as a function of |eta| in different ET ranges (double-differential)
- ET range: 125 GeV to 2500 GeV (|eta| < 0.6), spanning seven orders of magnitude
- Luminosity: 36.1 +/- 0.8 fb^-1 (2015 + 2016 data, uncertainty 2.1%)
- Ten-fold increase in statistics over previous 13 TeV measurement (1701.06882)
- Signal purity above 90% in all bins
- First comparison to NNLO QCD (NNLOJET): excellent description of data
- NNLO scale uncertainties reduced by factor 2-20 compared to NLO (0.6-5% vs 10-15%)
- NLO JETPHOX with mu = ET tends to underestimate data; mu = ET/2 improves normalization
- Sherpa 2.2.2 ME+PS@NLO provides improved description of data normalization over JETPHOX
- Systematic-dominated region extended to ET ~ 1 TeV (previously 600 GeV)
- Total experimental systematic: 3-17% depending on ET and eta

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Tight selection: lateral and longitudinal shower-shape variables in EM calorimeter, energy fraction leaking to hadronic calorimeter
- Separate requirements for converted and unconverted candidates
- Identification efficiency measured using three data-driven methods:
  1. Electron extrapolation from Z -> ee
  2. Matrix method using track isolation
  3. Inclusive photon method using tight/loose ID ratio
- Efficiency above 90% (95%) for unconverted (converted) candidates with ET > 125 GeV
- Efficiency scale factors (data/MC ratio) compatible with unity; uncertainties 1-3%
- Non-tight definition: fails four first-layer EM shower-shape variables (w_s3, f_side, Delta_E_s, E_ratio) but passes total lateral shower width and all other tight criteria

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 using topological clusters of calorimeter cells
- Excluded area around photon barycentre: Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.125 x 0.175
- Photon leakage correction applied
- UE and pileup correction via jet-area method (event-by-event); typical corrections: 3.5 GeV (|eta| < 1.37), 1.3 GeV (1.56 < |eta| < 2.37)
- Data-driven correction to E_T^iso in MC (peak position matching)
- ET-dependent isolation cut: **E_T^iso < 4.2 x 10^-3 x E_T^gamma + 4.8 GeV** (identical to 8 TeV and previous 13 TeV papers)
- Particle-level isolation: sum of transverse energy of all stable particles (lifetime > 10 ps, excluding muons and neutrinos) in cone R = 0.4, with UE subtraction
- Parton-level isolation (JETPHOX): same E_T^iso,cut applied to partons in cone R = 0.4

### Background Estimation (2D Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Four regions in (E_T^iso, tightness) plane:
  - A (signal): tight AND isolated (E_T^iso < E_T^iso,cut)
  - B: tight AND non-isolated (E_T^iso,cut + 2 GeV < E_T^iso < 50 GeV)
  - C: non-tight AND isolated
  - D: non-tight AND non-isolated
- Signal yield: N_A^sig = N_A - R_bg * (N_B - f_B * N_A^sig)(N_C - f_C * N_A^sig) / (N_D - f_D * N_A^sig)
- With R_bg = 1 this is the standard ABCD method with signal leakage corrections
- Signal leakage fractions f_K = N_K^sig / N_A^sig from Pythia MC; Sherpa cross-check
- Signal purity > 90% in all bins
- R_bg validation: background control regions B and D subdivided into B', B'' and D', D'' using additional E_T^iso boundaries; R_bg measured after subtracting signal leakage with Pythia or Sherpa; maximum deviations 15-40% from unity (depending on ET and eta) propagated as systematic
- E -> gamma background: evaluated from MC (Drell-Yan Z/gamma* -> ee, W -> enu); sub-percent, no subtraction applied, full size taken as systematic

### Unfolding
- Bin-by-bin correction factors: C_MC = N_part^MC / N_det^MC (Pythia nominal)
- Correction factors vary between 1.1 and 1.3 depending on ET and eta
- Sherpa alternative for systematic assessment
- Iterative Bayesian unfolding cross-check (consistent with bin-by-bin)
- Double-differential cross-section (d2sigma/d|eta|dET) also presented

### Systematic Uncertainties

**Experimental systematics:**
- **Photon energy scale**: 76 independent components propagated separately to preserve correlations; typical 1-16% on cross-section (dominant at high ET due to steeply falling spectrum)
- **Photon energy resolution**: several independent sources; typically < 0.5%
- **Photon identification efficiency**: 1-3% (scale factors applied to MC)
- **Photon isolation modeling**: < 2% (most ET), up to 3% for ET > 500 GeV in some eta regions (data-driven correction ON/OFF comparison)
- **R_bg correlation**: < 2.5%, typically < 1%
- **Signal leakage fractions (model dependence)**: < 2%, typically < 1%
- **Unfolding model dependence**: < 2%, typically < 1%
- **Background control region definition**: variations of lower E_T^iso limits (+/- 1 GeV), upper limit removal, inverted ID variable choice
- **Signal modeling (hard/bremsstrahlung mixture)**: typically < 1% (0 or 2x bremsstrahlung component)
- **Pileup**: < 1.5% (pile-up reweighting variation)
- **Trigger efficiency**: < 0.4%
- **Luminosity**: 2.1% (fully correlated across bins for ET > 150 GeV; 2.2% for 125-150 GeV bin from 2015 data alone)
- **Total experimental systematic**: 3-17% (dominated by photon energy scale, photon ID, and luminosity)
- Bootstrap technique used for evaluating statistical influence on systematics

**Theoretical systematics (NLO JETPHOX):**
- Scale variations (mu_R, mu_F, mu_f by factors 0.5 and 2, 14 variations, with 0.5 <= mu_A/mu_B <= 2): 10-15%
- PDF uncertainty (MMHT2014 50 Hessian sets): 1-6%
- alpha_s(m_Z) uncertainty: correlated with PDFs (0.118/0.122 or 0.120/0.124); < 2%
- Non-perturbative corrections (hadronization + UE): 1% flat uncertainty

**Theoretical systematics (NNLO NNLOJET):**
- Scale variations (mu_R, mu_F, 6 variations): **0.6-5%** (dramatically reduced from NLO)
- PDF and alpha_s uncertainties: approximated from JETPHOX NLO (expected similar or smaller)
- Calculation statistical uncertainty included
- Uses hybrid-cone isolation (Frixione at small Delta R = 0.1, fixed cone at Delta R = 0.4)

### NLO Theory Programs
- **JETPHOX 1.3.1_2**: NLO QCD for direct + fragmentation; MMHT2014 PDFs, BFG set II FFs; alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.120; alpha_em = 1/137.036; parton-level fixed-cone isolation matching detector cut
- **Sherpa 2.2.2 (ME+PS@NLO QCD)**: NLO QCD for gamma+jet, LO for gamma+2jet and gamma+3jet; Frixione smooth isolation + fixed cone; NNPDF3.0 NNLO PDFs; includes parton showers, hadronization, UE; dynamic merging scale Q_cut = 20 GeV
- **NNLOJET**: NNLO QCD for direct photon only (no fragmentation); hybrid-cone isolation; NNPDF3.1 NNLO PDFs; alpha_em in G_mu scheme (1/132.232)

### Data/Theory Comparisons
- JETPHOX with mu = ET: tends to underestimate data by 5-15% across most of phase space
- JETPHOX with mu = ET/2: improved normalization, closer to data
- Sherpa 2.2.2: above JETPHOX due to higher-order contributions (parton showers, gamma+2jet virtual corrections); improved normalization
- NNLOJET NNLO: provides excellent description of data; dramatically reduced scale uncertainties (factor 2-20 improvement over NLO)
- Multiple PDF sets compared: MMHT2014, CT14, NNPDF3.0, HERAPDF2.0, ABMP16
- Data has potential to constrain gluon PDF in combined NNLO fit

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD method maturity**: This paper represents the most refined application of the ATLAS 2D sideband (ABCD) method with parametric ET-dependent isolation. Every aspect -- signal leakage fractions, R_bg validation regions, non-tight definition, gap between isolated and non-isolated boundaries -- has been validated with high statistics and serves as the gold-standard reference for PPG12's adaptation.
- **ET-dependent isolation**: The same E_T^iso < 4.8 + 0.0042*ET formula used across 8 TeV and both 13 TeV papers confirms this parametric approach is robust. PPG12 uses the same functional form (reco_iso_max = b + s*ET) with different parameters optimized for sPHENIX.
- **R_bg validation methodology**: The B'/B''/D'/D'' subdivision approach with progressive E_T^iso boundary shifts is a concrete recipe for PPG12 to validate its own ABCD correlation assumption. The 15-40% deviations from unity observed here provide context for PPG12's own R_bg studies.
- **NNLO comparison**: The first NNLO comparison shows scale uncertainties reduced to 0.6-5% (from 10-15% at NLO). PPG12 compares to JETPHOX at NLO; this paper demonstrates the magnitude of NNLO corrections and reduced theoretical uncertainties that future PPG12 comparisons should target.
- **76 energy scale components**: The 76-parameter energy scale model, propagated separately to preserve correlations, is vastly more sophisticated than PPG12's few energy-scale variations. However, the principle (propagate correlated and uncorrelated sources separately) is the same.
- **Systematic hierarchy**: The dominant systematics (energy scale > photon ID > luminosity > R_bg) match PPG12's hierarchy (energy scale > purity > efficiency > luminosity), confirming similar physics drivers.
- **Non-tight definition**: The specific four first-layer variables (w_s3, f_side, Delta_E_s, E_ratio) chosen for inversion are those least correlated with E_T^iso. PPG12's BDT-based tight/non-tight should similarly minimize correlation between the classification variable and isolation.

# Measurement of the inclusive isolated photon production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV

## Abstract

The production cross section of inclusive isolated photons has been measured by the ALICE experiment at the CERN LHC in pp collisions at a centre-of-momentum energy of sqrt(s) = 7 TeV. The measurement is performed with the electromagnetic calorimeter EMCal and the central tracking detectors, covering a range of |eta| < 0.27 in pseudorapidity and a transverse momentum range of 10 < pT < 60 GeV/c. The result extends the pT coverage of previously published results of the ATLAS and CMS experiments at the same collision energy to smaller pT. The measurement is compared to next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and to the results from the ATLAS and CMS experiments. All measurements and theory predictions are in agreement with each other.

## Key Results

- Isolated photon cross section measured for 10 < pT < 60 GeV/c at midrapidity |eta| < 0.27
- Extends ATLAS/CMS pT coverage downward by more than a factor of two (ATLAS starts at 25 GeV, CMS at 25 GeV)
- Probes xT range 0.003-0.017 at midrapidity, sensitive to low-x gluon PDF
- NLO JETPHOX (CT14 PDF, BFG II FF, scales mu = pT) agrees with data; data/theory ratio consistent with unity
- Purity: ~20% at 10 GeV/c, rising to ~50-60% above 18 GeV/c (saturates due to merged pi0 contribution)
- Total efficiency for isolated photon reconstruction: ~60% on average
- Luminosity: 473 +/- 28(stat) +/- 17(syst) nb^-1 from EMCal trigger

## Methodology

### Detector
- EMCal: lead-scintillator sampling calorimeter at R = 4.28 m from IP
- 10 supermodules during 2011 data taking; |eta| < 0.7, 80 < phi < 180 degrees
- Cell size: 6 x 6 cm^2, granularity Delta_eta x Delta_phi = 0.0143 x 0.0143 (approximately twice the Moliere radius)
- Energy resolution: sigma_E/E = A + B/sqrt(E) + C/E with A = 1.7%, B = 11.3%, C = 4.8%
- APD readout with known nuclear interaction (neutron) anomalous signal problem
- T-Card cross-talk effect identified and modeled in simulation

### Cluster reconstruction and selection
- Clustering: group cells with E > 100 MeV starting from seed cell > 300 MeV
- Minimum 2 cells per cluster
- Distance > 1 cell from supermodule border (energy leakage suppression)
- Timing cut: |Delta_t| < 30 ns (select main bunch crossing)
- Anomalous signal rejection: F+ = 1 - E+/E_max < 97% (E+ = sum of 4 adjacent cells)
- Nonlinearity correction from electron test beam data (~7% at 0.5 GeV, negligible above 3 GeV)
- Fiducial acceptance after all cuts: |eta| < 0.67, 82 < phi < 178 degrees

### Charged particle veto (CPV)
- TPC+ITS tracks extrapolated to EMCal surface
- Momentum-dependent matching: Delta_eta_residual and Delta_phi_residual cuts as function of pT_track
- Matching efficiency: ~92% at 1 GeV rising to ~96% at 10 GeV
- Clusters passing CPV called "neutral clusters"

### Shower shape identification (sigma_long^2)
- sigma_long^2: square of the larger eigenvalue of the energy distribution in the eta-phi plane
- Calculated from energy-weighted second moments: sigma_long^2 = (sigma_phi_phi + sigma_eta_eta)/2 + sqrt((sigma_phi_phi - sigma_eta_eta)^2/4 + sigma_eta_phi^4)
- Logarithmic weights: w_i = max(0, 4.5 + ln(E_i / E_cluster))
- Single photon peak at sigma_long^2 ~ 0.25
- Narrow cluster selection (photon candidates): 0.1 < sigma_long^2 < sigma_max^2 where sigma_max = 0.4 (10-14 GeV), 0.35 (14-16 GeV), 0.3 (>16 GeV)
- Merged pi0 decay photons create distinct pT-dependent band at higher sigma_long^2 values; overlap with single photon region increases with energy
- T-Card cross-talk causes broader sigma_long^2 tail in data vs default MC; modeled via dedicated MC tuning

### Isolation selection
- Cone radius R = 0.4
- Isolation momentum: piso_T = sum(pT_cluster) + sum(pT_track) in cone (both neutral EMCal clusters and charged tracks)
- Isolated: piso_T < 2 GeV/c
- Fiducial acceptance restricted to |eta_gamma| < 0.27 (full cone must be in both EMCal and tracker)
- Charged tracks: |eta| < 0.9, pT > 0.2 GeV/c

### ABCD purity extraction (key methodology for PPG12 comparison)
- Four regions defined by (sigma_long^2, piso_T):
  - A = narrow + isolated (signal region): 0.1 < sigma_long^2 < sigma_max, piso_T < 2 GeV/c
  - B = narrow + non-isolated: 0.1 < sigma_long^2 < sigma_max, piso_T > 3 GeV/c
  - C = wide + isolated: sigma_long^2 in background range, piso_T < 2 GeV/c
  - D = wide + non-isolated: sigma_long^2 in background range, piso_T > 3 GeV/c
- Wide cluster ranges: pT-dependent, e.g., 0.55 < sigma_long^2 < 1.75 for pT < 12 GeV
- Data-driven purity: P_dd = 1 - (N_n^iso / N_n^{anti-iso}) / (N_w^iso / N_w^{anti-iso})
- MC correction factor alpha_MC applied: accounts for correlation between sigma_long^2 and piso_T (ratio of background isolation fractions in narrow vs wide clusters differs from unity)
- P = 1 - (N_n^iso / N_n^{anti-iso}) / (N_w^iso / N_w^{anti-iso}) * alpha_MC
- Double ratio f(sigma_long^2) studied to verify MC correction validity; found to be approximately constant but with possible slope, leading to modified correction using linear extrapolation
- Additional cross-checks: tracks-only isolation (piso_T < 1.9 GeV/c), varying anti-isolation cut, varying MC correction normalization
- Final purity: mean of all cross-check variations; ~20% at 10 GeV/c, saturating at ~50-60% above 18 GeV/c

### Efficiency
- Reconstruction: epsilon_rec ~ 70% (limited by excluded regions and SM border cuts)
- Identification (shower shape): reduces to epsilon_rec * epsilon_id ~ 60%
- Isolation: further reduces to epsilon_rec * epsilon_id * epsilon_iso ~ 50%
- Total efficiency: epsilon_gamma^iso = (epsilon_rec * epsilon_id * epsilon_iso) / kappa_iso ~ 60%
- kappa_iso: fraction of generated photons that are isolated (~85-90%)

### Trigger
- EMCal Level-0 trigger: E > 5.5 GeV in 2x2 cell tile
- Trigger efficiency: epsilon_trig = 0.90 +/- 0.06 (stat), limited by inactive TRU cards and sliding window algorithm
- Synchronization bias correction: C = N(main bunch) / N(main + next bunch), corrected per pT bin

### Cross section
- d^2sigma / (dpT deta) = (1/epsilon_trig) * (1/L) * P / epsilon_gamma^iso * dN_n^iso / dpT

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Closest detector analog:** ALICE EMCal is a lead-scintillator sampling calorimeter (like sPHENIX CEMCal) with similar cell granularity (~0.014 vs ~0.024 for sPHENIX) and similar challenges (APD anomalous signals, T-Card cross-talk, material budget effects). CMS and ATLAS use PbWO4 crystal calorimeters with much finer granularity and superior intrinsic resolution.
- **ABCD method:** ALICE explicitly uses the four-region ABCD approach with shower shape (sigma_long^2) and isolation momentum as the two axes -- this is essentially identical to PPG12's (BDT score, isolation) 2D classification. The region labeling (A, B, C, D) and the data-driven purity formula are the same.
- **Shower shape variable:** sigma_long^2 is the eigenvalue of the cluster energy ellipse, capturing the same physics as sPHENIX's shower shape BDT features (weta, wphi, e-ratios). The pT-dependent cut on sigma_max is analogous to PPG12's ET-dependent BDT threshold.
- **MC correction of ABCD:** ALICE applies a correction factor alpha_MC to account for the correlation between sigma_long^2 and piso_T for background clusters. PPG12 applies analogous signal leakage corrections (cB, cC, cD) to the ABCD formula. The physics is the same: background shower shape and isolation are not truly independent.
- **Low-pT challenges:** ALICE's measurement at 10-60 GeV/c overlaps with PPG12's 8-35 GeV/c range. The dominant systematic is the isolation probability (20% at low pT) -- same physics as PPG12's purity systematic. The large contamination at low pT (~80% at 10 GeV/c) is comparable to PPG12 levels.
- **T-Card cross-talk:** ALICE's careful treatment of EMCal electronics cross-talk effects on the shower shape is directly relevant to any shower-shape anomalies seen in sPHENIX CEMCal data.

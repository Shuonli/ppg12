# Measurement of the inclusive isolated-photon production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV

## Abstract

The production cross section of inclusive isolated photons has been measured by the ALICE experiment at the CERN LHC in pp collisions at centre-of-momentum energy of sqrt(s) = 13 TeV collected during the LHC Run 2 data-taking period. The measurement is performed by combining the measurements of the electromagnetic calorimeter EMCal and the central tracking detectors ITS and TPC, covering a pseudorapidity range of |eta| < 0.67 and a transverse momentum range of 7 < pT < 200 GeV/c. The result extends to lower pT and xT ranges, the lowest xT of any isolated photon measurements to date, extending significantly those measured by the ATLAS and CMS experiments towards lower pT at the same collision energy with a small overlap between the measurements. The measurement is compared with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and the results from the ATLAS and CMS experiments as well as with measurements at other collision energies. The measurement and theory prediction are in agreement with each other within the experimental and theoretical uncertainties.

## Key Results

- Isolated photon cross section measured for 7 < pT < 200 GeV/c at |eta| < 0.67
- Lowest xT (= 2pT/sqrt(s)) ever measured with isolated photons: xT down to 1.08 x 10^-3
- Extends LHC reach by an order of magnitude in pT below ATLAS and CMS at same sqrt(s)
- NLO JETPHOX (NNPDF4.0 PDF, BFG II FF) agrees with data within uncertainties
- Cross section ratio sqrt(s) = 13 TeV / 7 TeV consistent with NLO prediction (~1.5-2.1)
- xT scaling comparison across all available data (ISR to LHC) with n ~ 4.5 shows consistency
- Purity: ~5% at 7 GeV/c, ~50% at 18 GeV/c, rising to ~80% above 80 GeV/c
- Luminosity: 10.79 pb^-1 (pT > 12 GeV/c), 1.16 pb^-1 (pT < 12 GeV/c); normalization uncertainty 1.5%

## Methodology

### Detector (improved from 7 TeV analysis)
- EMCal: twenty supermodules (vs 10 at 7 TeV); expanded azimuthal coverage
  - Full SM (24 x 48 cells): |eta| < 0.7, 80 < phi < 187 degrees
  - 2/3 SM (24 x 32 cells): 0.22 < |eta| < 0.7, 260 < phi < 320 degrees
  - 1/3 SM (8 x 48 cells): covering gap at |eta| < 0.22
- Cell size: 6 x 6 cm^2 (Delta_eta x Delta_phi ~ 0.0143 x 0.0143)
- Energy resolution: sigma_E/E = 1.4% + 9.5%/sqrt(E) + 2.9%/E (improved over 7 TeV)
- Radial distance: 4.28 m from interaction point

### Cluster reconstruction and selection
- Seed energy: 500 MeV (increased from 300 MeV at 7 TeV)
- Minimum 2 cells per cluster
- Distance d_mask > 2 cells from misbehaving/dead cells
- Distance > 1 cell from SM border (except at eta = 0 where SMs are adjacent)
- Timing: |Delta_t| < 20 ns (tighter than 30 ns at 7 TeV)
- Anomalous signal (APD neutron interactions): F+ < 97%; for E > 80 GeV additionally require cells from more than one T-Card
- Energy nonlinearity correction: ~6% at 1 GeV to ~2% at 20-100 GeV, rising to ~3% at 200 GeV (signal saturation)
- Additional pi0 mass matching correction: factor ~1.025 at 1 GeV, ~0.975 above 7 GeV

### Charged particle veto
- Same hybrid ITS+TPC approach as 7 TeV (90% class (i) + 10% class (ii) tracks)
- Momentum-dependent Delta_eta and Delta_phi residual cuts

### Shower shape identification (sigma_long^2)
- Same eigenvalue calculation as 7 TeV analysis
- Narrow cluster (signal): 0.1 < sigma_long^2 < 0.3 (simplified from pT-dependent cut at 7 TeV)
- NLM (number of local maxima) < 3 requirement
- Cross-talk effect from T-Card modeled in simulation
- Merged pi0 contribution increases with energy; eta meson merging visible above 40 GeV/c

### Isolation (key change from 7 TeV)
- Cone radius R = 0.4 (same)
- **Tracks only** for isolation momentum: piso,ch,UE_T = sum(pT_track) in cone
  - Advantage: expanded eta acceptance from |eta| < 0.27 to |eta| < 0.67 (no need for full EMCal cone containment)
  - Threshold: piso,ch,UE_T < 1.5 GeV/c (equivalent to piso_T < 2 GeV/c with tracks + clusters)
- Track requirement: |eta| < 0.9, pT > 0.15 GeV/c
- For clusters at 0.5 < |eta| < 0.67: isolation momentum scaled up by fraction of cone area outside tracker acceptance
- UE not subtracted from isolation (corrected at cross section level via kappa_iso)

### ABCD purity extraction
- Same four-region approach as 7 TeV:
  - Narrow (0.1 < sigma_long^2 < 0.3) vs wide (0.4 < sigma_long^2 < 2.4) clusters
  - Isolated (piso,ch,UE_T < 1.5 GeV/c) vs anti-isolated (2.5 < piso,ch,UE_T < 10 GeV/c)
- MC correction alpha_MC from jet-jet + gamma-jet PYTHIA 8 simulations
- Modified correction via linear extrapolation of double ratio f(sigma_long^2) to handle data-MC discrepancy
- Purity fitted by two sigmoid functions to reduce point-to-point statistical fluctuations:
  - Low pT (7-30 GeV/c): a1 = 0.617, b1 = 0.292, c1 = 14.28
  - High pT (30-200 GeV/c): a2 = 0.852, b2 = 0.034, c2 = 2.4

### Efficiency
- Reconstruction: epsilon_rec ~ 50% (limited by d_mask and SM border exclusions)
- Identification: epsilon_rec * epsilon_id ~ 35-45%
- Isolation: epsilon_rec * epsilon_id * epsilon_iso ~ 30-40%
- Total: epsilon_gamma^iso = (reco * id * iso) / kappa_iso ~ 30-45%
- kappa_iso correction factor: accounts for UE effect on isolation (divides measurement, not applied to theory)

### Multi-trigger combination
- Three triggers: MB, L1-gamma-low (threshold ~5.5 GeV), L1-gamma-high (~12 GeV)
- Trigger rejection factors: RF(L1-low, MB) = 471 +/- 3; RF(L1-high, L1-low) = 12.65 +/- 0.03
- Trigger efficiency: 88-97% depending on threshold and pT
- Luminosity: L(pT > 12 GeV/c) = 10.79 pb^-1; L(pT < 12 GeV/c) = 1.16 pb^-1

### Cross section formula
- d^2sigma / (dpT deta) = sigma_MB / (sum_i N_evt^i * RF^i) * sum_i [d^2N_iso^i / (dpT deta) * 1/epsilon_trig^i] * P / (epsilon_gamma^iso * kappa_iso * Acc)
- kappa_iso divides measurement (corrects for UE effect rather than applying to theory)
- Acc = Delta_eta * Delta_phi / (2pi)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Most directly comparable measurement to PPG12:** ALICE uses a lead-scintillator sampling EMCal with similar granularity and challenges to sPHENIX CEMCal, and employs the same ABCD purity extraction methodology with (shower shape, isolation) as the two axes.
- **pT overlap with PPG12:** ALICE's 7-200 GeV/c range fully encompasses PPG12's 8-35 GeV/c range at a different sqrt(s). The purity behavior (heavily contaminated at low pT, improving with pT) is qualitatively identical.
- **Tracks-only isolation:** The switch from tracks+clusters (7 TeV) to tracks-only (13 TeV) with an adjusted threshold is an important methodological choice that PPG12 could consider. It dramatically expands the eta acceptance at the cost of missing neutral isolation energy.
- **Sigmoid purity fitting:** ALICE fits the measured purity with sigmoid functions to reduce statistical fluctuations -- PPG12 could adopt a similar smoothing approach for its purity vs ET.
- **kappa_iso UE correction:** ALICE applies the underlying event correction to the data rather than theory, making the cross section directly comparable to NLO calculations without UE. PPG12's parametric isolation cut (reco_iso_max = b + s*ET) partially absorbs UE effects.
- **Isolation probability systematic:** The dominant systematic at low pT is the correlation between sigma_long^2 and isolation momentum (called "isolation probability" -- 27.6% at 7 GeV/c). This is the same physics as PPG12's purity systematic from signal leakage in ABCD regions.
- **T-Card cross-talk:** The 12% "no MC tuning" uncertainty at 7 GeV/c from cross-talk modeling is directly relevant to sPHENIX electronics effects on shower shapes.
- **xT scaling compilation:** Fig. 9 in the paper shows all isolated photon data on a common xT scale with n = 4.5, providing a universal reference for PPG12's measurement at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

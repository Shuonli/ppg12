# Measurement of differential cross sections for inclusive isolated-photon and photon+jet production in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV

## Abstract

Measurements of inclusive isolated-photon and photon+jet production in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV are presented. The analysis uses data collected by the CMS experiment in 2015, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 2.26 fb^-1. The cross section for inclusive isolated photon production is measured as a function of the photon transverse energy in a fiducial region. The cross section for photon+jet production is measured as a function of the photon transverse energy in the same fiducial region with identical photon requirements and with the highest transverse momentum jet. All measurements are in agreement with predictions from next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD.

## Key Results

- Inclusive isolated-photon cross section measured for ET > 190 GeV, |y| < 2.5, in four rapidity bins
- Photon+jet cross section measured simultaneously with pT_jet > 30 GeV, |y_jet| < 2.4
- Cross sections increased by factor 3-5 compared to 7 TeV measurements; ET range extended to 1 TeV
- NLO JETPHOX predictions (NNPDF3.0 PDF, BFG II FF) agree with data within uncertainties
- Comparison with multiple PDF sets (NNPDF3.0, MMHT14, CT14, HERAPDF2.0) shows small differences, all within theoretical uncertainties
- Data can further constrain the proton gluon PDF

## Methodology

### BDT-based photon identification (key innovation)
- Boosted Decision Tree (BDT) trained using TMVA v4.1.2 toolkit
- Separate BDT trained for each bin of photon rapidity and ET
- BDT input variables:
  - Photon eta, phi, and energy
  - Shower shape variables: 3x3/total energy ratio, E22/E55, sigma_eta_eta, covariance sigma_eta_phi, energy-weighted spreads sigma_eta and sigma_phi
  - For endcaps: preshower width sigma_RR and preshower energy fraction
  - Median energy density rho (pileup mitigation)
- Signal template: PYTHIA 8 photon+jet simulation, validated with Z->mu+mu-gamma and Z->e+e- data
- Background template: data sideband with relaxed hadron isolation (7-13 GeV barrel, 6-12 GeV endcaps)
- Two-template binned likelihood fit to BDT output distribution
- Signal template systematic: Z->e+e- data-MC comparison used to modify BDT input distributions
- Background template bias: estimated from simulation, corrections < 5%

### Photon selection
- Fiducial region: ET > 190 GeV, |y| < 2.5 (excluding 1.44 < |eta| < 1.56 transition region)
- Generator-level isolation: sum pT in cone R = 0.4 < 5 GeV
- H/E < 0.08 (barrel) / 0.05 (endcaps)
- Photon isolation (R = 0.3): sum ET of other photons < 15 GeV
- Charged hadron isolation (R = 0.3): sum pT < 2.0 GeV (barrel) / 1.5 GeV (endcaps)
- sigma_eta_eta < 0.015 (barrel) / 0.045 (endcaps)
- Electron veto: absence of pixel detector hits consistent with electron track

### Unfolding
- Iterative D'Agostini method via RooUnfold
- Response matrix from PYTHIA 8 photon+jet sample
- Unfolding corrections small, ~1%; verified with independent MadGraph sample

### Cross section
- d^2sigma / (dy dET) = U(N_gamma) / (Delta_y * Delta_ET * epsilon * SF * L)
- epsilon: product of trigger, reconstruction, and selection efficiencies
- SF: data-to-MC scale factors for selection and electron veto

## Relevance to PPG12

- This is the most directly analogous CMS analysis to PPG12 in terms of methodology: both use BDT-based shower shape discrimination for photon identification, with signal/background templates fitted to extract the photon yield. PPG12 uses a BDT score trained on 25 shower-shape features (XGBoost) while CMS uses a TMVA BDT with ~10 variables including shower shapes, but the core approach is identical.
- CMS derives background BDT templates from data sidebands (relaxed hadron isolation), analogous to PPG12's non-tight (low BDT) control region in the ABCD method. The sideband approach validates that isolation-relaxed data provides an accurate background shape.
- The signal template validation using Z->e+e- data-MC comparison is a rigorous approach to control systematic uncertainties in the BDT shape; PPG12 relies on simulation validation without a clean electromagnetic calibration sample at RHIC.
- JETPHOX NLO comparison with NNPDF3.0 PDFs and scale variations is directly used in PPG12's theory comparison framework.
- CMS's ET > 190 GeV reach is well above PPG12's 8-35 GeV range, but the methodology is scale-invariant and applicable at both energies.

# Measurement of prompt photon production in sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV p+Pb collisions with ATLAS

## Abstract

The inclusive production rates of isolated, prompt photons in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV are studied with the ATLAS detector at the Large Hadron Collider using a dataset with an integrated luminosity of 165 nb^-1 recorded in 2016. The cross-section and nuclear modification factor R_pPb are measured as a function of photon transverse energy from 20 GeV to 550 GeV and in three nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass pseudorapidity regions: [-2.83, -2.02], [-1.84, 0.91], and [1.09, 1.90]. The cross-section and R_pPb values are compared with the results of a next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculation, with and without nuclear parton distribution function modifications, and with expectations based on a model of the energy loss of partons prior to the hard scattering. The data disfavour a large amount of energy loss and provide new constraints on the parton densities in nuclei.

## Key Results

- Differential cross-section measured as a function of photon ET from 20 GeV to 550 GeV in three eta* regions, spanning five orders of magnitude
- NLO pQCD (JETPHOX + CT14 + EPPS16 nPDF) systematically ~10-20% lower than data at low ET, closer at high ET; consistent with known NLO-vs-NNLO discrepancy
- R_pPb consistent with unity in all pseudorapidity regions within uncertainties, indicating modest nuclear effects
- At forward rapidity (eta* = 1.09-1.90), probing gluon x_A ~ 10^-2: R_pPb consistent with unity; nPDF prediction shows slight suppression at low ET (shadowing) and enhancement at high ET (anti-shadowing crossover)
- At backward rapidity (eta* = -2.83 to -2.02), probing quark x_A ~ 0.2: isospin effect (neutron content) causes small suppression; nPDF effects show anti-shadowing to EMC crossover
- Forward-to-backward R_pPb ratio sensitive to gluon shadowing vs quark anti-shadowing; data systematically above nPDF predictions at low ET, approximately within theoretical uncertainty
- Initial-state energy loss model with mean free path lambda_q = 1.5 fm disfavoured; data prefer no or limited energy loss
- Purity: ~45% at ET = 20 GeV, rising to 80% at 100 GeV and 99% at 300 GeV

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Tight calorimeter shower-shape requirements in barrel (|eta_lab| < 1.37) and endcap (1.56 < |eta_lab| < 2.37)
- Transition region 1.37 < |eta_lab| < 1.56 excluded due to high inactive material
- Energy calibration corrects for upstream material, lateral/longitudinal leakage, and sampling fraction
- Efficiency: reconstruction ~80-95%; total selection efficiency (reco x ID x isolation) ~ 70-95% depending on ET and eta

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 (chosen to match 8 TeV pp reference measurement)
- Calorimeter-based isolation: E_T^iso < 4.8 + 4.2 x 10^-3 x E_T(gamma) [GeV] (ET-dependent parametric cut)
- Photon core excluded: Delta eta x Delta phi = 0.125 x 0.175 around photon
- Ambient energy correction for underlying event applied

### pp Reference Construction
- Reference derived from previously measured pp data at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV by ATLAS, using identical particle-level isolation
- Extrapolation factors applied to map 8 TeV pp lab-frame kinematics to 8.16 TeV p+Pb boosted centre-of-mass frame
- Factors computed as ratios of JETPHOX cross-sections; alternative PYTHIA 8 factors used for systematic uncertainty
- Extrapolation factors dominated by centre-of-mass boost (delta_eta = 0.465); typically within a few percent of unity at mid-rapidity, larger at high ET and large rapidity

### Background Estimation (Double Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Two axes: isolation (isolated vs non-isolated) and identification (tight vs non-tight shower shapes)
- Signal region: tight and isolated
- Background correlation assumed uncorrelated along two axes
- Signal leakage fractions determined from simulation
- Purity extracted via double-sideband procedure; validated with PYTHIA 8 MC isolation distributions

### MC Simulation
- PYTHIA 8.186 with NNPDF23 LO, A14 tune; centre-of-mass boost applied
- Overlay with real p+Pb minimum-bias data events for underlying event modelling
- Full GEANT4 detector simulation
- SHERPA 2.2.4 cross-check yielded consistent results

### Nuclear Modification Factor
- R_pPb = (d sigma_{p+Pb}) / (A x d sigma_{pp}), with A = 208
- pp reference spectrum extrapolated from 8 TeV using JETPHOX-derived multiplicative factors
- R_pPb reported for ET > 25 GeV (where reference data available)
- Forward-to-backward R_pPb ratio computed to cancel common systematics

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Purity determination: dominant at low ET (~13% at lowest ET, < 1% for ET > 100 GeV); driven by sideband correlation uncertainty (~10%)
- Detector performance (energy scale): dominant at high ET (5-15% at 500 GeV in endcap)
- Combined cross-section uncertainty: 15% at low/high ET, minimum ~6% at ET ~ 100 GeV
- R_pPb uncertainty: ~5% at low and high ET, ~3% at mid-ET
- Luminosity: 2.4% p+Pb, 1.9% pp (separate normalization)
- Extrapolation factors: few percent, subdominant

### Theory Comparison
- JETPHOX NLO with CT14 free-proton PDF; EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs
- Scale uncertainty: envelope of factor-2 variations of mu_R, mu_F, mu_f (~12-18%)
- PDF uncertainty via CT14 error sets (68% CL)
- nPDF uncertainty from EPPS16 (90% CL, converted to 68%) and nCTEQ15
- Initial-state energy-loss model (Vitev et al.) with mu = 0.35 GeV and lambda_q = 1 or 1.5 fm

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Nuclear baseline**: PPG12 measures isolated photons in pp at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. This paper provides the nuclear modification picture at higher energy, confirming that prompt photons are largely unaffected by cold nuclear matter and constraining initial-state energy loss -- important context for interpreting any sPHENIX d+Au or A+A extensions.
- **Isolation convention**: ATLAS uses an ET-dependent parametric isolation cut E_T^iso < 4.8 + 4.2 x 10^-3 x ET in R = 0.4, directly analogous to PPG12's parametric reco_iso_max = b + s x ET. The parametric approach was pioneered in the 8 TeV pp paper (1605.03495) and retained here.
- **ABCD method in nuclear collisions**: Demonstrates that the double-sideband (ABCD) background subtraction works reliably in p+Pb with purity from ~45% to 99%. PPG12 uses the same ABCD framework.
- **JETPHOX comparison**: NLO JETPHOX systematically underestimates the cross-section by 10-20% at low ET, motivating NNLO corrections. PPG12 also compares to JETPHOX and should note this known NLO deficit.
- **nPDF constraints**: The Bjorken-x range probed (3 x 10^-3 to 0.4) overlaps with the x range relevant at RHIC (x ~ 0.04-0.2 at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for 8-35 GeV photons), providing complementary nPDF constraints.
- **Initial-state energy loss**: Data disfavour large parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter, relevant for interpreting any nuclear effects at RHIC energies.

# Measurement of the inclusive isolated prompt photon cross-section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV using 35 pb^-1 of ATLAS data

## Abstract

A measurement of the differential cross-section for the inclusive production of isolated prompt photons in pp collisions at a center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is presented. The measurement covers the pseudorapidity ranges |eta| < 1.37 and 1.52 <= |eta| < 2.37 in the transverse energy range 45 <= ET < 400 GeV. The results are based on an integrated luminosity of 35 pb^-1, collected with the ATLAS detector at the LHC. The yields of the signal photons are measured using a data-driven technique, based on the observed distribution of the hadronic energy in a narrow cone around the photon candidate and the photon selection criteria. The results are compared with next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations and found to be in good agreement over four orders of magnitude in cross-section.

## Key Results

- Differential cross-section measured as a function of photon ET from 45 to 400 GeV in four pseudorapidity bins: |eta| < 0.6, 0.6 <= |eta| < 1.37, 1.52 <= |eta| < 1.81, 1.81 <= |eta| < 2.37
- Good agreement with NLO pQCD (JETPHOX 1.2.2) using CTEQ 6.6 PDFs over four orders of magnitude in cross-section
- Scale uncertainty is the leading theoretical systematic at ~10%; PDF uncertainty ~5% at 68% CL
- MSTW 2008 and NNPDF2.0 PDFs yield 3-5% and 1-4% higher central values than CTEQ 6.6, respectively
- Purity of isolated prompt photons increases from 91% at ET = 45 GeV to near 100% at ET > 200 GeV

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Tight selection criteria based on lateral and longitudinal shower-shape variables in the electromagnetic calorimeter (ECAL)
- Separate treatment of converted and unconverted photon candidates
- Identification efficiency (epsilon_ID) varies from 90% to 97% depending on eta and increasing with ET
- Systematic uncertainty on epsilon_ID: 1.5% to 3%, with contributions from detector simulation, background contamination, (un)converted photon misclassification, direct/fragmentation photon fraction, and MC generator choice

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 centered around photon direction
- Calorimeter-based isolation energy (E_T^iso): sum of calorimeter energy in cone, corrected for photon shower leakage
- Isolation cut: E_T^iso < 3 GeV (signal region)
- Ambient energy correction for underlying event and pileup applied using method from Refs. [17, 18]
- Theoretical (parton-level) isolation: E_T^iso < E_T^cut within cone R = 0.4; JETPHOX uses E_T^iso < 4 GeV

### Background Estimation (2D Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Two dimensions: transverse isolation energy (E_T^iso) and photon quality (tight vs non-tight shower shapes)
- Signal region: E_T^iso < 3 GeV AND tight identification
- Non-isolated sideband: E_T^iso > 5 GeV
- Non-tight sideband: fails tight criteria but passes a background-enriching subset
- Signal leakage corrections computed from MC: up to 17% at high ET for tight isolated photons leaking into non-isolated region; up to 6% for other control regions
- Background correlation parameter R_BKG assumed to be 1 (validated in MC)
- Non-tight distribution normalized to tight distribution in E_T^iso > 5 GeV region where signal contamination is small

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Purity/yield: fragmentation fraction (up to 8%), pileup (5%, with fluctuations up to 8% in 1.52 <= |eta| < 1.81)
- Photon identification efficiency: 1.5%-3% depending on eta
- Photon energy scale: dominant systematic at high ET (2%-6%)
- Photon energy resolution: ~1%
- Unfolding: < 3% (SVD approach)
- Luminosity: 3.4% (separate normalization uncertainty)
- Total systematic uncertainty dominates over statistical uncertainty across the entire kinematic range

### Unfolding
- SVD (Singular Value Decomposition) approach
- Bin-by-bin cross-check shows < 3% difference with SVD result

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD method**: This paper establishes the 2D sideband technique that PPG12 adapts. ATLAS uses E_T^iso < 3 GeV as the isolated boundary and E_T^iso > 5 GeV as the non-isolated boundary (with a gap), while PPG12 uses a parametric ET-dependent isolation cut with a similar ABCD structure.
- **Isolation cone**: ATLAS uses R = 0.4 with a fixed 3 GeV cut here, while PPG12 uses R = 0.3 with a parametric ET-dependent cut. The fixed cut was later upgraded to ET-dependent in the 8 TeV paper.
- **Shower-shape identification**: ATLAS uses discrete tight/non-tight categories based on calorimeter shower variables, analogous to PPG12's BDT-based tight/non-tight classification.
- **Signal leakage corrections**: The c_B, c_C, c_D framework in PPG12 directly mirrors the ATLAS signal leakage fractions (17% tight-iso into non-iso, 6% elsewhere).
- **Purity**: Rising from ~91% at low ET to ~100% at high ET, qualitatively similar to PPG12 purity behavior.

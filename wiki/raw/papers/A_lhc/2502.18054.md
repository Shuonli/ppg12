# Measurement of isolated prompt photon production in pp and p-Pb collisions at the LHC

## Abstract

This paper presents the measurement of the isolated prompt photon inclusive production cross section in pp and p-Pb collisions by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC. The measurement is performed in p-Pb collisions at centre-of-mass energies per nucleon pair of sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and 8.16 TeV, as well as in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV and 8 TeV. The cross section is obtained at midrapidity (|y| < 0.7) using a charged-track based isolation momentum p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c in a cone with radius R = 0.4. The data for both collision systems are well reproduced by perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations at next-to-leading order (NLO) using recent parton distribution functions for free (PDF) and bound (nPDF) nucleons. Furthermore, the nuclear modification factor R_pA for both collision energies is consistent with unity for pT > 20 GeV/c. However, deviations from unity (R_pA < 1) of up to 20% are observed for pT < 20 GeV/c with limited significance, indicating the possible presence of nuclear effects in the initial state of the collision. The suppression increases with decreasing pT with a significance of 2.3 sigma for a non-zero slope and yields R_pA < 1 with a significance of 1.8 sigma at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV for pT < 20 GeV/c. In addition, a significance of 1.1 sigma is observed for R_pA < 1 at the lower collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for pT < 14 GeV/c. The magnitude and shape of the suppression are consistent with pQCD predictions at NLO using nPDFs that incorporate nuclear shadowing effects in the Pb nucleus.

## Key Results

- Isolated prompt photon cross-section measured in four systems: pp at sqrt(s) = 5.02 and 8 TeV; p-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 and 8.16 TeV
- pT range: 12-60 GeV/c (5.02 TeV) to 12-80 GeV/c (8/8.16 TeV) at midrapidity |y| < 0.7
- Cross-sections agree with JETPHOX NLO (NNPDF4.0 + nNNPDF3.0 + BFG II FF) in all systems
- **R_pA consistent with unity for pT > 20 GeV/c** at both 5.02 and 8.16 TeV
- **Hint of suppression at low pT (< 20 GeV/c)**:
  - sqrt(s_NN) = 8.16 TeV: R_pA < 1 with significance 1.8 sigma; non-zero downward slope with significance 2.3 sigma
  - sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: R_pA < 1 with significance 1.1 sigma for pT < 14 GeV/c
  - Suppression magnitude up to ~20% at pT ~ 12 GeV/c
  - Consistent with nPDF shadowing predictions (nNNPDF3.0, nCTEQ15HQ)
- Probes Bjorken-x down to x ~ 2pT/sqrt(s) ~ 2.9 x 10^-3, extending low-x reach by factor ~2 compared to ATLAS p+Pb measurement
- ALICE R_pA agrees with ATLAS R_pPb measurement at 8.16 TeV for pT > 25 GeV/c (ATLAS starts at 25 GeV)
- Comparison between ALICE (tight isolation p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c) and ATLAS (looser E_T^iso < 4.8 + 0.0042 x E_T) suggests tighter isolation reduces fragmentation contribution and improves NLO description

### Purity
- pT ~ 12 GeV/c: ~20% (limited by large pi0 background and finite calorimeter granularity)
- Increases to ~60-70% at highest pT
- Plateau around 40-50% for 20 < pT < 40 GeV/c (merged pi0 clusters contaminate signal region)
- Slightly higher purity in p-Pb at 8.16 TeV than 5.02 TeV (due to CPV selection)
- Slightly lower purity in p-Pb than pp (larger UE)

### Efficiency
- Increases slightly with pT due to increasingly narrow clusters satisfying shower-shape cut
- Convolution of: isolation (~90%), shower shape (80-90%), track matching/CPV (~85%), cluster reconstruction (~70%)
- Differences between datasets from: masked EMCal channels, material budget (TRD modules Run 1 vs Run 2), multiplicity sensitivity, trigger efficiency

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- EMCal clustering: seed 500 MeV, aggregation 100 MeV, minimum 2 cells, exotics removal (F+ < 0.95)
- Shower shape: sigma^2_long (same formulation as 2409.12641)
  - Signal: 0.1 < sigma^2_long < 0.3
  - Wide/background: 0.4 < sigma^2_long < 2.0
- Charged particle veto (CPV): track-cluster matching with |Delta phi| < 0.05 rad, |Delta eta| < 0.05, E/p < 1.75
  - Not used for p-Pb at 5.02 TeV (no TPC readout in pp reference)
- Cross-talk modelling between EMCal cells included in simulation

### Isolation Definition
- Charged-track based: p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c in R = 0.4 cone
- Signal efficiency ~90%, background rejection > 70%
- UE estimation: perpendicular cone method and kT jet-finder (FastJet) as cross-check
  - Median UE density < 1 GeV/c (pp), < 1.5 GeV/c (p-Pb)
- Geometrical correction for edge clusters (|eta| > 0.5) where cone extends beyond tracking acceptance

### Background Estimation
- **ABCD method** (primary, for pp 8 TeV and p-Pb 8.16 TeV):
  - P_ABCD = 1 - (N_n^iso / N_n^{anti-iso}) / (N_w^iso / N_w^{anti-iso})_data x alpha_MC
  - alpha_MC corrects for shower-shape/isolation correlation (typical values 1.1-1.4)
  - Anti-isolated: p_T^{iso,ch} > 4 GeV/c
  - Residual data/MC correlation mismatch evaluated via double ratio f(sigma^2_long); slope consistent with zero
- **Template fit method** (for p-Pb 5.02 TeV):
  - Fits sigma^2_long distribution with signal template (gamma-jet MC) + background template (anti-isolated data sideband)
  - Single free parameter: number of signal clusters
  - Correction for shower-shape/isolation correlation via reweighting
- Purity fitted with modified sigmoid: P(pT) = a1 / [1 + exp(-b1 x (pT - c1))] + a3 x pT (with a3 = 0 in some cases)

### MC Simulation
- PYTHIA 8 (Monash 2013 tune): gamma-jet (signal) and jet-jet (background)
- p-Pb: PYTHIA 8 gamma-jet/jet-jet embedded into DPMJET inelastic events
- GEANT3 detector simulation with cross-talk modelling

### Theory Comparison
- JETPHOX 1.3.1 NLO: NNPDF4.0 (proton), nNNPDF3.0 and nCTEQ15HQ (nuclear)
- BFG II fragmentation function
- Isolation: p_T^{iso} < 2 GeV/c in R = 0.4 (higher threshold than data to account for charged-only vs all-particle isolation; calibrated from PYTHIA 8 neutral fraction)
- Scale variations: simultaneous 0.5 pT <= mu <= 2 pT for mu_R, mu_F, mu_f
- PDF uncertainties at 90% CL from (n)PDF replica sets

### Nuclear Modification Factor
- R_pA = d^2 sigma_{pA} / (A_Pb x d^2 sigma_{pp}), with A_Pb = 208
- pp reference at matching sqrt(s): pp at 5.02 TeV (published) and pp at 8 TeV (this paper)
- Centre-of-mass rapidity: eta* (related to lab eta via boost, typically -1.17 < eta* < 0.23)
- Statistical significance of suppression: constant fit R_pA < 1 and linear slope deviation from zero

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Photon purity: dominant (8-15% for cross-section; 9-19% for R_pA)
- Trigger mimicking: 2-8% (data-driven vs MC trigger efficiency)
- UE estimation: 2-4% (perpendicular cone vs kT method)
- Shower shape signal range: 1-7%
- CPV: ~1%
- Energy scale: 2%
- Material budget: 2-3%
- Normalization: 3.6-6.8% (luminosity dependent)
- Total cross-section: 11-17% (pT and system dependent)
- Total R_pA: 11-23% (dominated by purity at low pT)
- ~65% of systematic uncertainty on R_pA is pT-correlated (energy scale, material budget)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Low-pT reach and shadowing sensitivity**: This paper pushes the isolated photon R_pA measurement down to pT = 12 GeV/c, overlapping with the PPG12 pT range (8-35 GeV). The 2.3-sigma hint of shadowing suppression at pT < 20 GeV/c is directly relevant for understanding nuclear PDF effects at RHIC kinematics, where sPHENIX d+Au or Au+Au extensions would probe similar x ranges.
- **ABCD method and template fit**: ALICE uses both ABCD and template-fit purity methods; PPG12 uses the ABCD method. The alpha_MC correction (typical values 1.1-1.4) for shower-shape/isolation correlation is the ALICE equivalent of the signal leakage corrections c_B, c_C, c_D in PPG12.
- **Charged isolation vs calorimetric isolation**: ALICE uses p_T^{iso,ch} < 1.5 GeV/c (charged tracks only), while PPG12 uses calorimetric E_T^iso with parametric cut. The ALICE approach is more robust against neutral UE particles but requires good tracking. The comparison with ATLAS's looser calorimetric isolation suggests tighter isolation reduces fragmentation contribution and improves NLO agreement.
- **Purity at low pT**: Signal purity of ~20% at pT = 12 GeV/c is challenging. ALICE addresses this with sigmoid fits to smooth statistical fluctuations and systematic variations of the ABCD boundaries. PPG12 faces similar challenges at its lowest pT bins and can benefit from these techniques.
- **NLO JETPHOX isolation threshold**: ALICE uses p_T^{iso} < 2 GeV/c in JETPHOX (higher than data's 1.5 GeV/c) to account for the charged-only isolation in data. PPG12 should similarly calibrate the JETPHOX isolation threshold to match its parametric calorimetric isolation.
- **nPDF constraints**: The measurement constrains nPDFs at x ~ 3 x 10^-3, complementary to RHIC x ranges. The comparison with nNNPDF3.0 and nCTEQ15HQ provides benchmark nPDF predictions that can be compared to RHIC-energy expectations.
- **Systematic methodology**: Detailed treatment of trigger mimicking, UE estimation alternatives, and shower-shape variations provides a template for evaluating PPG12 systematics.

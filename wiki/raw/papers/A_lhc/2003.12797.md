# The production of isolated photons in PbPb and pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Abstract

The transverse energy spectra of photons isolated from other particles are measured using proton-proton (pp) and lead-lead (PbPb) collisions at the LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with integrated luminosities of 27.4 pb^-1 and 404 mu_b^-1 for pp and PbPb data, respectively. The results are presented for photons with 25 < ET < 200 GeV in the pseudorapidity range |eta| < 1.44, and for different centrality intervals for PbPb collisions. Photon production in PbPb collisions is consistent with that in pp collisions scaled by the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, demonstrating that photons do not interact with the quark-gluon plasma. Therefore, isolated photons can provide information about the initial energy of the associated parton in photon+jet measurements. The results are compared with predictions from the next-to-leading-order JETPHOX generator for different parton distribution functions (PDFs) and nuclear PDFs (nPDFs).

## Key Results

- Isolated photon cross section measured in pp at 5.02 TeV for 25 < ET < 200 GeV, |eta| < 1.44
- PbPb spectra measured in four centrality bins: 0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-100%, and inclusive 0-100%
- R_AA consistent with unity across all ET and centrality bins, confirming photons do not interact with QGP
- Data consistent with JETPHOX NLO predictions using CT14 PDFs (pp), EPPS16+CT14 nPDFs and nCTEQ15 nPDFs (PbPb)
- First measurement of isolated photons in PbPb at 5.02 TeV; significantly higher precision and larger ET range than previous 2.76 TeV measurement
- Isospin effect visible in CT14(PbPb)/CT14(pp) ratio but small (~few %)

## Methodology

### Event selection
- pp: integrated luminosity 27.4 pb^-1, luminosity uncertainty 2.3%
- PbPb: 404 mu_b^-1, centrality from HF calorimeter, Glauber model for T_AA
- Vertex requirement: |z| < 15 cm, at least 2 tracks
- PbPb additional: HF coincidence, pixel cluster shape compatibility

### Photon reconstruction and identification
- Barrel ECAL only (|eta| < 1.442), PbWO4 crystals
- "Island" clustering for PbPb, "GED" (Global Event Description) for pp
- H/E < 0.1 in cone R = 0.15
- Electron rejection: discard if Delta_eta < 0.03 and Delta_phi < 0.03 between photon and any electron track with pT > 10 GeV
- sigma_eta_eta < 0.01 (tight shower shape cut for prompt photon enrichment)

### Isolation
- I = sum(ET_ECAL) + sum(ET_HCAL) + sum(pT_tracks with pT > 2 GeV) in cone R = 0.4
- Isolation requirement: I < 1 GeV (after underlying event subtraction in PbPb)
- UE correction in PbPb: corrections from PYTHIA+HYDJET simulation

### Signal extraction (sigma_eta_eta template fit)
- Two-component template fit of sigma_eta_eta distribution
- Signal template: simulation (PYTHIA for pp, PYTHIA+HYDJET for PbPb)
- Background template: data nonisolated sideband (1 < I < 5 GeV)
- Signal contamination in sideband subtracted using simulation shape
- Purity determined from template fit; decreases in more central PbPb collisions
- Example: 64.1% purity for 40 < ET < 50 GeV in 10-30% centrality

### Efficiency
- Total efficiency: signal selection x trigger x reconstruction
- Reconstruction efficiency ~99% for both pp and PbPb
- Trigger efficiency from data; scale factors from Z->e+e- tag-and-probe
- Total efficiency ~50-70% depending on ET

### Unfolding
- Matrix inversion method without regularization (RooUnfold)
- Response matrix from PYTHIA+HYDJET (PbPb) or PYTHIA (pp)
- Reweighting to JETPHOX spectrum shape tested as systematic variation

### R_AA calculation
- R_AA = (1/T_AA) * (1/N_MB) * (d^2N_PbPb / dET deta) / (d^2sigma_pp / dET deta)

## Relevance to PPG12

- The sigma_eta_eta template fitting method is the most direct CMS analog to PPG12's ABCD approach. CMS uses the sigma_eta_eta distribution directly as the discriminating variable with signal (MC) and background (nonisolated sideband data) templates. PPG12 instead uses a 2D classification (BDT tight/non-tight x isolated/non-isolated) but the underlying physics is the same: shower shape separates signal from background, with isolation providing an orthogonal handle.
- The nonisolated sideband (1 < I < 5 GeV) for background template extraction is closely related to PPG12's non-isolated region (B, D quadrants in ABCD). Both exploit the assumption that the shower shape distribution of backgrounds is similar in isolated and non-isolated regions.
- Purity values (64% at 40-50 GeV in central PbPb, higher in pp) are comparable in magnitude to PPG12's purity levels at similar ET.
- The pp baseline measurement at 5.02 TeV with JETPHOX comparison provides a direct validation of the same NLO framework used by PPG12 at 200 GeV.
- R_AA ~ 1 for isolated photons is the foundational result demonstrating that photons are unmodified by the QGP, which is the physics motivation for using photon cross sections as clean pQCD probes.
- The pileup systematic (0-11% in pp) is a relevant comparison point for PPG12's double-interaction systematic studies.

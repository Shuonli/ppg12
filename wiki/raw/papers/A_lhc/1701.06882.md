# Measurement of the cross section for inclusive isolated-photon production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using the ATLAS detector

## Abstract

Inclusive isolated-photon production in pp collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV is studied with the ATLAS detector at the LHC using a data set with an integrated luminosity of 3.2 fb^-1. The cross section is measured as a function of the photon transverse energy above 125 GeV in different regions of photon pseudorapidity. Next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD and Monte Carlo event-generator predictions are compared to the cross-section measurements and provide an adequate description of the data.

## Key Results

- Cross-section measured for ET > 125 GeV up to 1.5 TeV, in four eta bins: |eta| < 0.6, 0.6 < |eta| < 1.37, 1.56 < |eta| < 1.81, 1.81 < |eta| < 2.37
- Luminosity: 3.16 +/- 0.07 fb^-1 (2015 data only)
- Fiducial cross-section: sigma_meas = 399 +/- 13 (exp.) +/- 8 (lumi.) pb
- NLO JETPHOX prediction: sigma_NLO = 352 +36/-29 (scale) +/- 3 (PDF) +/- 6 (alpha_s) +/- 4 (non-perturb.) pb; 12% lower than data but consistent within uncertainties
- Cross-sections ~2x larger than 8 TeV at low ET, ~10x at high ET (as expected from increased sqrt(s))
- Signal purity above 90% at ET = 125 GeV, increasing with ET
- NLO theory describes data within 10-15% uncertainties, pointing to need for NNLO corrections
- Pythia needs ~10-14% upward normalization; Sherpa needs ~30% (both LO generators)

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- Tight selection using shower-shape variables in the EM calorimeter
- Separate criteria for converted and unconverted photons
- MC shower-shape corrections applied to match data
- Identification efficiency: 92-98% at ET = 125 GeV, 86-98% at 1 TeV (depending on eta and conversion status)
- Efficiency uncertainty: 1-5% depending on eta and ET
- Insertable B-Layer (IBL) added in 2014, improving tracking for the first time

### Isolation Definition
- Cone size: Delta R = 0.4 using topological clusters
- ET-dependent isolation: **E_T^iso < 4.8 + 4.2 x 10^-3 x E_T^gamma [GeV]** (same as 8 TeV paper)
- Ambient energy correction via jet-area method (k_t algorithm with R = 0.5 used to determine event energy density rho)
- Data-driven corrections to E_T^iso in MC to match peak position in data
- Non-isolated boundary for ABCD: E_T^iso > E_T^iso,cut + 2 GeV and E_T^iso < 50 GeV

### Background Estimation (2D Sideband / ABCD Method)
- Standard ATLAS ABCD equation: N_A^sig = N_A - R_bg * (N_B - f_B * N_A^sig)(N_C - f_C * N_A^sig) / (N_D - f_D * N_A^sig)
- Nominal R_bg = 1 (assumed independence of isolation and identification for background)
- Signal leakage fractions f_K estimated from Pythia MC; Sherpa cross-check
- Non-tight definition: fails at least one of four tight requirements on first-layer EM calorimeter shower shapes, but satisfies total lateral shower width and all other tight criteria
- Signal purity > 90% for ET > 125 GeV in all eta regions
- R_bg systematic: range 0.8-1.2 (central eta) to 0.6-1.4 (forward eta, high ET) based on validation regions

### Unfolding
- Bin-by-bin correction factors C_MC = N_part^MC / N_reco^MC from Pythia
- Correction factors range from 1.04 to 1.24 depending on ET and eta
- Sherpa used as systematic check for both unfolding and signal leakage
- Bayesian iterative unfolding used as cross-check
- Non-perturbative corrections (hadronization + UE): consistent with unity within 1%; 1% uncertainty assigned

- Total experimental systematic: 3-6% at ET = 125 GeV, increasing at high ET
- Dominant sources: photon energy scale, photon identification, R_bg correlation
- Systematic uncertainties evaluated to preserve correlations with 8 TeV measurement for combined PDF fits

### NLO Theory (JETPHOX 1.3.1_2)
- MMHT2014 PDFs (nominal), also CT14, NNPDF3.0
- BFG set II fragmentation functions at NLO
- Scales: mu_R = mu_F = mu_f = ET; 14 variations (simultaneous, individual, fixing one)
- Scale uncertainty: 10-15% (dominant)
- PDF uncertainty (MMHT2014 50 sets, Hessian method): 1% at 125 GeV to 3-4% at high ET
- alpha_s uncertainty: < 2% (varied 0.118 to 0.122 with correlated PDFs)
- Non-perturbative corrections: 1% uncertainty

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD with ET-dependent isolation**: Confirms the ATLAS ABCD method with parametric isolation works at 13 TeV, validating the approach PPG12 uses at 200 GeV.
- **R_bg systematic procedure**: The detailed validation-region approach for testing R_bg = 1 (subdividing background control regions B and D into B', B'', D', D'', varying E_T^iso boundaries by +/- 1 GeV) provides a template for PPG12's background correlation systematics.
- **Systematic uncertainty table**: The granular breakdown (Table 2) with 15 sources provides a reference for PPG12's systematic budget.
- **Non-perturbative corrections**: The 1% uncertainty from hadronization/UE effects illustrates the small size of these corrections at LHC energies; at RHIC energies (PPG12) these may be larger.
- **Correlation preservation**: The treatment of correlated systematics across measurements (for combined PDF fits) is a methodology note for PPG12's multi-variation systematics pipeline.

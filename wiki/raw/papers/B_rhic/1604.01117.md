# Jet-like Correlations with Direct-Photon and Neutral-Pion Triggers at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

STAR analyzes azimuthal correlations of charged hadrons with direct photon (gamma_dir) and neutral pion (pi0) trigger particles in central Au+Au and minimum-bias p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Data were collected in 2011 (Au+Au, 2.8 nb^-1) and 2009 (p+p, 23 pb^-1). The charged-hadron per-trigger yields at mid-rapidity from central Au+Au collisions are compared to p+p to quantify the suppression via the nuclear modification factor I_AA. The suppression of away-side yields per gamma_dir trigger is independent of trigger p_T in the range 8-20 GeV/c, whereas the suppression is smaller at low associated hadron p_T. Within uncertainty, similar levels of suppression (factor ~3-5 at high z_T) are observed for gamma_dir and pi0 triggers as a function of z_T, despite expected differences from geometric biases and the color factor. The modified fragmentation function is not universal: lost energy reappears predominantly at low p_T (below ~2 GeV/c) regardless of trigger energy.

## Key Results

- Away-side I_AA for both gamma_dir and pi0 triggers shows suppression by a factor ~3-5 at high z_T (z_T > 0.3) in 0-12% central Au+Au
- I_AA^gamma_dir and I_AA^pi0 are similar within uncertainties as a function of z_T, meaning the expected differences from color-factor effects (gluon vs quark jets on away side) and path-length dependence are not resolved
- At low z_T (0.1-0.2), both I_AA^gamma_dir and I_AA^pi0 show indication of less suppression (approaching or exceeding unity), consistent with energy redistribution to soft particles
- I_AA^gamma_dir is independent of trigger p_T for 0.3 < z_T < 0.4, indicating that the energy loss of the away-side parton is not sensitive to the initial parton energy in the range 8-20 GeV/c
- I_AA^gamma_dir as a function of p_T^assoc shows low-p_T hadrons are less suppressed than high-p_T hadrons
- No enhancement at low z_T for wide-angle integration (|Delta_phi - pi| < 1.4 rad) compared to narrow (|Delta_phi - pi| < 0.6 rad), except marginally for z_T < 0.2 with pi0 triggers
- The pi0 trigger carries ~80 +/- 5% of the original parton p_T (from PYTHIA), so z_T for pi0 triggers approximates z_T^corr = p_T^assoc / p_T^jet; D(z_T^corr) for pi0 agrees with D(z_T) for gamma_dir in p+p
- The direct photon fraction (1-R) in the gamma_rich sample is 40% in p+p and 70% in central Au+Au
- The modified fragmentation function is not universal: lost energy reappears at fixed low p_T (~2 GeV/c), not at fixed z_T

## Methodology

### Detector and Data
- STAR Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter (BEMC): lead-scintillator sampling calorimeter, towers of 0.05 x 0.05 in eta x phi
- BEMC Barrel Shower Maximum Detector (BSMD): wire chamber at ~5 radiation lengths, granularity 0.007 x 0.007 in eta x phi
- Time Projection Chamber (TPC): charged-particle tracking with |eta| < 1.0
- Au+Au: 2.8 nb^-1 integrated luminosity (2011), centrality from TPC multiplicity in |eta| < 0.5
- p+p: 23 pb^-1 (2009)
- Event trigger: ET > 8 GeV in a BEMC cluster with |eta| < 0.9

### Photon/pi0 Discrimination (Transverse Shower Profile)
- TSP = E_cluster / (sum_i e_i * r_i^1.5), where e_i are individual BSMD strip energies and r_i are distances from cluster center
- pi0_rich sample: TSP < 0.08 (~95% pure pi0)
- gamma_rich sample: 0.2 < TSP < 0.6 (enhanced direct photon fraction)
- Track rejection: no charged track with p > 3 GeV/c pointing at trigger tower (varied 1-4 GeV/c as systematic)

### Direct Photon Yield Extraction
- Exploits the zero near-side yield for true gamma_dir triggers
- Y_gamma_dir = (Y_away_gamma_rich - R * Y_away_pi0_rich) / (1 - R)
- R = Y_near_gamma_rich / Y_near_pi0_rich (ratio of near-side yields)
- 1 - R = N_gamma_dir / N_gamma_rich represents the signal fraction
- Signal fraction: 40% (p+p), 70% (Au+Au)
- Almost all background sources (asymmetric decay photons, fragmentation photons) removed, validated via PYTHIA to within 15%

### Correlation Analysis
- Azimuthal correlation: (1/N_trig)(dN_assoc/d(Delta_phi))
- Associated hadrons: 1.2 < p_T^assoc < p_T^trig, |eta| < 1.0, integrated over Delta_eta = 2
- Trigger selection: 12 < p_T^trig < 20 GeV/c (or 8 < p_T^trig < 20 GeV/c for trigger p_T dependence study)
- Background subtraction: mixed-event technique for pair-acceptance correction
- Au+Au: flow-modulated background B[1 + 2<v2^trig><v2^assoc>cos(2*Delta_phi)], level set by ZYA1 (zero yield at 1 radian) method
- p+p: flat uncorrelated background
- Near-side integration: |Delta_phi| < 1.4 rad; away-side integration: |Delta_phi - pi| < 1.4 rad (also 0.6 rad for systematic study)
- Tracking efficiency: ~70% (central Au+Au), ~90% (min-bias p+p)

### Systematic Uncertainties
- Dominant: pi0/gamma TSP discrimination (10-25%)
- Track quality: 5-10%
- Yield extraction at low z_T in Au+Au: up to 50% (near-side pi0)
- v2, ZYA1 normalization range, integration windows all evaluated per data point
- Independent sources added in quadrature; correlated sources take maximum deviation

## Relevance to PPG12

- The STAR BEMC+BSMD Transverse Shower Profile (TSP) method for gamma/pi0 discrimination is the most direct predecessor to the sPHENIX CEMC shower-shape BDT approach in PPG12. Both exploit the electromagnetic shower width difference between single photons and merged pi0 decay photons at high p_T.
- Trigger p_T range (8-20 GeV/c for gamma_dir) overlaps substantially with the PPG12 reco p_T range (8-35 GeV), making this the most directly comparable RHIC gamma-triggered measurement in terms of kinematics
- The p+p baseline at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (23 pb^-1) is a key prior measurement at the same collision energy as PPG12. The per-trigger yields can be cross-checked against the PPG12 isolated photon cross section convolved with NLO fragmentation functions.
- The result that I_AA is independent of trigger p_T in 8-20 GeV/c motivates extending the measurement to higher p_T with sPHENIX, where PPG12 reaches 35 GeV
- Demonstrates that at RHIC energies, direct photon and pi0 triggers yield similar away-side suppression, providing physics context for why the isolated photon cross section (without requiring away-side measurement) is a clean test of pQCD at these energies
- The 1-R signal extraction method (using near-side yields to determine the gamma_rich purity) is conceptually related to the ABCD method in PPG12, where non-tight/non-isolated control regions constrain the background, though PPG12 uses shower-shape BDT score and isolation simultaneously rather than a single TSP variable
- The observation that lost energy appears at low p_T (< 2 GeV/c) rather than at fixed z_T means that isolation cones in PPG12 (R = 0.3 with ET-dependent threshold) may capture some of this redistributed energy, which is important for understanding the connection between inclusive and isolated photon yields

# Medium modification of jet fragmentation in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV measured in direct photon-hadron correlations

## Abstract

The jet fragmentation function is measured with direct photon-hadron correlations in p+p and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The p_T of the photon is an excellent approximation to the initial p_T of the jet and the ratio z_T = p_T^h / p_T^gamma is used as a proxy for the jet fragmentation function. A statistical subtraction is used to extract direct photon-hadron yields in Au+Au while a photon isolation cut is applied in p+p. I_AA, the ratio of jet fragment yield in Au+Au to p+p, indicates modification of the jet fragmentation function. Suppression, most likely due to energy loss in the medium, is seen at high z_T. The fragment yield at low z_T is enhanced at large angles.

## Key Results

- First evidence for medium modification of the jet fragmentation function at RHIC via direct photon-hadron correlations
- I_AA shows suppression at low xi (high z_T > 0.45), consistent with parton energy loss
- I_AA shows enhancement at high xi (low z_T < 0.2), consistent with energy redistribution to soft particles
- chi2/dof = 17.6/4 for hypothesis I_AA = 1 at xi > 0.8, probability < 0.1% -- statistically significant modification
- Enhancement at large xi is predominantly at large angles (|Delta_phi - pi| > pi/6): ratio of I_AA(full) to I_AA(narrow) is 1.9 +/- 0.3(stat) +/- 0.3(syst) for xi > 0.8
- Restricting away-side integration to |Delta_phi - pi| < pi/6 eliminates the enhancement while preserving suppression at low xi
- Photon trigger: 5 < p_T^gamma < 9 GeV/c; hadron: 0.5 < p_T^h < 7 GeV/c
- Fragmentation function variable: xi = ln(1/z_T), extending to z_T ~ 0.1

## Methodology

### Photon Identification

**Au+Au (statistical subtraction):**
- Inclusive photon-hadron correlations measured in PHENIX central arms (|eta| < 0.35, pi/2 azimuth per arm)
- Electromagnetic calorimeters (PbSc and PbGl) for photon measurement
- Direct photon yield extracted via R_gamma method: Y_dir = (R_gamma * Y_inc - Y_dec) / (R_gamma - 1)
- R_gamma from previous PHENIX direct photon measurements
- Decay photon correlations from pi0-h correlations translated via MC weighting (same method as 0903.3399)
- Background subtraction: absolute normalization with v2 flow modulation, v3 effect included as systematic uncertainty
- Cannot use isolation cut in high-multiplicity Au+Au environment

**p+p (isolation cut):**
- Photon isolation cut applied: total transverse electromagnetic energy and charged track momentum within cone R = 0.3 around photon must be less than 10% of photon energy
- Photons combining with another photon to form pi0 or eta mass are rejected event-by-event
- Additional statistical subtraction for remaining decay photons that appear isolated or whose partner is lost
- Background: ZYAM (zero yield at minimum), flat in Delta_phi since no flow in p+p
- Isolation-cut method gives smaller uncertainties than pure statistical subtraction

### Correlation Analysis
- Charged hadrons from drift chambers and pad chambers
- Two-particle correlations: real/mixed event ratio, acceptance-corrected
- Background modulation: 1 + 2<v2^t * v2^a> cos(2 Delta_phi), with v3 systematic
- Associated hadron efficiency from GEANT simulation: 8.8% (Au+Au) and 8.0% (p+p) normalization uncertainty
- Fragmentation function measured as function of xi = ln(1/z_T) for full away-side (|Delta_phi - pi| < pi/2) and restricted ranges

### Data
- Au+Au: 3.9 billion minimum bias events (2007) + 2.9 billion (2010), 0-40% centrality
- p+p: 0.5 billion photon-triggered events from 2005 (3.8 pb^-1) and 2006 (10.7 pb^-1)
- PHENIX detector: EMCal, drift chambers, pad chambers, BBC for centrality/vertex

### Model Comparisons
- BW-MLLA (Borghini-Wiedemann modified leading log approximation): modified parton splitting probability in medium, predicts suppression at high z and enhancement at low z; reproduces pi0 R_AA for 0-10% central
- YaJEM-DE (Yet Another Jet Energy Loss Model): traces radiated gluon energy redistribution, predicts gamma_dir-h I_AA for 9-12 GeV/c triggers at 0-40% centrality
- Both models qualitatively reproduce the trend of suppression at low xi transitioning to enhancement at high xi, but do not reproduce the exact transition location

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Isolation cut in p+p**: This paper uses a photon isolation cut with cone R = 0.3 and threshold < 10% of photon energy in p+p, which is conceptually identical to the PPG12 parametric isolation cut (R = 0.3 with ET-dependent threshold). This is the closest RHIC precedent for the PPG12 isolation strategy.
- **Same collision system and energy**: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV p+p, same as PPG12. The p+p baseline fragmentation function measured here is directly enabled by a precise isolated photon cross section like PPG12.
- **Photon pT range overlap**: The 5-9 GeV/c photon trigger range is below the PPG12 range (8-35 GeV), but the methods overlap at 8-9 GeV.
- **Statistical subtraction limitations**: The paper demonstrates that isolation cuts cannot be applied in Au+Au (motivating statistical subtraction), while PPG12 works in p+p where isolation cuts are directly applicable.
- **Fragmentation function proxy**: The z_T variable used here directly depends on the photon p_T calibration that PPG12 measures.
- **EMCal shower shape + charged track veto**: The photon ID method (shower shape cuts, charged hadron veto) is a precursor to the PPG12 BDT approach which incorporates these features as input variables.
- **pi0/eta rejection**: Event-by-event pi0/eta mass-window rejection combined with isolation is a key technique that PPG12 extends with BDT-based shower-shape discrimination.

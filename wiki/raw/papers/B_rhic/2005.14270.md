# Measurement of jet-medium interactions via direct photon-hadron correlations in Au+Au and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

PHENIX presents direct photon-hadron correlations in 200 GeV Au+Au, d+Au, and p+p collisions, for direct photon p_T from 5-12 GeV/c. The data combine Au+Au runs from 2007, 2010, and 2011 (totaling ~11.2 billion minimum-bias events) with d+Au data from 2008 and p+p baselines from 2005-2006. No significant modification of jet fragmentation is observed in d+Au collisions, indicating that cold nuclear matter effects are small or absent. In Au+Au, hadrons carrying a large fraction of the quark's momentum (low xi = ln(1/z_T)) are suppressed compared to p+p and d+Au. As the momentum fraction decreases (high xi), the yield of hadrons in Au+Au increases to an excess over p+p. The excess is at large angles and at low hadron p_T, and is most pronounced for hadrons associated with lower momentum direct photons. Comparison to CoLBT-hydro and BW-MLLA theoretical calculations suggests that the hadron excess arises from medium response to energy deposited by jets.

## Key Results

- First d+Au direct photon-hadron measurement at RHIC: I_dA is consistent with unity across all xi bins, confirming negligible cold nuclear matter effects on jet fragmentation (chi2/ndf values: 7.4/5, 4.0/5, 10.0/5 for photon p_T bins 5-7, 7-9, 9-12 GeV/c)
- I_AA shows suppression at low xi (high z_T, high momentum hadrons) and enhancement at high xi (low z_T, soft hadrons) in 0-40% central Au+Au
- Differential photon p_T dependence: the transition where I_AA exceeds unity occurs at increasing xi as the direct photon p_T increases (i.e., enhancement appears at approximately fixed low hadron p_T, not at fixed z_T)
- Enhancement is largest for the lowest photon p_T bin (5-7 GeV/c) and for the full away-side integration range (|Delta_phi - pi| < pi/2), indicating excess particles appear predominantly at large angles
- Angular dependence study: comparing integration windows of |Delta_phi - pi| < pi/6, pi/3, and pi/2 shows the jet core is suppressed similarly, while the enhancement grows at large angles
- Ratio of I_AA(high xi) / I_AA(low xi) decreases with increasing photon p_T, meaning softer jets are broadened more than harder jets
- CoLBT-hydro model (jet transport + hydrodynamic medium response) reproduces the data trends across all three photon p_T bins

## Methodology

### Photon Identification and Direct Photon Extraction
- EMCal: PbSc (lead-scintillator, 4 sectors in west arm, 2 in east) and PbGl (lead-glass Cherenkov, 2 sectors in east arm)
- Energy resolution: sigma_E/E = 8.1%/sqrt(E) + 2.1% (PbSc), 5.9%/sqrt(E) + 0.8% (PbGl)
- Segmentation: Delta_eta x Delta_phi ~ 0.011 x 0.011 (PbSc), 0.008 x 0.008 (PbGl)
- Shower-shape cut for photon selection; charged track veto applied to EMCal clusters
- Acceptance: |eta| < 0.35, two arms of 90 degrees each in azimuth

### Au+Au Statistical Subtraction
- Uses R_gamma = N_inclusive / N_decay measured independently
- Y_direct = (R_gamma * Y_inclusive - Y_decay) / (R_gamma - 1)
- Decay photon-hadron correlations estimated via MC pair-by-pair mapping from pi0-hadron correlations
- 20% of decay yield from non-pi0 meson decays (eta, etc.)
- pi0 reconstructed via invariant mass 120-160 MeV/c2 up to p_T ~ 15 GeV (merging limit)
- Background subtraction: absolute normalization with v2 flow modulation
- b0 * (1 + 2 * v2^trigger * v2^associate * cos(2*Delta_phi))

### d+Au Isolation + Tagging Method
- Event-by-event isolation cut plus pi0 tagging (different from Au+Au statistical subtraction)
- pi0 tagging: pairs of photons with p_T > 0.5 GeV/c, invariant mass 120-160 MeV/c2
- Isolation criterion: sum of energy in cone R < R_max around trigger photon must be < 10% of photon energy + average background energy
- Cone size R_max = 0.4; underlying event correction applied per centrality class
- Residual falsely-isolated decay photons subtracted via R_tilde = N_iso_inc / N_iso_dec factor
- ZYAM background subtraction in restricted Delta_phi range 0.9-1.6 rad

### Kinematic Variables
- xi = ln(1/z_T) where z_T = p_T^hadron / p_T^gamma; focuses on the low-z_T (high-xi) enhancement region
- Direct photon triggers: 5-15 GeV/c, split into bins: 5-7, 7-9, 9-12 GeV/c
- Associated hadron p_T: 0.5-7 GeV/c; |eta| < 0.35
- I_AA = Y_AA / Y_pp quantifies nuclear modification

### Data Sets
- Au+Au: 4.4 billion MB events (2011) + 3.9 billion (2007) + 2.9 billion (2010), 0-40% centrality
- d+Au: 3 billion events (2008), minimum bias, with EMCal photon trigger
- p+p: from 2005-2006 (Phys. Rev. D 82, 072001)

## Relevance to PPG12

- Provides the most comprehensive PHENIX direct photon-hadron result at the same sqrt(s) = 200 GeV as PPG12, establishing physics context for isolated photon measurements at this energy
- The photon p_T range (5-12 GeV/c) overlaps the lower end of the PPG12 p_T range (8-35 GeV), and the p+p baseline data are a consistency check for the PPG12 cross section
- The d+Au result confirming negligible cold nuclear matter effects is important for interpreting the p+p isolated photon cross section as a QCD baseline (i.e., no initial-state nuclear modifications at these kinematics)
- Shower-shape analysis in PbSc/PbGl calorimeters is a predecessor to the BDT-based shower-shape discrimination used in PPG12 for the sPHENIX CEMC
- The statistical subtraction technique (R_gamma method) used in Au+Au is replaced in PPG12 by the ABCD method with BDT + isolation, which provides event-by-event photon identification rather than statistical extraction
- The isolation cone approach used in the d+Au analysis (R = 0.4, 10% energy threshold) is conceptually similar to the parametric isolation cut in PPG12 (R = 0.3, ET-dependent threshold), though the specific implementations differ
- Demonstrates that understanding the direct photon p_T spectrum precisely in p+p is essential for any gamma-jet or photon-hadron physics program at RHIC -- PPG12 provides this spectrum with sPHENIX

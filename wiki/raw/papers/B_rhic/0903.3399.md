# Photon-Hadron Jet Correlations in p+p and Au+Au Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

PHENIX reports the observation of suppression of back-to-back correlations in the direct photon+jet channel in Au+Au relative to p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Two-particle correlations of direct photon triggers with associated hadrons are obtained by statistical subtraction of the decay photon-hadron background. The away-side nuclear suppression factor I_AA in central Au+Au collisions is 0.32 +/- 0.12(stat) +/- 0.09(syst) for hadrons of 3 < p_T^h < 5 GeV/c in coincidence with photons of 5 < p_T^gamma < 15 GeV/c. The direct photon associated yields in p+p scale approximately with z_T = p_T^h / p_T^gamma, as expected for a measure of the away-side parton fragmentation function.

## Key Results

- Away-side I_AA = 0.32 +/- 0.12(stat) +/- 0.09(syst) for central Au+Au (0-20%), demonstrating strong suppression comparable to single-hadron and dihadron suppression
- Direct photon per-trigger yields in p+p scale with z_T, confirming they measure the away-side parton fragmentation function
- Near-side direct photon associated yields are small relative to decay photons, confirming prompt photon production signature
- Suppression observed across centralities (0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%) with decreasing effect in more peripheral collisions
- Photon trigger p_T range: 5-15 GeV/c; associated hadron p_T: 1-5 GeV/c

## Methodology

### Photon Identification
- EMCal cluster shower-shape cuts provide >98% photon candidate purity for energies > 5 GeV
- Two EMCal types: PbSc (6 sectors, sigma_E/E = 8.1%/sqrt(E) + 2.1%) and PbGl (2 sectors, sigma_E/E = 5.9%/sqrt(E) + 0.8%)
- Fine segmentation: Delta_eta x Delta_phi ~ 0.01 x 0.01 (PbSc) and ~0.008 x 0.008 (PbGl)
- Charged-particle veto applied to EMCal clusters
- Merged pi0 clusters rejected via anomalous shower shape above ~13 GeV/c

### Direct Photon Extraction (Statistical Subtraction)
- Cannot use isolation cuts in high-multiplicity Au+Au environment
- Uses R_gamma = N_gamma_inclusive / N_gamma_decay from previous PHENIX measurements
- Y_direct = (R_gamma * Y_inclusive - Y_decay) / (R_gamma - 1)
- Decay photon yields estimated from measured pi0-h and eta-h correlations via MC-based pair-by-pair weighting
- Weight function W(p_T^gamma, p_T^pi) combines decay probability density P and single-photon efficiency epsilon_gamma
- Cluster merging efficiency from GEANT simulation
- eta/pi0 ratio delta_gamma_h/pi0 = 1.24 +/- 0.05

### Two-Particle Correlations
- C(Delta_phi) = N_real / N_mixed corrects for acceptance
- Jet pair ratio function: JPR = C(Delta_phi) - xi(1 + 2<v2^gamma><v2^h> cos(2 Delta_phi))
- Two background methods: ZYAM (zero yield at minimum) and ABS (absolute normalization)
- ABS method used for Au+Au results; ZYAM for p+p
- Away-side yields integrated over |Delta_phi - pi| < pi/5

### Data
- Au+Au: ~950 million minimum bias events (2004 RHIC run)
- p+p: 471 million photon-triggered events from 2005 (3 pb^-1) and 2006 (10.7 pb^-1)
- PHENIX central arms: +/-0.35 pseudorapidity, 90 degrees azimuth each

## Relevance to PPG12

- Establishes the statistical subtraction method (R_gamma technique) for extracting direct photon signals at RHIC, which PPG12 replaces with shower-shape BDT + isolation + ABCD
- Demonstrates that at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the direct photon p+p baseline is essential for gamma-jet measurements in heavy-ion collisions
- The EMCal shower-shape analysis for photon/pi0 discrimination is a precursor to the BDT-based approach in PPG12
- Photon p_T range (5-15 GeV/c) overlaps the lower end of the PPG12 range (8-35 GeV)
- The p+p direct photon cross section measured here serves as a consistency check for the PPG12 isolated photon cross section
- Highlights the importance of understanding decay photon backgrounds, which PPG12 addresses via tight BDT + isolation cuts rather than statistical subtraction

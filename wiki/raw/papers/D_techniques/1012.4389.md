# Measurement of the Inclusive Isolated Prompt Photon Cross Section in pp Collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV with the ATLAS Detector

## Abstract

A measurement of the cross section for the inclusive production of isolated prompt photons in pp collisions at a centre-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is presented. The measurement covers the pseudorapidity ranges |eta_gamma| < 1.37 and 1.52 <= |eta_gamma| < 1.81 in the transverse energy range 15 <= E_T^gamma < 100 GeV. The results are based on an integrated luminosity of 880 nb^{-1}, collected with the ATLAS detector at the LHC. Photon candidates are identified by combining information from the calorimeters and from the inner tracker. Residual background in the selected sample is estimated from data based on the observed distribution of the transverse isolation energy in a narrow cone around the photon candidate. The results are compared to predictions from next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations.

## Key Results / Algorithm

### ABCD Sideband Counting Method (Section VII.A)

The method uses a 2D plane defined by two discriminating axes:

| | Isolated (E_T^iso < 3 GeV) | Non-isolated (E_T^iso > 5 GeV) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tight** (pass ID) | **A** (signal region) | **B** (bkg control) |
| **Non-tight** (fail ID subset) | **C** (bkg control) | **D** (bkg control) |

**Basic formula** (assuming no signal leakage into control regions):

```
N_A^sig = N_A - N_B * N_C / N_D
```

This assumes the isolation and identification variables are uncorrelated for background, so that the background populates the 2D plane uniformly: `N_A^bkg / N_B^bkg = N_C^bkg / N_D^bkg`.

### Signal Leakage Corrections (Eq. 2)

Signal photons can leak into the control regions B, C, D. The corrected formula accounts for this:

```
N_A^sig = N_A - (N_B - c_B * N_A^sig) * (N_C - c_C * N_A^sig) / (N_D - c_D * N_A^sig)
```

where the leakage fractions are:

```
c_K = N_K^sig / N_A^sig    (for K in {B, C, D})
```

These are extracted from signal MC simulation. Typical values:
- **c_B**: 3% to 17%, increasing with E_T^gamma (signal photons leaking into non-isolated region)
- **c_C**: 2% to 14%, decreasing with E_T^gamma (signal photons failing tight ID)
- **c_D**: always less than 2% (signal in both non-tight and non-isolated)

Total effect of leakage corrections on measured purity: typically < 5%.

Note: This equation is implicit in N_A^sig and must be solved iteratively or algebraically.

### Background Correlation Correction (Eq. 4, Section IX.B.6)

If background isolation and identification are correlated (R_bkg != 1), the formula becomes:

```
N_A^sig = N_A - R_bkg * (N_B - c_B * N_A^sig) * (N_C - c_C * N_A^sig) / (N_D - c_D * N_A^sig)
```

where:

```
R_bkg = (N_A^bkg * N_D^bkg) / (N_B^bkg * N_C^bkg)
```

ATLAS finds this correlation is small but non-negligible: < 0.6% effect for |eta| < 1.37, ~3.6% for 1.52 <= |eta| < 1.81.

### Non-Tight Definition

"Non-tight" candidates fail at least one of a subset of tight identification criteria (shower shape variables based on the first layer of the electromagnetic calorimeter), but pass all criteria not in that subset. The specific variables used for the non-tight definition are:
- Total RMS width w_{s,tot} of energy distribution along eta
- Energy asymmetry E_ratio between first and second maxima
- Energy difference Delta_E between second maximum and minimum
- Fraction F_side of energy in 7 strips not contained in 3 core strips
- RMS width w_{s,3} of energy distribution in 3 core strips

This subset is chosen so that the correlation between isolation and identification for background is minimized.

### Photon Identification Variables (Appendix A)

ATLAS uses multiple shower shape variables organized by calorimeter layer:

**Second (middle) layer:**
- R_had (hadronic leakage ratio)
- R_eta = E^{S2}_{3x7} / E^{S2}_{7x7} (eta energy ratio)
- R_phi = E^{S2}_{3x3} / E^{S2}_{3x7} (phi energy ratio)
- w_2 (lateral width)

**First (front/strip) layer:**
- w_{s,tot} (total lateral width)
- E_ratio (first/second maximum asymmetry)
- Delta_E (second maximum minus minimum energy)
- F_side (side energy fraction)
- w_{s,3} (3-strip lateral width)

### Isolation Definition

- Cone radius R = 0.4 in eta-phi space
- E_T^iso computed from electromagnetic + hadronic calorimeter cells
- Core region (5x7 cells around photon) excluded from sum
- Photon leakage correction applied (function of photon E_T)
- Ambient energy density correction for underlying event/pileup (jet-area method)
- Signal region: E_T^iso < 3 GeV
- Control region: E_T^iso > 5 GeV

### Cross-Section Formula (Eq. 3)

```
d_sigma / d_E_T^gamma = N_yield * U / (L_int * Delta_E_T * epsilon_trigger * epsilon_reco * epsilon_ID)
```

where:
- N_yield: signal yield from ABCD
- U: unfolding coefficient (bin-by-bin correction for energy resolution/scale)
- L_int: integrated luminosity (880 nb^{-1})
- Delta_E_T: bin width
- epsilon_trigger, epsilon_reco, epsilon_ID: trigger, reconstruction, identification efficiencies

The unfolding coefficients U are bin-by-bin correction factors (ratio of true to reconstructed E_T distributions from MC), justified by small bin-to-bin migrations (few percent). Values are slightly above 1 and decrease toward 1 at high E_T.

### Cross-Check: Isolation Template Fit (Section VII.B)

An alternative method fits the E_T^iso distribution of tight photon candidates to signal + background templates:
- Signal template: from W/Z -> e+e- electrons in data (similar E_T^iso profile to photons)
- Background template: from non-tight (reverse-cut) candidates in data
- Binned maximum likelihood fit
- Results agree with ABCD within 2% and within systematic uncertainties

### Systematic Uncertainties

Major sources:
1. **Energy scale** (~10% on cross section, fully correlated across E_T bins)
2. **Luminosity** (11%)
3. **Identification efficiency** (shower shape corrections, material upstream)
4. **Signal yield** (non-tight definition, isolation control region boundaries, leakage corrections, background correlation R_bkg)
5. **Unfolding** (energy scale, resolution, MC model dependence)

### Results

Cross section measured in 8 E_T bins from 15 to 100 GeV across three eta regions. Good agreement with JETPHOX NLO pQCD for E_T > 25 GeV. Below 25 GeV, JETPHOX predictions are higher than measured, but both measurement and theory have large uncertainties at low x_T = 2*E_T/sqrt(s).

## Methodology

The analysis follows a standard prompt photon measurement strategy: reconstruct and identify photon candidates using calorimeter shower shapes, apply isolation to suppress hadronic background, estimate residual background via data-driven ABCD method, correct for efficiency and detector effects, and compare to NLO pQCD (JETPHOX with CTEQ 6.6 PDFs).

## Relevance to PPG12

- **ABCD method template**: PPG12 directly adapts the ATLAS ABCD sideband method for sPHENIX isolated photon analysis. The 2x2 matrix structure is identical:
  - Axis 1: BDT score (tight vs non-tight) -- analogous to ATLAS shower shape ID
  - Axis 2: Isolation (isolated vs non-isolated) -- cone R=0.3, parametric cut
  - Signal region A: tight + isolated
  - Background estimated as: `B_A = R * (B * C) / D` with leakage corrections c_B, c_C, c_D
- **Leakage corrections**: PPG12 computes c_B, c_C, c_D from signal MC, identical in concept to ATLAS Eq. 2
- **R_bkg correlation factor**: PPG12's ABCD implementation in `CalculatePhotonYield.C` includes the correlation factor R (evaluated from MC or data sidebands)
- **Parametric cuts**: While ATLAS uses flat thresholds, PPG12 uses ET-dependent parametric cuts for both BDT (`tight_bdt_min = intercept + slope * ET`) and isolation (`reco_iso_max = b + s * ET`), which is an enhancement
- **Cross-section formula**: PPG12 uses an analogous formula: yield / (luminosity * bin_width * efficiency * purity), with Bayesian unfolding instead of bin-by-bin correction factors
- **Comparison to NLO**: PPG12 compares to JETPHOX NLO pQCD, same as ATLAS
- **Systematic variations**: PPG12's `make_bdt_variations.py` generates systematic configs that vary the ABCD region boundaries, isolation thresholds, and BDT cuts, analogous to ATLAS systematics on the non-tight definition and isolation control regions

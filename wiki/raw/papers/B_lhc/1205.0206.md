# Studies of jet quenching using isolated-photon+jet correlations in PbPb and pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

## Abstract

First study of isolated-photon+jet correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using 150 ub^-1 of PbPb data and 200 nb^-1 of pp data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV from CMS, photon+jet pT imbalance is measured as a function of collision centrality. For isolated photons with pT^gamma > 60 GeV/c and associated jets with pT^jet > 30 GeV/c, a significant decrease in x_Jgamma = pT^jet / pT^gamma is observed in central PbPb relative to PYTHIA+HYDJET reference. More photons are found without an associated jet above threshold, while no significant broadening of the azimuthal correlation is seen.

## Key Results

- **x_Jgamma (momentum imbalance)**: Mean value drops from 0.86 (pp/PYTHIA reference) to 0.73 +/- 0.02(stat) +/- 0.04(syst) in 0-10% central PbPb, demonstrating significant jet energy loss
- **R_Jgamma (jet survival fraction)**: Fraction of photons with an associated jet above 30 GeV/c decreases from ~0.69 (pp/PYTHIA) to 0.49 +/- 0.03(stat) +/- 0.02(syst) in central PbPb -- an additional 20% of jets fall below threshold due to energy loss
- **Azimuthal correlation sigma(Delta_phi_Jgamma)**: No significant centrality-dependent broadening observed; jets remain back-to-back with photon even in central collisions
- **Centrality dependence**: Progressive shift of x_Jgamma distribution toward lower values and progressive decrease of R_Jgamma from peripheral to central collisions
- **pT ranges**: pT^gamma > 60 GeV/c, pT^jet > 30 GeV/c, |eta^gamma| < 1.44, |eta^jet| < 1.6

## Methodology

- **Photon isolation**: UE-subtracted sum of transverse energy in cone Delta_R = 0.4 required to be < 1 GeV (tightened from 5 GeV MC threshold to suppress UE fluctuations in PbPb)
- **Photon identification**: Shower shape variable sigma_eta_eta < 0.01; H/E < 0.1; purity estimated via template fit to sigma_eta_eta distribution (74-83% depending on pT and centrality)
- **Jet reconstruction**: Anti-kT with R = 0.3, particle-flow based; UE subtraction following iterative method; small R minimizes sensitivity to UE fluctuations
- **Background subtraction**: (1) Decay photon background from sideband shower-shape region (0.011 < sigma_eta_eta < 0.017), scaled by background fraction; (2) Fake/combinatoric jet background from event mixing (photon paired with jets from minimum-bias events of matching centrality)
- **Centrality bins**: 0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-100% based on HF transverse energy
- **MC reference**: PYTHIA (Z2 tune) embedded in HYDJET for UE simulation; validated against data via Z boson mass reconstruction

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Establishes the golden channel**: This paper launched the photon-tagged jet program at the LHC and demonstrated why isolated photons are the ideal calibrated probe of parton energy loss. The photon's pT provides a direct estimate of the initial parton pT before energy loss, which dijet measurements cannot provide.
- **PPG12 provides the essential pp baseline at RHIC energy**: sPHENIX will make analogous gamma-jet measurements at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, where the quark/gluon mixture differs from LHC. The PPG12 isolated photon cross section in pp provides the reference spectrum needed for R_AA measurements.
- **Complementary RHIC energy regime**: At RHIC energies, the QGP temperature and path length are different from LHC, probing different aspects of the jet-medium interaction. PPG12's photon measurement is the first step toward the sPHENIX gamma-jet program.
- **Technique validation**: The ABCD background subtraction method used in PPG12 (tight/non-tight x isolated/non-isolated) is closely related to the CMS template-fit approach for photon purity, providing cross-validation of the methodology.

# Measurement of the cross section for isolated-photon plus jet production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using the ATLAS detector

## Abstract

Dynamics of isolated-photon production in association with a jet in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV are studied with 3.2 fb^-1 of ATLAS data. Cross sections are measured as functions of photon ET (> 125 GeV), leading jet pT (> 100 GeV), azimuthal separation Delta_phi, photon-jet invariant mass m_gamma-jet, and scattering angle cos(theta*). Tree-level plus parton-shower predictions from Sherpa and Pythia, as well as NLO QCD predictions from JETPHOX and NLO Sherpa, are compared to data. The dominant production process is qg -> q gamma (t-channel quark exchange).

## Key Results

- **Fiducial cross section**: sigma_meas = 300 +/- 10(exp) +/- 6(lumi) pb; JETPHOX NLO = 291 +25/-21(scale) pb; NLO Sherpa = 319 +54/-45(scale) pb -- both consistent with data
- **ET^gamma distribution**: Measured over nearly six orders of magnitude from 125 GeV to ~1.5 TeV; NLO JETPHOX agrees within ~10% across entire range; NLO Sherpa agrees within uncertainties but has larger scale uncertainty (15-25%)
- **cos(theta*) distribution**: Confirms dominance of t-channel quark exchange, exhibiting (1 - |cos theta*|)^-1 behaviour rather than the (1 - |cos theta*|)^-2 characteristic of gluon exchange in dijets
- **Azimuthal separation**: Peak near pi (back-to-back), tail toward pi/2 from higher-order radiation; NLO Sherpa better describes the away-from-pi region
- **Direct vs fragmentation**: Fragmentation component decreases from ~35% at ET = 125 GeV to ~15% at ET = 1 TeV in Pythia; LO Sherpa includes this via higher-multiplicity matrix elements
- **Signal purity**: Above 90% in all bins after tight identification and isolation requirements
- **Systematic uncertainties**: Total 4-10% depending on observable; dominated by jet and photon energy scales

## Methodology

- **Photon selection**: ET^gamma > 125 GeV, |eta^gamma| < 2.37 (excluding 1.37-1.56 crack region), tight identification based on shower shapes, isolation E_T^iso < 4.2e-3 * ET^gamma + 10 GeV (ET-dependent parametric cut)
- **Jet selection**: Anti-kT R = 0.4, pT > 100 GeV, |y^jet| < 2.37; jet-photon overlap removal at Delta_R = 0.8
- **Background subtraction (ABCD method)**: Two-dimensional sideband in (E_T^iso, tight/non-tight) plane. Signal = tight + isolated (region A); backgrounds from tight+non-isolated (B), non-tight+isolated (C), non-tight+non-isolated (D). Signal yield extracted via: N_sig = N_A - R_bg * (N_B - f_B * N_sig) * (N_C - f_C * N_sig) / (N_D - f_D * N_sig), where R_bg is background correlation factor (nominally 1) and f_K are signal leakage fractions from MC. This is directly analogous to the PPG12 ABCD method.
- **Unfolding**: Bin-by-bin correction factors C_MC from Sherpa LO; cross-checked with iterative Bayesian unfolding
- **NLO predictions**: JETPHOX (parton-level, MMHT2014 PDF, BFG-II fragmentation functions, scales mu_R = mu_F = mu_f = ET^gamma) with parton-to-hadron corrections; NLO Sherpa (particle-level, NNPDF3.0 NNLO, gamma+1,2 jets at NLO, gamma+3,4 jets at LO, Frixione isolation)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Direct methodological analogue**: This is the closest LHC parallel to PPG12's isolated photon cross-section measurement. Both use the ABCD (tight/non-tight x isolated/non-isolated) background subtraction, both compare to NLO JETPHOX, and both measure differential cross sections as functions of photon ET.
- **JETPHOX validation at higher energy**: PPG12 compares its pp sqrt(s) = 200 GeV cross section to JETPHOX NLO predictions. This ATLAS paper validates JETPHOX at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, providing confidence that the theory framework works across a wide energy range. Any discrepancies seen in PPG12 vs JETPHOX at 200 GeV can be interpreted in the context of this LHC validation.
- **ET-dependent isolation**: ATLAS uses a parametric isolation cut E_T^iso < 4.2e-3 * ET + 10 GeV, similar in spirit to PPG12's parametric reco_iso_max = reco_iso_max_b + reco_iso_max_s * ET. This validates the approach of ET-dependent isolation rather than flat cuts.
- **Signal purity and leakage corrections**: The ATLAS ABCD formalism with signal leakage fractions (f_B, f_C, f_D) is the same mathematical structure as PPG12's c_B, c_C, c_D corrections. The >90% purity achieved at LHC energies provides a benchmark for the PPG12 analysis at lower energies where backgrounds are more challenging.
- **Fragmentation photon contribution**: ATLAS quantifies the direct vs fragmentation decomposition, which is also relevant to understanding the composition of PPG12's photon sample at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

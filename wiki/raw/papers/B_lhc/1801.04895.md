# Observation of medium induced modifications of jet fragmentation in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV using isolated-photon-tagged jets

## Abstract

First measurement of fragmentation functions for jets associated with an isolated photon in pp and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV using CMS. Fragmentation functions are obtained for jets with pT^jet > 30 GeV/c in events containing an isolated photon with pT^gamma > 60 GeV/c, using charged tracks with pT^trk > 1 GeV/c in a cone of R = 0.3 around the jet axis. In central PbPb collisions, modifications of the fragmentation functions are observed compared to pp: an excess of low-pT particles and depletion of high-pT particles, with a transition around 3 GeV/c. This is the first measurement of in-medium shower modifications of partons (quark-dominated) with well-defined initial kinematics.

## Key Results

- **Fragmentation function modification in central PbPb**: Clear excess of soft particles (large xi) and depletion of hard particles (small xi) in 0-10% central PbPb relative to pp, with crossover around xi ~ 2 (corresponding to pT^trk ~ 3 GeV/c for pT^gamma = 60 GeV/c)
- **Two fragmentation variables**:
  - xi_jet = ln(|p_jet|^2 / (p_trk . p_jet)) -- uses reconstructed jet momentum as reference
  - xi_gamma_T = ln(|pT^gamma|^2 / (pT^trk . pT^gamma)) -- uses photon pT as proxy for initial parton pT
- **xi_gamma_T shows larger modifications**: Because xi_jet is affected by jet quenching (lower reconstructed jet pT), the modifications appear diluted in xi_jet. The photon-based xi_gamma_T reveals the full magnitude of medium effects since the photon pT is unmodified.
- **Centrality dependence**: Modifications grow progressively from peripheral (50-100%, consistent with pp) to most central (0-10%); no significant differences observed in the 50% most peripheral collisions
- **Statistical significance**: Chi-squared tests give p-values of 10^-3 (xi_jet) and 10^-20 (xi_gamma_T) for PbPb vs pp differences in 0-10% centrality
- **Quark-jet dominated**: At LHC energies, photon+jet production is dominated by quark jets for pT^jet > 30 GeV/c, making this a measurement specifically of quark energy loss and shower modification
- **pp reference**: pp fragmentation functions measured at same sqrt(s), smeared to match PbPb jet energy resolution for fair comparison

## Methodology

- **Photon selection**: pT^gamma > 60 GeV/c, |eta^gamma| < 1.44 (ECAL barrel), tight shower shape cut (sigma_eta_eta), H/E < threshold, isolation requirements matching Ref. [29]
- **Jet reconstruction**: Anti-kT R = 0.3, pT^jet > 30 GeV/c, |eta^jet| < 1.6; UE subtracted; energy scale and resolution corrections from PYTHIA 8 + HYDJET embedding
- **Photon-jet pairing**: Delta_phi_jgamma > 7pi/8 (back-to-back requirement)
- **Tracks**: pT^trk > 1 GeV/c within Delta_R = 0.3 of jet axis; corrected for tracking efficiency, acceptance, and misreconstruction rate
- **Background subtraction (three components)**:
  1. UE tracks within jet cone -- estimated by correlating selected jets with tracks from minimum-bias events
  2. Fake/unrelated jets from UE fluctuations or different hard scatterings -- estimated by correlating photons with jets from minimum-bias events
  3. Residual UE density correction for difference between hard-scattering and MB events from simulation
- **Photon purity correction**: Template fit to shower shape distribution; purity ~65% in 0-10% PbPb, ~85% in 0-50% PbPb. Decay photon fragmentation estimated from wider shower shape sideband.
- **pp smearing**: pp distributions are smeared to match the jet energy resolution in each PbPb centrality bin for fair ratio comparison
- **Luminosity**: 404 ub^-1 PbPb, 27.4 pb^-1 pp

## Systematic Uncertainties

- **Jet energy scale**: Dominant for xi_jet (7.3% PbPb, 6.5% pp); smaller for xi_gamma_T (2.4%, 0.6%)
- **Tracking efficiency**: 5% (PbPb), 4% (pp), constant vs pT^trk and centrality
- **Background subtraction**: 3.6% for PbPb (combined from alternative eta-reflection method and UE density scaling)
- **Long-range eta correlations (flow)**: 4.1% (3.3%) for xi_jet (xi_gamma_T) in PbPb
- **Photon purity**: 1.2% (1.4%) for xi_jet (xi_gamma_T)
- **Jet fragmentation pattern dependence**: 11% for xi_jet < 1 and 4.3% (7.0%) for xi_jet (xi_gamma_T) > 2.5 in PbPb
- **Electron contamination**: 0.6% (0.5%) for xi_jet (xi_gamma_T)

## Relevance to PPG12

- **sPHENIX will make this measurement at RHIC energy**: The sPHENIX detector, with its full calorimetry and tracking, is designed to perform photon-tagged jet fragmentation measurements at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. PPG12's isolated photon cross section in pp is the essential first step -- it validates photon reconstruction, isolation, and purity extraction at sPHENIX, all of which are prerequisites for the fragmentation function measurement.
- **Quark-jet energy loss at lower sqrt(s)**: At RHIC energies, the quark/gluon composition of photon-tagged jets differs from LHC due to different parton distribution function sampling. PPG12 enables the study of quark-dominated energy loss in a complementary kinematic regime where the QGP temperature and lifetime are different.
- **Calibrated parton kinematics**: The key advantage of the photon-tagged channel is that the photon pT provides a calibrated estimate of the initial parton pT before energy loss. This CMS paper demonstrates the power of this approach (xi_gamma_T vs xi_jet), and sPHENIX will exploit the same technique. PPG12 establishes the photon pT spectrum needed for this calibration at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- **Medium response and soft particle production**: The observed excess of soft particles in central PbPb could arise from parton energy loss or medium response (recoil). sPHENIX measurements at RHIC energy, with the PPG12 baseline, will test whether the same pattern persists at lower collision energies where the medium properties differ.
- **Background methodology**: The event-mixing technique for UE subtraction and the photon purity correction via shower-shape template fits are methodologically similar to PPG12's approach (ABCD background subtraction, shower shape BDT), providing validation of the techniques at higher energy.

# Comparison of inclusive and photon-tagged jet suppression in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions with ATLAS

## Abstract

Parton energy loss in the QGP is studied via photon-tagged jet production in 1.7 nb^-1 of Pb+Pb and 260 pb^-1 of pp data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV with ATLAS. Events contain an isolated photon with pT > 50 GeV and jets with pT > 50 GeV at Delta_phi > 7pi/8. The nuclear modification factor R_AA and fractional energy loss S_loss are reported for photon-tagged jets and compared to inclusive jets. Photon-tagged jets (75-80% quark-initiated at 50 GeV) show significantly less suppression than inclusive jets (30-40% quark-initiated), demonstrating sensitivity to the colour charge of the initiating parton.

## Key Results

- **R_AA for photon-tagged jets**: 0.60-0.75 in 0-10% central Pb+Pb, significantly higher than inclusive jet R_AA (~0.5) at the same pT, for pT^jet < 200 GeV
- **Quark-initiated fractions**: Photon-tagged jets are 75-80% quark-initiated at pT = 50 GeV (vs 30-40% for inclusive jets), converging near 50-60% for both at pT = 300 GeV; confirmed consistently across PYTHIA 8, Herwig 7.2, and Sherpa 2.2.4
- **Fractional energy loss S_loss**: For photon-tagged jets, Delta_pT ranges from 10-30 GeV and S_loss from 0.07-0.10 in 0-10% centrality. Inclusive jets show larger S_loss (~0.10-0.14) at low pT, consistent with larger gluon-initiated fraction losing more energy
- **S_loss convergence**: At pT > 200 GeV, where quark fractions become similar, S_loss values for photon-tagged and inclusive jets converge within uncertainties
- **Centrality dependence**: More suppression (lower R_AA) in more central collisions; 30-80% shows R_AA closer to unity
- **Isospin and nPDF effects**: Isospin reduces photon-tagged jet cross section by ~5% (larger effect than for inclusive jets due to quark composition sensitivity); nPDF effects are modest (~2-3%)

## Methodology

- **Photon selection**: pT^gamma > 50 GeV, |eta^gamma| < 2.37, tight shower shape, isolation energy < 3 GeV (pp) or centrality-dependent cut at 90% efficiency (Pb+Pb)
- **Jet reconstruction**: Anti-kT R = 0.4, |eta^jet| < 2.8, pT^jet > 50 GeV; UE subtracted event-by-event; JES calibrated via in situ gamma+jet, Z+jet, and multijet balance in pp
- **Photon-jet pairing**: Delta_phi(gamma,jet) > 7pi/8; all qualifying jets per event included
- **Background subtraction**: (1) Combinatoric jet background via event mixing matched in centrality and flow-plane angle; (2) Decay photon background via ABCD-like double sideband (identification x isolation), scaled by purity
- **Unfolding**: 2D iterative Bayesian (RooUnfold) on (pT^gamma, pT^jet) with Pythia 8 response matrices; 2-3 iterations depending on centrality
- **S_loss extraction**: Extended power-law fit to pp and Pb+Pb spectra; iterative Jacobian correction; conceptually similar to PHENIX pseudo-quantile approach
- **pp cross-section comparison**: Pythia 8, Sherpa 2.2.4, Herwig 7.2 all compared; none perfectly describe data, highlighting need for proper pp baseline

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Colour-charge dependent energy loss at RHIC**: This paper provides the strongest LHC evidence that jet quenching depends on the initiating parton's colour charge. At sPHENIX sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the quark/gluon mixture in both photon-tagged and inclusive jet samples is different from LHC (lower energy means more quark-dominated processes). PPG12's pp photon cross section is the essential baseline for extracting R_AA at RHIC and testing whether the colour-charge dependence persists at lower energies and temperatures.
- **S_loss methodology**: The fractional energy loss S_loss, originally developed at RHIC by PHENIX, is applied here at LHC energies. sPHENIX will close the loop by applying the same analysis to photon-tagged jets at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, with PPG12 providing the denominator.
- **Spectrum shape matters**: The paper explicitly demonstrates that R_AA depends on the steepness of the pp spectrum. Correct measurement of the pp photon cross section (PPG12) is therefore essential -- even small biases in the reference spectrum propagate into R_AA.
- **pp baseline validation**: ATLAS found that none of the MC generators perfectly describe the photon-tagged jet pp cross section (especially the fragmentation photon contribution). This underscores the need for data-driven pp baselines like PPG12 rather than relying on MC alone.

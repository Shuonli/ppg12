# Direct-Photon Production in p+p Collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV at Midrapidity

## Abstract

The differential cross section for the production of direct photons in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV at midrapidity was measured in the PHENIX detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Inclusive-direct photons were measured in the transverse momentum range from 5.5 to 25 GeV/c, extending the range beyond previous measurements. Event structure was studied with an isolation criterion. Next-to-leading-order perturbative-quantum-chromodynamics calculations give a good description of the spectrum. When the cross section is expressed versus xT, the PHENIX data are seen to be in agreement with measurements from other experiments at different center-of-mass energies.

## Key Results

- Inclusive-direct photon cross section measured over 5.25 < pT < 25 GeV/c at midrapidity |eta| < 0.25, extending the previous PHENIX measurement (hep-ex/0502006) from 15 to 25 GeV/c
- Power-law fit to data for 8 < pT < 25 GeV/c yields n = 7.08 +/- 0.09(stat) +/- 0.1(syst) with chi2/NDF = 8.3/10
- NLO pQCD calculations (Vogelsang, CTEQ6M PDF, BFGII FF) at scales mu = pT/2, pT, 2pT agree well with the data
- Ratio of isolated to inclusive direct photons is >90% for pT > 10 GeV/c, consistent with NLO pQCD predictions; isolation cut reduces hadronic decay background by ~60%
- xT scaling analysis (neff = 4.5) shows consistency across sqrt(s) from 19.4 GeV to 7 TeV, confirming pQCD subprocess picture
- Tabulated cross section values from 5.25 to 25 GeV/c (18 data points) in Appendix Table II
- gamma_dir / pi0 ratio increases with pT, confirming growing direct photon signal purity at high pT

## Methodology

### Photon identification
- EMCal (PbSc + PbGl) in two arms, each covering pi/2 in azimuth and |eta| < 0.35
- Tower granularity: delta_eta x delta_phi = 0.011 x 0.011 (PbSc), 0.008 x 0.008 (PbGl)
- Energy resolution: sigma_E/E = 8.1%/sqrt(E) + 5.7%; energy scale systematic < 1.5%
- Fiducial cuts: |eta_f| < 0.25, delta_phi_f = pi/2 - 0.2 per arm
- Shower shape cut efficiency for photons: 0.98; charged-track veto applied
- ToF cut: +/-5 ns from expected arrival time (cosmic ray reduction)

### Background subtraction (statistical pi0-tagging method)
- N_dir = N_incl - (1+A)(1+R) N_pi0
  - N_pi0: pi0-tagged photons via invariant mass 105-165 MeV (3 sigma window), partner E_min = 0.5 GeV
  - (1+R): correction for untagged pi0 photons (missing partner due to E_min or dead areas), from single-particle MC
  - A: ratio of non-pi0 decay photons to pi0 photons (eta, omega, eta'); A -> 0.235 at high pT
- pi0 photon merging handled: two photons resolved up to pi0 pT of 12 (17) GeV/c in PbSc (PbGl); 50% merging at 17 (25) GeV/c; shower shape rejects >90% of merged clusters
- Systematic uncertainties amplified by factor W = N_incl/N_dir at low pT where background is large

### Isolation cut
- Cone radius r = sqrt(delta_eta^2 + delta_phi^2) = 0.5
- Energy in cone < 10% of photon energy
- Cone energy = EMCal clusters (E > 0.15 GeV) + charged tracks (0.2 < p < 15 GeV/c)
- Isolated direct photon yield: N_iso_dir = N_iso_incl - (n_iso_pi0 + N_iso_pi0 * R) - A_iso * (1+R) * N_iso_pi0

### Cross section calculation
- E d3sigma/dp3 = (1/L) * (1/2pi pT) * (N_dir / delta_pT delta_y) * (1/epsilon) * (1/epsilon_bias)
- Integrated luminosity: ~8.0 pb^-1 (2006 RHIC run, EMCal trigger with BBC coincidence)
- BBC trigger cross section: 23.0 mb (55% of inelastic), measured with 9.7% uncertainty via Vernier scan
- BBC high-pT bias correction: epsilon_bias = 0.78 +/- 0.02, independent of pT

- Fig 10: Inclusive direct photon cross section vs pT compared to NLO pQCD (mu = pT/2, pT, 2pT). Data agree well with theory; scale variation spans the data. Bottom panel shows (Data-Theory)/Theory consistent with zero within uncertainties.

## Systematic Uncertainties

- Energy scale (1.5%): propagates to 10-15% cross section uncertainty depending on pT
- Luminosity: 9.7% normalization uncertainty
- pi0 tagging (1+R correction): dominant at low pT due to E_min sensitivity
- Merging correction: dominant component at high pT but suppressed by (W-1) << 1 factor
- Hadronic decay ratio A: ~5% uncertainty
- Full uncertainty tables in Appendix (Tables VI, VII)

## Relevance to PPG12

- This is the direct predecessor measurement for PPG12's isolated photon cross section at the same sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. PPG12 uses sPHENIX's full-azimuth, larger-eta EMCal (|eta| < 0.7 vs PHENIX's |eta| < 0.25) with dramatically improved statistics (16.6 pb^-1 Run 24 vs 8 pb^-1 Run 6).
- PPG12 extends the pT range (8-35 GeV/c) and employs BDT-based shower shape discrimination instead of simple shower shape cuts, and the ABCD background subtraction method instead of the pi0-tagging statistical subtraction.
- The isolation criterion in PPG12 (cone R=0.3, parametric ET-dependent cut) is tighter than the PHENIX cone R=0.5 with 10% threshold. PPG12's ET-dependent isolation is designed to handle underlying event more robustly.
- NLO pQCD comparison framework (Vogelsang calculations, CTEQ PDFs, scale variations) is directly inherited by PPG12, which also compares to JETPHOX NLO calculations.
- The xT scaling analysis and isolated/inclusive ratio provide key validation benchmarks for PPG12 results.
- Cross section values in Table II serve as direct comparison data for the PPG12 measurement.

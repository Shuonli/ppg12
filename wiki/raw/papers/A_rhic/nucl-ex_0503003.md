## Abstract

First measurement of direct photon production in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The direct photon signal is extracted statistically as a function of centrality and compared to NLO pQCD calculations. The direct photon yield scales with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (Ncoll) for all centralities, in sharp contrast to the strong suppression observed for pi0.

## Key Results

- **Direct photon spectra measured** up to pT ~ 13 GeV/c in nine centrality bins plus minimum bias, using 30 x 10^6 minimum bias events plus Level-2 trigger sample equivalent to 55 x 10^6 additional MB events (RHIC Run 2, 2001).
- **Ncoll scaling confirmed**: R_AA(pT > 6 GeV/c) for direct photons is consistent with unity at all centralities, meaning the yield is consistent with Ncoll-scaled NLO pQCD predictions.
- **Contrast with pi0 suppression**: While pi0 R_AA falls to ~0.2-0.3 in central collisions, direct photon R_AA remains ~1, demonstrating that initial-state parton distribution modifications are minor.
- **NLO pQCD comparison**: Calculations with mu = pT, CTEQ6 PDFs, and GRV fragmentation functions (Gordon & Vogelsang) agree well with the data. Fragmentation contribution is ~50% at 3.5 GeV/c and ~35% at 10 GeV/c.
- **Double ratio technique**: Rgamma = (gamma/pi0)_measured / (gamma/pi0)_background, where deviation above unity signals direct photons. Energy scale uncertainties partially cancel in this ratio.
- **Systematic uncertainties**: pi0 yield ~15-20%, inclusive gamma yield ~16-19%, gamma/pi0 ratio ~10-14%, plus 4% background calculation uncertainty.
- **Energy calibration accuracy**: better than 1.5%, verified by pi0 mass position, minimum ionizing particles, and RICH-identified electron E/p matching.
- **Centrality increases signal significance** because pi0 suppression reduces the decay photon background, making the direct photon signal relatively larger.
- **No evidence for significant thermal photon excess** above Ncoll-scaled pQCD at pT > 4 GeV/c, contrasting with SPS Pb+Pb results where the yield exceeded Ncoll-scaled pp by a factor ~2.

## Methodology

1. **Detector**: PHENIX EMCal (PbSc + PbGl), |eta| < 0.35, ~22.5 deg azimuthal sectors, fine segmentation 0.01 x 0.01 in phi x eta. BBC + ZDC for centrality.
2. **Photon identification**: EMCal cluster PID via time-of-flight and shower profile. Charged contaminants subtracted using PC3 track matching (~4% above 3 GeV/c). Neutral hadron contamination (antineutrons) < 1% above 5 GeV/c from GEANT simulation.
3. **pi0/eta measurement**: Invariant mass analysis of photon pairs; combinatorial background from mixed events.
4. **Direct photon extraction**: Statistical subtraction via double ratio. Background photon cocktail based on measured pi0 and eta spectra with mT-scaling for other mesons (eta', Ks0, omega). eta/pi0 ratio = 0.45 +/- 0.05 at high pT.
5. **Efficiency corrections**: Embedding simulated single particles (gamma, pi0, eta) from full PHENIX GEANT into real events. Overall pi0 correction ~2.5 with < 25% centrality dependence. Energy smearing: 5% constant term for PbSc, 7% for PbGl.
6. **Centrality**: Glauber Monte Carlo with BBC/ZDC correlation. Nine bins from 0-10% to 80-92%.
7. **NLO pQCD**: Gordon & Vogelsang calculation with mu_R = mu_F = pT, CTEQ6 PDFs, GRV fragmentation functions. Scale variation from pT/2 to 2pT provides theory uncertainty band.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Analysis framework**: The statistical subtraction method via double ratio Rgamma is the conceptual ancestor of the ABCD background subtraction used in PPG12. Both rely on comparing measured gamma/pi0 to expected background ratios.
- **NLO pQCD baseline**: Same Gordon & Vogelsang NLO calculation is compared against in PPG12 pp measurement (with JETPHOX as the modern evolution). CTEQ6 PDFs are a predecessor to CTEQ-TEA sets used in PPG12 theory comparisons.
- **Ncoll scaling as baseline**: Establishes the principle that direct photon production in the absence of nuclear modifications scales with Ncoll, which is the reference for identifying thermal/medium-enhanced photon production in heavy-ion data.
- **Fragmentation photon contribution**: The 35-50% fragmentation fraction at these pT values is relevant to understanding isolation cut efficiency in PPG12, since fragmentation photons are surrounded by hadronic activity.
- **EMCal shower shape and PID**: PHENIX PbSc/PbGl shower shape analysis is a precursor to sPHENIX CEMC cluster shape variables used for BDT photon identification.
- **Systematic uncertainty methodology**: Treatment of energy scale, yield extraction, and yield correction uncertainties in independent PbSc/PbGl subsystems informs the systematic framework in PPG12.

# Measurement of Direct-Photon Cross Section and Double-Helicity Asymmetry at sqrt(s) = 510 GeV in polarized p+p Collisions

## Abstract

Measurements of the cross section and double-helicity asymmetry ALL of direct-photon production in polarized p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 510 GeV are presented. The measurements have been performed at midrapidity (|eta| < 0.25) with the PHENIX detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. At relativistic energies, direct photons are dominantly produced from the initial quark-gluon hard scattering and do not interact via the strong force at leading order. Therefore, at sqrt(s) = 510 GeV, where leading-order effects dominate, these measurements provide clean and direct access to the gluon helicity in the polarized proton in the gluon-momentum-fraction range 0.02 < x < 0.08, with direct sensitivity to the sign of the gluon contribution.

## Key Results

### Cross section
- Inclusive direct photon cross section measured at midrapidity |eta| < 0.25 for 6 < pT < 30 GeV/c
- Isolated direct photon cross section measured with isolation cone R = 0.5, E_cone < 0.1*E_gamma
- NLO pQCD (Vogelsang, NNPDF3.0 PDF, GRV FF) agrees well with isolated direct photon data within uncertainties
- NLO pQCD underestimates the inclusive direct photon cross section by up to a factor of ~3 at pT < 12 GeV/c, attributed to multiparton interactions and parton showers not captured in fixed-order NLO
- For pT > 12 GeV/c, inclusive NLO pQCD is consistent with data within uncertainties
- Cross section analysis uses 11 pb^-1 integrated luminosity with |z-vertex| < 10 cm; asymmetry uses 108 pb^-1 with |z-vertex| < 30 cm
- Pileup correction: r_pileup ~ 0.8 (inclusive), ~0.9 (isolated), obtained by logarithmic extrapolation to zero event rate

### Double-helicity asymmetry (ALL)
- First measurement of ALL for direct photon production (the "golden channel" for gluon spin proposed in 1992 RHIC-spin proposal)
- ALL measured for isolated direct photons at 6 < pT < 20 GeV/c, corresponding to gluon momentum fraction 0.02 < x < 0.08
- DSSV14 polarized PDF calculation with NNPDF3.0 agrees well with data (chi2 = 4.7 for 7 data points)
- JAM22 negative gluon polarization scenario (delta_g < 0) disfavored at > 2.8 sigma level (chi2 = 12.6 vs 4.7 for delta_g > 0)
- Resolves a fundamental ambiguity between positive and negative gluon-spin solutions that previous pi0 and jet data could not distinguish due to mixed qg and gg contributions
- Average beam polarizations: P_B = 0.55, P_Y = 0.57 (2013 run)
- Relative luminosity systematic uncertainty on ALL: 3.8e-4
- Polarization scale uncertainty: 6.6%

## Methodology

### Direct photon extraction
- Same pi0-tagging method as previous PHENIX measurements at 200 GeV
- Inclusive: gamma_inc_dir = gamma_inc_total - (1 + R_miss_pi0 + delta_gamma_h/pi0) * gamma_inc_pi0
- Isolated: gamma_iso_dir = gamma_iso_total - gamma_iso_pi0 - (R_miss_pi0 + V*delta_gamma_h/pi0) * gamma_isopair_pi0
  - V = gamma_iso_eta / gamma_inc_eta (ranges 0.01-0.1 depending on pT)
- Pi0 tagging window: 110 < M_gammagamma < 160 MeV/c^2 (M_pi0 +/- 3sigma), partner E > 300 MeV
- Combinatorial background overestimation: ~8%, corrected via fit to background under pi0 peak
- delta_gamma_h/pi0 ~ 0.28 (eta: 0.21, omega + eta': 0.035 each); variation < 10% between 200 and 510 GeV

### Isolation criterion
- Cone radius r_cone = sqrt(delta_eta^2 + delta_phi^2) = 0.5
- E_cone < 0.1 * E_gamma
- Neutral particle threshold: 300 MeV (EMCal); charged particle threshold: 200 MeV/c (drift chambers)
- Isolation efficiency corrected using PYTHIA-simulated direct photon events with same criteria

### Detector
- EMCal: PbSc (6 sectors, 4 west + 2 east) + PbGl (2 sectors east); 5 m from beam
- Tower granularity: delta_eta x delta_phi ~ 0.011 x 0.011 (PbSc), 0.008 x 0.008 (PbGl)
- Pi0 photon pairs resolved to 12 (16) GeV/c in PbSc (PbGl); shower profile extends gamma/pi0 discrimination to 30 GeV/c
- EMCal trigger: minimum 3.7 GeV in 4x4 tower tile, coincident with BBC minimum-bias trigger
- ToF cut: |ToF| < 10 ns (reduces pileup from high collision rate, 0.04-0.17 BBC events/crossing)
- Fiducial region: 10 (12) towers from edge for PbSc (PbGl), i.e., ~0.1 rad

### ALL extraction
- ALL = (1/PB*PY) * (N++ - R*N+-) / (N++ + R*N+-)
- R = L++/L+- (relative luminosity from BBC)
- Background correction: A_dir_LL = (A_total_LL - r_pi0*A_pi0_LL - r_h*A_eta_LL) / (1 - r_pi0 - r_h)
  - r_pi0 = 10-14%, r_h = 0.6-1.4% (residual background fractions after isolation)
  - A_pi0_LL from sideband regions (47-97 and 177-227 MeV/c^2)
  - A_eta_LL from previous 200 GeV measurement with xT scaling assumption
- Systematic checks: bunch shuffling technique, subgroup analysis by bunch spin pattern

### Systematic uncertainties
- Energy scale + nonlinearity: 14-19% (inclusive), 7-13% (isolated) on cross section
- Pi0 yield extraction: 2-12% (inclusive), 0.5-2.5% (isolated)
- Other hadron decay fractions: 5-14% (inclusive), 0.4-6% (isolated)
- Photon conversion: 12.8 +/- 1.9% in west arm (vertex tracker material); 3 +/- 1% PbSc, 4.5 +/- 1.3% PbGl (other material)
- EMCal resolution: 2-8%; trigger: 2-4.5%
- Luminosity: 10% absolute normalization
- ALL systematics: background estimation, false asymmetries from pileup (low pT), relative luminosity (3.8e-4), polarization scale (6.6%)

## Key Figures

- Fig 1(a,b): Inclusive direct photon cross section vs pT at 510 GeV compared to NLO pQCD. Clear excess of data over theory at pT < 12 GeV/c (up to factor ~3), attributed to multiparton interactions. NLO pQCD agrees for pT > 12 GeV/c.
- Fig 1(c,d): Isolated direct photon cross section vs pT at 510 GeV compared to NLO pQCD. Good agreement with theory across the full pT range, with slight overestimation at lowest pT.
- Fig 2: ALL vs pT for isolated direct photons at 510 GeV. Data points consistent with DSSV14 (positive gluon polarization). JAM22 delta_g < 0 curves clearly disfavored (> 2.8 sigma) -- the first direct experimental discrimination of the sign of gluon polarization.

## Relevance to PPG12

- This paper demonstrates the maturity of the PHENIX direct photon framework at higher sqrt(s) and validates that NLO pQCD describes the isolated photon cross section well. PPG12 at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV expects similarly good NLO agreement, based on both this measurement and the earlier 200 GeV results.
- The observation that NLO pQCD underestimates the inclusive (non-isolated) direct photon cross section at low pT by a factor of ~3 at 510 GeV motivates the use of isolation as a primary analysis tool in PPG12. The isolation cut effectively removes the soft/multiparton contributions that NLO cannot describe.
- The isolation cone definition (R = 0.5, E_cone/E_gamma < 10%) is larger than PPG12's cone (R = 0.3 with parametric ET-dependent threshold). The different cone sizes reflect different detector geometries (PHENIX |eta| < 0.35 limits cone size vs sPHENIX |eta| < 0.7).
- The pileup correction procedure (logarithmic extrapolation to zero rate, r_pileup factors) is analogous to PPG12's double-interaction correction via MBD pileup metrics.
- The pi0-tagging method (Eq. 1-2) is the same framework that PPG12's ABCD method replaces. The background fractions (r_pi0 = 10-14% after isolation) provide a benchmark for the expected signal purity in PPG12's tight+isolated (A) region.
- The vertex tracker material causing 12.8% photon conversion is not present in sPHENIX, but the general treatment of material budget and photon conversion losses is relevant to PPG12's efficiency corrections.

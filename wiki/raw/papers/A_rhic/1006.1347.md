# High pT Direct Photon and pi0 Triggered Azimuthal Jet Correlations and Measurement of kT for Isolated Direct Photons in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

Correlations of charged hadrons of 1 < pT < 10 GeV/c with high pT direct photons and pi0 mesons in the range 5 < pT < 15 GeV/c are used to study jet fragmentation in the gamma+jet and dijet channels, respectively. The magnitude of the partonic transverse momentum, kT, is obtained by comparing to a model incorporating a Gaussian kT smearing. The sensitivity of the associated charged hadron spectra to the underlying fragmentation function is tested and the data are compared to calculations using recent global fit results. The shape of the direct photon-associated hadron spectrum as well as its charge asymmetry are found to be consistent with a sample dominated by quark-gluon Compton scattering. No significant evidence of fragmentation photon correlated production is observed within experimental uncertainties.

## Key Results

- Isolated direct photon-hadron and pi0-hadron azimuthal correlations measured for trigger 5 < pT < 15 GeV/c and associated 1 < pT < 10 GeV/c
- Near-side yield for direct photon triggers is consistent with zero (< 15% of pi0 near-side yield for pT > 7 GeV/c), confirming negligible fragmentation photon contamination
- xE distributions for isolated direct photon triggers are significantly steeper than for pi0 triggers, consistent with probing the quark fragmentation function directly
- Combined xE fit (exponential: e^{-bxE}) yields b = 8.2 +/- 0.3, in excellent agreement with the quark fragmentation function (b = 8.2 for 0.2 < z < 1.0) and inconsistent with gluon fragmentation (b = 11.4)
- kT measurement: sqrt(<|kT|^2>) ~ 3 GeV/c from isolated direct photon triggers; pi0 triggers give somewhat larger values depending on fragmentation function choice (KKP, AKK, DSS)
- R_gamma values (ratio inclusive/decay photons): 1.19+/-0.06 (5-7 GeV/c), 1.33+/-0.05 (7-9), 1.54+/-0.05 (9-12), 1.80+/-0.11 (12-15 GeV/c)
- After tagging + isolation: R_iso_gamma increases to 1.38, 1.92, 2.87, 4.02 for the same pT bins, demonstrating effective purity enhancement
- Charge asymmetry of away-side hadrons consistent with quark-gluon Compton scattering dominance

## Methodology

### Data and detector
- 533 million photon-triggered events from 2005 (3.8 pb^-1) and 2006 (10.7 pb^-1) RHIC p+p runs
- PHENIX EMCal: PbSc (6 sectors) + PbGl (2 sectors); resolution sigma_E/E = 2.1% + 8.1%/sqrt(E) (PbSc), 0.8% + 5.9%/sqrt(E) (PbGl)
- Photon purity > 98% for E > 5 GeV using shower shape and charged-particle veto
- Charged hadrons: drift chamber + pad chambers, delta_p/p = 0.7% + 1.0%*p

### Three-step direct photon extraction
1. **Statistical subtraction** (Eq. 1): Y_direct = (1/(R_gamma - 1)) * (R_gamma * Y_inclusive - Y_decay)
   - R_gamma determined from the direct photon cross section measurement (1205.5533/hep-ex_0502006)
   - Y_decay estimated from measured pi0 and eta per-trigger yields weighted by decay probability functions P(pT_gamma, pT_meson)
   - delta_gamma(pi) = 1.24 +/- 0.05 (ratio total decay photons to pi0 decay photons)

2. **Decay photon tagging**: event-by-event identification of decay photons via invariant mass pairing
   - pi0 window: 108-165 MeV/c^2; eta window: 500-600 MeV/c^2; partner pT > 500 MeV/c
   - Tagging efficiency epsilon_tag: 0.43 (5-7 GeV/c) to 0.53 (12-15 GeV/c)
   - Effective R_gamma after tagging (R_miss_gamma) increases significantly

3. **Isolation cut**: sum of momenta (charged tracks + EMCal clusters) in cone R=0.3 < 10% of photon energy
   - Fiducial cuts: photons required ~0.1 rad from detector edge in both eta and phi
   - Isolation efficiency for decay photon removal (epsilon_niso): 0.40-0.48 depending on pT
   - Final R_iso_gamma after tagging + isolation: 1.38 to 4.02

### kT extraction (LO + Gaussian kT smearing model)
- Born-level pQCD 2->2 cross sections + Gaussian kT smearing with width sqrt(2)|kT|
- Fragmentation functions tested: KKP, AKK, DSS
- jT (parton shower transverse momentum) fixed at sqrt(<|jT|^2>) = 635 MeV/c from pi0-hadron data
- pout = |p_assoc| * sin(delta_phi) used to extract sqrt(<|pout|^2>)
- sqrt(<|pout|^2>) fitted from azimuthal correlation width using Eq. 16-17

### Kinematic observables
- xE = -p_trig . p_assoc / |p_trig|^2 (approximates fragmentation variable z for direct photon triggers)
- pout = |p_assoc| * sin(delta_phi) (sensitive to kT perpendicular to jet axis)
- Modified Hagedorn function fits: dN/dxE ~ N(n-1)(1/xhat_h) * 1/(1 + xE/xhat_h)^n

## Relevance to PPG12

- This paper establishes the isolation cut methodology at PHENIX (cone R=0.3, 10% energy threshold) that PPG12 directly inherits in concept, though PPG12 uses a parametric ET-dependent isolation cut (reco_iso_max = b + s*ET).
- The R_gamma values and decay photon tagging efficiencies demonstrate the challenge of extracting direct photons from the large hadronic decay background. PPG12's BDT-based approach (tight/non-tight classification) replaces the pi0-tagging method, offering improved rejection power and simpler systematic treatment.
- The confirmation that the isolated photon sample is dominated by quark-gluon Compton scattering (from xE slope and charge asymmetry) validates the physics picture underlying PPG12's isolated photon cross section measurement.
- The negligible near-side correlation for direct photon triggers (< 15% of pi0) validates that isolation effectively removes fragmentation photons -- a key assumption in PPG12's ABCD method where the isolated+tight region (A) is assumed to be signal-dominated.
- The cone size R=0.3 used in isolation matches PPG12's cone_size parameter (encoded as integer 3 in config YAML for R=0.3).
- The kT measurement (~3 GeV/c) quantifies the intrinsic acoplanarity that affects jet-photon momentum balance, relevant for understanding the isolation cone energy distribution in PPG12.

## Abstract

Measurement of e+e- pair production for m_ee < 0.3 GeV/c^2 and 1 < pT < 5 GeV/c in p+p and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. Enhanced yield above hadronic sources is observed. Treating the excess as photon internal conversions, the invariant yield of direct photons is deduced. In central Au+Au collisions, the excess above the Ncoll-scaled p+p contribution is exponential with inverse slope T = 221 +/- 19(stat) +/- 19(syst) MeV. Hydrodynamical models with T_init ~ 300-600 MeV at tau_0 ~ 0.6-0.15 fm/c are in qualitative agreement.

## Key Results

- **Thermal photon signal discovered**: Direct photon yield in central Au+Au exceeds Ncoll-scaled pp for pT < 2.5 GeV/c. This is the first evidence for thermal photon radiation from QGP at RHIC.
- **Inverse slope in central Au+Au**: T = 221 +/- 19(stat) +/- 19(syst) MeV for 0-20% centrality. Using power-law pp fit: T = 240 +/- 21 MeV.
- **Centrality dependence**: 0-20% central: T = 221 +/- 19 +/- 19 MeV; 20-40%: T = 217 +/- 18 +/- 16 MeV; Min. Bias: T = 233 +/- 14 +/- 19 MeV.
- **Integrated yield** (pT > 1 GeV/c): dN/dy = 1.50 +/- 0.23 +/- 0.35 for 0-20% centrality.
- **p+p direct photon fraction** r is consistent with NLO pQCD (Gordon & Vogelsang, mu = pT). Au+Au fraction exceeds pQCD expectation for pT < 3.5 GeV/c.
- **Internal conversion method**: Signal-to-background ratio improves by factor 5 above m_pi0 = 135 MeV, enabling detection of a 10% direct photon signal as a 50% excess in e+e- pairs.
- **Systematic uncertainty dominated** by eta/pi0 ratio: 0.48 +/- 0.03 (pp), 0.48 +/- 0.08 (Au+Au), giving ~7% (pp) and ~17% (Au+Au) cocktail uncertainty for 0.1 < m_ee < 0.3 GeV/c^2.
- **T_init implications**: Hydrodynamical models require T_init = 300-600 MeV at tau_0 = 0.6-0.15 fm/c to reproduce central data. Lattice QCD predicts QGP transition at ~170 MeV, so T_init well above T_c.

## Methodology

1. **Internal conversion technique**: Exploit the relation between real photon production and low-mass e+e- pair production via virtual photons (Eq. 1). The process-dependent factor S -> 1 as m_ee -> 0 or m_ee << pT. For pion Dalitz decays, S includes the form factor |F(m_ee^2)|^2 and a kinematic cutoff at M_hadron.
2. **Detector**: PHENIX central arms, |eta| < 0.35, pi/2 azimuthal coverage. 2004 Au+Au: 8 x 10^8 MB events; 2005 pp: 43 nb^-1 MB + 2.25 pb^-1 single electron trigger.
3. **Electron identification**: pT > 0.2 GeV/c. Pairs formed; conversion pairs removed by orientation cut. Combinatorial background from mixed events. Correlated background (~10% of signal in pp) from like-sign pair data.
4. **Hadronic cocktail**: Monte Carlo hadron decay generator based on PHENIX meson measurements. pi0, eta, omega, eta', phi contributions. Open charm from PYTHIA (negligible in this kinematic range).
5. **Two-component fit**: f(m_ee) = (1-r) f_c(m_ee) + r f_dir(m_ee), fitted for m_low < m_ee < 0.3 GeV/c^2 with m_low = 0.08, 0.1, 0.12 GeV/c^2. r is the direct photon fraction.
6. **Form factor assumption**: F(m_ee^2) = 1 for direct photons (point-like). Setting F to eta form factor would decrease r by ~10%.
7. **Conversion to real photon yield**: dN_dir(pT) = r x dN_incl(pT), where inclusive photon yield from e+e- pairs with m_ee < 0.03 GeV/c^2.
8. **pp fit**: Modified power law A_pp(1 + pT^2/b)^{-n} describes pp data well. Au+Au fitted as exponential + TAA-scaled pp fit.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Thermal photon baseline**: At intermediate pT (3-8 GeV/c), the PPG12 isolated photon cross section must account for the possibility of thermal photon contributions, although the thermal signal is exponentially suppressed above ~3-4 GeV/c and PPG12 operates at higher pT (8-35 GeV/c).
- **pp direct photon spectrum**: The pp data and NLO pQCD comparison in this paper establish the prompt photon baseline that PPG12 measures with improved precision using isolation cuts and BDT-based photon identification.
- **Hadronic cocktail methodology**: The EXODUS hadron decay generator and eta/pi0 ratio treatment inform how PPG12 handles background estimation, particularly the ABCD method's dependence on accurate modeling of hadronic backgrounds.
- **NLO pQCD validation**: Confirms that NLO pQCD (JETPHOX predecessor) correctly predicts the pp direct photon cross section at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, providing confidence in the theory calculations PPG12 compares against.
- **Form factor and systematic approach**: Treatment of systematic uncertainties from meson ratios and cocktail composition is relevant to PPG12's systematic uncertainty budget from purity and efficiency corrections.

## Abstract

PHENIX measures the direct photon spectrum from Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using external photon conversions at the silicon vertex detector (VTX) for 0%-93% centrality and 0.8-10 GeV/c in pT. An excess above Ncoll-scaled p+p (nonprompt direct photons) is observed for pT < 6 GeV/c. The nonprompt spectrum has an increasing inverse slope from ~0.2 to 0.4 GeV/c with increasing pT, suggesting sensitivity to earlier (hotter) stages of the collision at higher pT. The integrated nonprompt yield scales as (dNch/deta)^alpha with alpha ~ 1.1.

## Key Results

- **10x statistics improvement** over previous PHENIX measurements: 1.25 x 10^10 events from 2014 Au+Au data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (total recorded: 19 billion MB trigger events).
- **Continuous pT coverage**: 0.8-10 GeV/c in a single measurement technique, bridging the gap between previous low-pT conversion measurements and high-pT calorimetric results.
- **Fine centrality binning**: Nine 10% bins (0-10%, 10-20%, ..., 80-93%) plus four broader bins.
- **Rgamma**: Significant excess above unity for all centralities except 80-93% (consistent with pp). Below ~3 GeV/c, Rgamma is rather constant; above 3 GeV/c it increases with pT (pronounced in central collisions due to pi0 suppression reducing the background).
- **pT-dependent inverse slope** (key new result):
  - Low-pT range (0.8-1.9 GeV/c): Teff ~ 0.260 +/- 0.011 GeV/c (average, centrality-independent)
  - Mid-pT range (2.0-4.0 GeV/c): Teff ~ 0.376 +/- 0.037 GeV/c (significantly steeper)
  - Ratio of data to fixed-slope exponential (Teff = 0.260 GeV/c) rises monotonically above pT = 2 GeV/c, confirming the changing slope.
- **Inverse slope vs centrality** (0-20%): Teff = 0.277 +/- 0.017 +0.036/-0.014 GeV/c (low pT), 0.428 +/- 0.031 +0.031/-0.030 GeV/c (mid pT). No strong centrality dependence within uncertainties.
- **Integrated yield scaling**: dNgamma/dy scales with (dNch/deta)^alpha. For 1-5 GeV/c integration: alpha = 1.11 +/- 0.02(stat) +0.09/-0.08(syst). For nonprompt component: alpha consistent with ~1.1 across all pT slices.
- **Consistency with previous measurements**: Rgamma agrees with earlier PHENIX results from HBD conversions, internal conversions, and EMCal, which have independent systematics.
- **VTX conversion reconstruction**: New algorithm exploits the constraint that e+e- momenta are initially parallel and radially outward. Conversion radius resolution ~2 cm, azimuthal resolution ~4 mrad. Artificial mass proportional to conversion radius (VTX layers at 2.6, 5.1, 11.8, 16.7 cm; ~13% X0 total).
- **Systematic uncertainties** (Rgamma): Energy scale 3%, conversion loss 3%, residual background 1.5-4.5% (pT/centrality dependent), event mixing 1.5%, eta/pi0 ratio 1-1.5%. For gamma_hadr: input pi0 spectrum 10-24%.

## Methodology

1. **External photon conversion in VTX**: Silicon vertex detector (4 layers at r = 2.6, 5.1, 11.8, 16.7 cm) used as passive converter. VTX not used as active detector. Total material ~13% X0. Conversion candidates selected by pairing e+e- tracks and finding radius where delta_phi = 0.
2. **New track reconstruction**: For each trial radius (0-30 cm), the e+ and e- momenta are scanned for the azimuthal location where tracks are perpendicular to the given radius circle. Conversion point identified where delta_phi = 0.
3. **e+e- pair purity**: Uncorrelated backgrounds subtracted via event mixing (matched in 10% centrality, vertex within 2.5 cm, reaction plane within pi/6). Mass cut 0.04 < m_ee < 0.12 GeV/c^2 selects layers 3+4 of VTX.
4. **pi0 tagging**: Conversion candidates combined with EMCal showers (chi^2 < 3, E > 0.5 GeV). Foreground FG_eegamma decomposed into signal + uncorrelated BG (from event mixing) + correlated BG. Residual background corrected by polynomial fit to (FG-BG)/BG_uncorr in sidebands.
5. **Double ratio Rgamma**: Same formulation as 1405.3940 but with VTX conversion material and new track model.
6. **Conditional acceptance**: Determined from single-pi0 GEANT MC embedded into real Au+Au events at tower level. Centrality-dependent due to shower overlaps.
7. **Cocktail ratio gamma_hadr/gamma_pi0**: EXODUS generator. eta/pi0 from world data with radial flow correction Rflow = (K/pi)_AuAu / (K/pi)_pp. Universal high-pT eta/pi0 = 0.487 +/- 0.024. omega/pi0 = 0.9 +/- 0.06, eta'/pi0 = 0.25 +/- 0.075 at 5 GeV/c.
8. **pp baseline**: Fit to PHENIX pp data with d3N/d2pTdy = App / (1 + (pT/p0)^2)^n, parameters App = 1.60 x 10^-4, p0 = 1.45 GeV/c, n = 3.3.
9. **MC sampling for uncertainties**: Error sampling method propagates statistical and systematic uncertainties correctly in the presence of Rgamma < 1 unphysical values and correlated uncertainties.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **State-of-the-art Au+Au reference**: This is the definitive PHENIX measurement of direct photons in Au+Au at 200 GeV. PPG12's pp isolated photon cross section, combined with this Au+Au result, provides the complete picture of direct photon production at RHIC.
- **Prompt photon verification**: The agreement of the Au+Au data with Ncoll-scaled pp for pT > 5 GeV/c confirms that high-pT direct photons are dominated by initial hard scattering, validating the regime where PPG12 operates (8-35 GeV/c).
- **pT-dependent slope and early-stage physics**: The increasing Teff with pT suggests that higher-pT nonprompt photons probe earlier, hotter stages. This is relevant to understanding the physics probed at PPG12's lower pT edge (~8 GeV/c), where some residual nonprompt contribution may exist.
- **VTX conversion technique**: The VTX-based external conversion method and its track reconstruction algorithm represent the most advanced PHENIX photon measurement technique, informing the detector simulation and efficiency modeling approaches used at sPHENIX.
- **Event-mixing background subtraction**: The multi-step event-mixing approach (centrality+vertex+reaction-plane matching, residual background polynomial correction) is conceptually similar to the ABCD sideband methods in PPG12.
- **Fine centrality binning**: Nine 10% bins enable detailed study of scaling behavior. The scaling exponent alpha ~ 1.1 for nonprompt photons vs dNch/deta will be a key comparison point when sPHENIX extends these measurements.
- **Systematic uncertainty propagation**: The MC sampling method for propagating uncertainties through the Rgamma -> gamma_dir chain is a rigorous approach that PPG12's systematic variation pipeline emulates at the conceptual level.

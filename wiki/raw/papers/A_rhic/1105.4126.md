## Abstract

PHENIX measures the azimuthal anisotropy (v2) of direct photons in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for 1 < pT < 13 GeV/c. At low pT (1-4 GeV/c), the direct photon v2 is large and comparable to pi0 v2. Above 5-6 GeV/c, direct photon v2 is consistent with zero, as expected for initial hard scattering. The large low-pT v2, combined with the large thermal photon yield, presents a challenge to theoretical models.

## Key Results

- **Large direct photon v2 at low pT**: In minimum bias collisions, v2^{gamma,dir} ~ 0.1-0.15 for pT = 1-3 GeV/c, comparable in magnitude to pi0 v2.
- **Rapid transition to zero v2**: Direct photon v2 drops to zero above pT ~ 5 GeV/c, consistent with dominance of initial hard scattering (no collective flow).
- **v2 ratio**: v2^{gamma,dir} / v2^{pi0} is of order unity at low pT but drops rapidly above 3 GeV/c.
- **Centrality dependence**: Direct photon v2 increases with decreasing centrality (larger in 20-40% than 0-20%), similar to hadron v2 behavior.
- **High-pT v2 consistent with zero**: For pT > 6 GeV/c, direct photon v2 is consistent with zero at all centralities, within uncertainties about a factor 2 larger than predicted jet-fragmentation and jet-conversion contributions (~+/-0.01-0.02).
- **Reaction plane detector comparison**: BBC (|eta| = 3.1-3.9) and RXN (|eta| = 1.0-2.8) give consistent direct photon v2 results within systematic uncertainties, though pi0 v2 shows a jet-related bias especially at high pT with the closer RXN detector.
- **Direct photon puzzle**: The simultaneously large yield (requiring early emission at high T) and large v2 (requiring late emission when flow is developed) is very difficult for theoretical models to explain simultaneously. Models with early thermalization (tau_0 = 0.2-0.4 fm/c) reproduce the shape but severely underestimate the v2 magnitude.

## Methodology

1. **Elliptic flow measurement**: v2 measured using reaction plane method. Event-by-event reaction plane Phi_2 determined from either BBC or RXN detectors. Reaction plane resolution sigma_RP determined from sub-event correlations.
2. **pi0 v2**: Measured from pi0 -> 2gamma in EMCal using standard PHENIX methods.
3. **Inclusive photon v2**: Measured from EMCal photon clusters. Below 4 GeV/c: statistical subtraction (based on partner photon from pi0 tagging). Above 4 GeV/c: direct measurement from shower reconstruction.
4. **Direct photon v2 extraction**:
   v2^{gamma,dir} = (Rgamma * v2^{gamma,inc} - v2^{gamma,bg}) / (Rgamma - 1)
   where Rgamma from internal conversion measurement (0804.4168) for pT < 4 GeV/c and from calorimetric measurement (nucl-ex_0503003) for pT > 4 GeV/c.
5. **Background photon v2**: v2^{gamma,bg} calculated from hadronic cocktail, dominated by pi0 decay photons which inherit the pi0 v2.
6. **Systematic uncertainties**: Dominated by reaction plane resolution correction, particle identification, and Rgamma uncertainty (delta_Rgamma/Rgamma from Table I). Energy scale uncertainties largely cancel in v2 measurement.
7. **Data sample**: RHIC Run 4 (2004) Au+Au at 200 GeV; same dataset as 0804.4168.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Direct photon puzzle context**: PPG12 measures the pp isolated photon cross section, which establishes the prompt component baseline. The direct photon puzzle -- large thermal yield AND large thermal v2 -- motivates the need for precise pp baselines to separate prompt from thermal contributions in heavy-ion collisions.
- **Isolation of prompt vs thermal**: The fact that v2 transitions to zero above 5 GeV/c confirms that PPG12's operating range (8-35 GeV/c) is firmly in the prompt-dominated regime where NLO pQCD predictions apply and thermal contamination is negligible.
- **Rgamma framework**: The Rgamma-based extraction of direct photon properties (both yield and flow) is the same double-ratio framework used across all PHENIX photon measurements. PPG12's ABCD method is the sPHENIX evolution of this approach.
- **High-pT photon flow**: The consistency of v2 ~ 0 for pT > 6 GeV/c at all centralities validates that prompt (hard-scattered) photons do not acquire significant collective motion, supporting the use of pp isolated photon cross sections as a clean probe of initial-state physics.
- **Fragmentation and jet-conversion photon v2**: The predicted small positive (fragmentation) and small negative (jet-conversion) v2 contributions at high pT are not resolved by this measurement but are relevant to understanding potential small systematic effects in PPG12's isolation cut efficiency, since fragmentation photons fail isolation more often when the jet axis is in-plane vs out-of-plane.

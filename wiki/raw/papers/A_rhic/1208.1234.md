## Abstract

Direct photon production is measured in d+Au and p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using three independent methods: virtual photon (internal conversion), pi0-tagging, and statistical subtraction. The d+Au results are consistent with binary-scaled p+p across the full pT range (1-16 GeV/c). The nuclear modification factor RdA is consistent with unity. Standard cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects (Cronin enhancement, isospin, shadowing, initial-state energy loss) are all within experimental uncertainties. RAA in Au+Au is much larger than RdA below 2 GeV/c, confirming a significant medium effect.

## Key Results

- **Three independent analyses agree**: Virtual photon (1-5 GeV/c), pi0-tagging (3-16 GeV/c), and statistical subtraction (3-6 GeV/c) methods give consistent d+Au results in overlap regions.
- **pp fit function**: Empirical pQCD-inspired parameterization E d3sigma/dp3 = a * pT^{-(b+c*ln(xT))} * (1-xT^2)^n, where xT = 2pT/sqrt(s). Fit parameters: a = (6.6 +/- 3.3) x 10^-3, b = 6.4 +/- 0.3, c = 0.4 +/- 0.2, n = 17.6 +/- 14.9, chi^2/NDF = 22.4/16. Power-law exponent b + c*ln(xT) = 4.6-5.5 for 0.01 < xT < 0.1.
- **NLO pQCD comparison**: NLO pQCD agrees with pp data for pT > 2 GeV/c, with preference for scale mu = 0.5 pT.
- **RdA consistent with unity**: Nuclear modification factor RdA = d+Au / (Ncoll-scaled pp fit) is consistent with 1 across full pT range, within point-to-point and correlated uncertainties.
- **CNM effects**: Theoretical calculations with Cronin enhancement, isospin effect, nuclear shadowing (EKS98), and initial-state energy loss are all consistent with data but data lack precision to discriminate between models.
- **Power-law index at high pT**: d+Au fit for pT > 8 GeV/c gives n = 7.17 +/- 0.76(stat) +/- 0.01(syst), consistent with pp (n = 7.08 +/- 0.09) and Au+Au (n = 7.18 +/- 0.14).
- **RAA comparison**: Au+Au RAA >> 1 (reaching > 7) below 2 GeV/c, while d+Au RdA ~ 1, confirming the Au+Au excess is a final-state (medium) effect, not an initial-state effect.
- **pp data improved**: 2006 pp data added to earlier 2003 data, improving statistics for the pp baseline.

## Methodology

1. **Virtual photon method** (1-5 GeV/c): Same as 0804.4168. Internal conversion of virtual photons to low-mass e+e- pairs. Two-component fit separating direct and cocktail contributions.
2. **Pi0-tagging method** (3-16 GeV/c): Inclusive photon candidates identified in EMCal (E > 150 MeV, > 10 towers inside edge). Pairs reconstructed to 105 < m_gammagamma < 165 MeV tagged as pi0 decays. Direct photons: gamma_dir = gamma_incl - (1 + Rh/pi0)(1 + delta_miss) * gamma_pi0->2gamma. delta_miss from fast MC simulation (~6-8% uncertainty).
3. **Statistical subtraction method**: Rγ = (dN_gamma/dpT / dN_pi0->2gamma/dpT)_data / (same)_sim. Hadronic cocktail simulation for background estimation using mT-scaling. gamma_dir = (1 - Rgamma^-1) * gamma_incl.
4. **pp fit**: Combined fit to virtual photon (1.5-5 GeV/c) and real photon (> 5 GeV/c) data with pQCD-inspired function including scaling violation correction (c*ln(xT) term) and kinematic boundary factor (1-xT^2)^n.
5. **d+Au Glauber**: Binary collision scaling for nuclear modification factor. TdA uncertainty shown as separate box.
6. **CNM theory comparison**: Calculations by Kopeliovich et al. with various combinations of Cronin enhancement, isospin, EKS98 shadowing, and initial-state energy loss.

## Relevance to PPG12

- **pp direct photon baseline**: The pQCD-inspired fit function used here (with scaling violation corrections) provides the pp reference parameterization for interpreting all RHIC direct photon results. PPG12 will provide the first sPHENIX measurement of the isolated pp photon cross section at 200 GeV, extending and refining this PHENIX pp baseline.
- **Cold nuclear matter constraints**: RdA ~ 1 confirms that initial-state nuclear effects (shadowing, Cronin, isospin, energy loss) are small for direct photons at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. This validates the assumption in PPG12 that NLO pQCD predictions for pp are directly applicable without nuclear modification corrections.
- **Three analysis methods**: The convergence of virtual photon, pi0-tagging, and statistical subtraction methods validates the direct photon measurement methodology. PPG12's isolation-based approach with BDT photon identification is a fourth, independent method that provides additional cross-checks.
- **NLO pQCD scale preference**: The preference for mu = 0.5 pT is relevant to PPG12's JETPHOX NLO comparisons, where the scale choice (pT/2, pT, 2pT) defines the theory uncertainty band.
- **Power-law behavior**: The effective power-law index n ~ 7.1 for pT > 8 GeV/c characterizes the spectrum slope in PPG12's pT range.
- **RAA vs RdA comparison**: Demonstrates the physics reach of comparing pp, d+Au, and Au+Au direct photon cross sections, motivating PPG12's pp measurement as the critical first step.

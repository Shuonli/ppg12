## Abstract

PHENIX measures the centrality dependence of direct photon yield from Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV down to pT = 0.4 GeV/c using photon conversions to e+e- pairs. An improved technique minimizes systematic uncertainties at low pT. The direct photon excess above Ncoll-scaled pp is exponential with inverse slope ~240 MeV/c for 0.6-2.0 GeV/c. The shape is centrality-independent; the yield scales as Npart^alpha with alpha = 1.38 +/- 0.03(stat) +/- 0.07(syst).

## Key Results

- **pT reach extended to 0.4 GeV/c**: First real (not virtual) direct photon measurement at such low pT at RHIC. Uses photon conversions in the HBD readout plane.
- **Inverse slopes** (Teff, 0.6-2.0 GeV/c): 0-20%: 239 +/- 25 +/- 7 MeV/c; 20-40%: 260 +/- 33 +/- 8 MeV/c; 40-60%: 225 +/- 28 +/- 6 MeV/c; 60-92%: 238 +/- 50 +/- 6 MeV/c; 0-92% (MB): 242 +/- 28 +/- 7 MeV/c.
- **Centrality-independent shape**: Inverse slopes consistent across all four centrality bins within uncertainties.
- **Npart scaling**: Integrated thermal photon yield scales as Npart^alpha with alpha = 1.38 +/- 0.03(stat) +/- 0.07(syst), consistent across different pT lower thresholds (0.4-1.4 GeV/c). Also consistent with Nqp^beta with beta = 1.27 +/- 0.03(stat) +/- 0.07(syst).
- **Rgamma**: Significant excess above unity in central collisions (Rgamma ~ 1.2-1.4 for pT = 1-3 GeV/c in 0-20% centrality). Consistent with earlier virtual photon measurement (0804.4168).
- **Two independent datasets**: 2007 run (1.4 x 10^9 MB events) and 2010 run (2.6 x 10^9 MB events) with consistent Rgamma results.
- **Conversion purity**: 99% photon conversion sample purity from GEANT simulation.
- **pp baseline fit**: a(1 + pT^2/b)^c with a = (8.3 +/- 7.5) x 10^-3, b = 2.26 +/- 0.78, c = -3.45 +/- 0.08.

## Methodology

1. **External conversion in HBD**: Photons convert to e+e- in the HBD readout plane (radius ~60 cm, X/X0 ~ 2.5%). Track reconstruction assumes vertex origin, producing artificial opening angle (~10 mrad) and mass (M_vtx ~ 12 MeV/c^2). Alternate track model (ATM) assuming HBD origin gives M_HBD ~ few MeV/c^2.
2. **Conversion selection**: 2D cut on M_vtx (10-15 MeV/c^2) and M_HBD (< 4.5 MeV/c^2).
3. **pi0 tagging**: Conversion photons combined with EMCal photon-like showers to reconstruct pi0 mass. False tagging from hadrons reduced by shower shape and energy threshold cuts (E > 0.4 GeV for 2010, 0.6 GeV for 2007).
4. **Double ratio Rgamma**: Rgamma = <epsilon_gamma * f> * (N_incl/N_pi0tag)_data * (gamma_hadron/gamma_pi0)_sim. Efficiency/acceptance of e+e- pair cancels exactly in the ratio.
5. **Conditional acceptance**: <epsilon_gamma * f> = probability that second pi0 decay photon is detected in EMCal, given first was converted. Determined by full GEANT (2007) or fast MC simulation (2010). Cross-checked between methods.
6. **Hadron decay cocktail**: EXODUS generator with measured pi0 spectra, eta/pi0 = 0.46 +/- 0.06, omega/pi0 = 0.9 +/- 0.06, eta'/pi0 = 0.25 +/- 0.075 at 5 GeV/c. mT-scaling for eta, omega, eta'.
7. **Systematic uncertainties on Rgamma** (Table I): pi0 reconstruction 4% (Type A), energy scale 4% (Type B), conversion loss 2% (Type C), eta/pi0 ratio 2.2% (Type C). Total < 6%.
8. **Direct photon yield**: gamma_direct = (Rgamma - 1) * gamma_hadron. Additional 10% systematic on input pi0 distribution.
9. **Momentum resolution**: sigma_pT/pT = 0.9% + 0.5% * pT_ee (for HBD back-plane origin).

## Relevance to PPG12

- **Conversion technique and pi0 tagging**: The HBD external conversion technique with pi0 tagging is analogous to how PPG12 uses EMCal shower shapes and BDT scores to separate signal from background photons. Both analyses rely on a "tagging" step that creates a known composition subsample.
- **Conditional acceptance <epsilon*f>**: This concept of conditional detection probability, averaged over parent kinematics, parallels PPG12's reconstruction efficiency corrections that account for the probability of correctly reconstructing an isolated photon given the cluster kinematics.
- **Systematic uncertainty taxonomy**: The Type A/B/C classification of uncertainties (uncorrelated, pT-correlated, constant multiplicative) mirrors the systematic uncertainty framework in PPG12.
- **Npart scaling physics**: The alpha ~ 1.38 scaling constrains the centrality dependence of thermal radiation. For PPG12's pp measurement, the relevant quantity is the prompt (Ncoll-scaled) contribution, but understanding the thermal component matters for interpreting any future Au+Au extensions.
- **Hadron cocktail and eta/pi0**: The eta/pi0 ratio and mT-scaling assumptions directly affect the background estimation. PPG12's ABCD method avoids much of this by using data-driven background estimation, but the physics motivation is the same.
- **Momentum resolution at low pT**: Understanding the vertex-dependent momentum resolution is relevant to PPG12's vertex-dependent shower shape and ET corrections, especially in the context of double-interaction (pileup) studies.

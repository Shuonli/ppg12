# Mid-Rapidity Direct-Photon Production in p+p Collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

## Abstract

A measurement of direct photons in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is presented. A photon excess above background from pi0 -> gamma+gamma, eta -> gamma+gamma and other decays is observed in the transverse momentum range 5.5 < pT < 7 GeV/c. The result is compared to a next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculation. Within errors, good agreement is found between the QCD calculation and the measured result.

## Key Results

- First observation of direct photon signal at RHIC in p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Signal observed in a narrow pT window: 5.5 < pT < 7 GeV/c (3 data points at pT = 5.75, 6.25, 6.75 GeV/c)
- Cross section values: E d3sigma/d3p = 5.61e-7, 2.68e-7, 2.37e-7 mb GeV^-2 c^3 at pT = 5.75, 6.25, 6.75 GeV/c
- Large statistical and systematic uncertainties (37-56% systematic, 42-75% statistical)
- Consistent with NLO pQCD calculations (Gordon-Vogelsang) using CTEQ6 PDF and GRV fragmentation function at scales mu = pT/2, pT, 2pT
- At pT = 6.75 GeV/c, gamma_cand/pi0 ratio shows clear excess above calculated background, establishing the direct photon signal
- Provides validation of NLO pQCD as a baseline reference for Au+Au direct photon measurements (no suppression observed for direct photons in Au+Au, unlike pi0)

## Methodology

### Detector and data
- Run 2 (2001-2002) dataset; minimum-bias sample: 16.7 million events (L = 0.77 nb^-1); 2x2 EMCal trigger sample: 18.7 million events (L = 40.3 nb^-1)
- EMCal: PbSc (6 sectors) + PbGl (2 sectors), each sector |eta| < 0.35, delta_phi ~ 22.5 degrees
- PbSc tower: 5.5 x 5.5 cm^2; PbGl tower: 4 x 4 cm^2; delta_phi x delta_eta ~ 0.01 x 0.01
- Two decay photons from pi0 resolved up to pT ~ 20 GeV/c
- BBC minimum-bias trigger cross section: 21.8 +/- 2.1 mb (~50% of inelastic pp at 200 GeV)

### Direct photon extraction (double-ratio method)
- Direct photon candidates defined by rejecting EMCal hits paired with another hit in the pi0 (110-170 MeV/c^2) or eta (500-620 MeV/c^2) invariant mass windows
- Partner photon requirement: pT > 0.4 GeV/c
- Loss of genuine direct photons from false tagging: < 2% for pT > 3 GeV/c
- Fiducial area: minimum 16 (20) towers from edge for PbSc (PbGl) to ensure partner photon acceptance
- No isolation cut applied
- R_gamma = (gamma_cand/pi0) / (gamma_bkg/pi0), then gamma_direct = (1 - R_gamma^-1) * gamma_cand
- Background calculated using Monte Carlo with measured pi0 spectrum input; eta/pi0 ratio = 0.48 +/- 0.1 (mT scaling)

### Systematic uncertainties at pT = 6.75 GeV/c
- Pi0 spectrum: 13% (PbGl), 12% (PbSc) total (yield extraction 5%, yield correction 6-8%, energy scale 8-9%)
- gamma_cand spectrum: 12% (PbGl), 11% (PbSc) total (non-gamma bkg 4%, yield correction 5-6%, energy scale 9%)
- gamma_cand/pi0 ratio: 9% (combined PbGl+PbSc); gamma_bkg/pi0: 4%
- Energy scale uncertainty: 1.5% absolute, leading to ~9% cross section uncertainty
- Luminosity normalization: 9.6%
- BBC bias factor: f = 0.75 +/- 0.02

### Cross section formula
- E d3sigma/d3p = (1/L_hat) * (1/2pi pT) * (C_reco * C_conv * C_loss / f) * (N_cand_direct / delta_pT delta_y)
- C_reco: acceptance and efficiency; C_conv: photon conversion correction (4.1-7.4% depending on arm); C_loss: correction for genuine direct photon loss from tagging

## Relevance to PPG12

- This is the founding PHENIX direct photon measurement at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. It established the methodology (pi0-tagging, double-ratio R_gamma, NLO pQCD comparison framework) that was refined in the 2012 publication (1205.5533) and which PPG12 conceptually inherits.
- The very limited pT range (5.5-7 GeV/c with only 3 data points) and large uncertainties demonstrate the statistical limitations of Run 2 data. PPG12's Run 24 sPHENIX dataset with full azimuthal coverage and 16.6 pb^-1 represents a several-orders-of-magnitude improvement.
- PPG12 uses a fundamentally different background subtraction approach (BDT-based ABCD method) rather than the pi0-tagging statistical subtraction, motivated by the much larger EMCal (|eta| < 0.7 vs 0.35) and the need to handle the different cluster merging properties of the sPHENIX EMCal.
- The lack of an isolation cut in this early measurement (and the subsequent 1205.5533 comparison showing ~90% isolated/inclusive ratio) influenced the decision to use isolation as a primary discriminant in PPG12's ABCD framework.
- NLO pQCD (Gordon-Vogelsang with CTEQ PDFs, GRV FF) serves as the same theory comparison framework used by PPG12 via JETPHOX.

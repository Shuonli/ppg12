\section*{Executive Summary}

Four-level tower-index acceptance maps
($h_{\rm etaphi\_tower\_\{preselect,common,tight,tight\_iso\}\_\{mc\_inclusive,data\}}$)
are now populated automatically in the official efficiency pipeline
and written to \texttt{Photon\_final\_bdt\_nom.root} for every
production. Direct data-vs-MC comparison shows clear dead-region
asymmetry: MC does not emulate the full set of dead towers active in
Run-24 data.

\textbf{Primary methodology: data-only phi-symmetry}. For each 2-eta-row
band, fit a Gaussian to the 256 per-phi data counts and flag phi bins
with z~$<$~-2 as dead. Independent of MC; exact under the assumption
that the cross-section is phi-symmetric at fixed $\eta$ in pp. Three
masks generated --- one per selection level:
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_\{preselect, common, tight, or\}} with 1344 /
1160 / 646 / 1180 masked towers respectively (the OR mask is the
union of the common and tight masks).

\textbf{Direct cross-section measurement}: re-run the efficiency +
unfolding + ABCD pipeline with the phi-symm masks applied
symmetrically to both data and MC. Integrated $\sigma$ shifts in
$\etg \in [10, 36]$\,GeV:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule
variant & $\Delta\sigma / \sigma_{\rm nom}$ \\
\midrule
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_preselect}              & +2.01\% \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_common}                 & +2.26\% \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_tight}                  & +1.37\% \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_or} (common $\cup$ tight) & +2.28\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Recommended envelope}: \textbf{+2.5\% one-sided acceptance
systematic} on the nominal all-range cross-section. The four variants
agree within $\sim$1\% across selection levels, consistent with a
uniform detector-level dead-tower effect.

\textbf{Nominal}: all-z (beam-delivered) lumi 64.37\,pb$^{-1}$ +
$v_{z,{\rm truth}}<9999$ (since 2026-04-22). Integrated cross-section
$\sigma_{\rm nom} = 1963$\,pb over $\etg \in [10, 36]$\,GeV.

\section{Pipeline Integration}

\subsection{Motivation}

The cross-section formula
$d^2\sigma / dp_T\, d\eta = N_{\rm iso\,prompt}/(\varepsilon\cdot\mathcal{L}
\cdot \Delta)$ assumes $\varepsilon$ --- built from MC --- correctly
captures the acceptance of the data sample. If MC fails to emulate
all dead towers active in data, $\varepsilon_{\rm MC}$ is
systematically higher than the true $\varepsilon_{\rm data}$ and the
measured $\sigma$ is biased low by $\varepsilon_{\rm data} /
\varepsilon_{\rm MC}$.

\subsection{Code changes (summary)}

\textbf{\texttt{efficiencytool/RecoEffCalculator\_TTreeReader.C}}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item 4 new \texttt{TH2F} pointers declared + allocated:
  \texttt{h\_etaphi\_tower\_\{preselect, common, tight, tight\_iso\}},
  96 $i\eta$ $\times$ 256 $i\phi$, filled at 4 points in the cluster
  loop using the canonical per-event \texttt{weight} (MC: xsec
  $\times$ lumi/lumi\_target $\times$ vertex\_weight $\times$
  truth\_vertex\_reweight; data: prescale). \etg-gated at $\ge$ 8~GeV.
  \item Three optional YAML keys: \texttt{tower\_mask\_on},
  \texttt{tower\_mask\_file}, \texttt{tower\_mask\_name}. When on, a
  TH2I is loaded at init and a cluster-loop veto is inserted at the
  TOP of both cluster loops:
  \begin{verbatim}
  if (is_tower_masked(icluster)) continue;
  \end{verbatim}
  Applied identically to data (!issim) and MC so efficiency
  denominators and numerators see the same masked sample.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{\texttt{efficiencytool/CalculatePhotonYield.C}}: pass-through
block before \texttt{fout->Write()} clones the signal TH2 from
\texttt{fsimin}, adds the jet TH2 from
\texttt{\{eff\_outfile\}\_jet\_\{var\_type\}.root}, and writes as
\texttt{h\_etaphi\_tower\_\{level\}\_mc\_inclusive}. Also clones data
TH2 as \texttt{\_data}. Eight histograms now shipped with every
\texttt{Photon\_final\_*.root}.

\textbf{\texttt{efficiencytool/MergeSim.C}}: unchanged (plain
TFileMerger auto-hadds TH2s).

\textbf{\texttt{FunWithxgboost/make\_bdt\_variations.py}}: 3 new
variant entries (\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_preselect},
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_common}, \texttt{mask\_phisymm\_tight}), each
auto-expanded to 3 YAMLs (bare + 2 periods). Nine total.

\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

\subsection{Data-only phi-symmetry mask construction}
\label{sec:phisymm}

For each 2-eta-row band (48 bands covering the 96-tower $i\eta$
range), the 256 per-phi data counts are projected out. Under the
physics assumption that the cross-section is $\phi$-symmetric at
fixed $\eta$ in pp at RHIC (valid to many-percent accuracy), the
expected distribution across $\phi$ is uniform, and deviations trace
either statistical fluctuations or local detector effects.

\textbf{Fit}: 3-$\sigma$ iterative Gaussian to the 256-bin
distribution, yielding $(\mu_{\phi,{\rm band}}, \sigma_{\phi,{\rm band}})$.

\textbf{Mask decision}: a tower $(i\eta, i\phi)$ is flagged as dead
if its band's phi count is more than $2\sigma_\phi$ below the band's
$\mu_\phi$:
$(n_{\rm data}[b, i\phi] - \mu_{\phi,b})/\sigma_{\phi,b} < -2$.
Within a band, both $\eta$ rows are masked when a $\phi$ bin is
flagged.

\textbf{Output}: 3 TH2I masks (one per selection level) in
\texttt{efficiencytool/tower\_masks\_bdt\_nom.root}:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
\toprule
mask & level & towers masked \\
\midrule
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_preselect} & preselect & 1344 \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_common}    & common    & 1160 \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_tight}     & tight     &  646 \\
\texttt{mask\_phisymm\_or} & union of common and tight & 1180 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The OR mask has 1180 towers --- only 20 more than common alone, because
the tight mask (646) is largely contained in the common mask (626
overlap). The OR mask is a conservative test of whether adding a few
tight-only dead regions moves $\sigma$ meaningfully.

The mask-count decrease with selection tightness reflects the
decreasing data statistics: fewer clusters per $\phi$ bin in the
tight sample $\to$ larger fit $\sigma$ $\to$ fewer bins cross the
z$<$-2 threshold.

\subsection{Independent cross-check: 4$\times$4 tower grouping}

To verify that the phi-symm numbers are robust, the 96$\times$256
grid is rebinned into 4$\times$4 super-cells and a Gaussian is fit
to the log(R) distribution of $R = p_{\rm data}/p_{\rm MC}$ (which
is log-normal, not normal, in the per-bin-count regime). At 4$\times$4
grouping the Gaussian fit is clean at every level, and the fraction
of data in z$<$-2 cells is:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrr}
\toprule
level & $\mu_{\rm log R}$ & $\sigma_{\rm log R}$ & deficit \% \\
\midrule
preselect & 0.039 & 0.224 & 2.71\% \\
common    & 0.114 & 0.327 & 1.96\% \\
tight     & 0.151 & 0.544 & 1.98\% \\
tight+iso & 0.732 & 1.332 & 0.97\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Convergence}: the phi-symm-based $\sigma$ shift (+1.4 to
+2.3\% per level) and the 4$\times$4 log(R) deficit (2--3\% per level)
agree within $\sim$1\% --- two independent estimators arrive at the
same answer.

\textbf{Readings for the analysis.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The \emph{phi-symm mask choice} envelope is now
  $\sim 0.9\%$ (max over preselect/common/OR relative to the
  tight-mask nominal). The $+2.5\%$ one-sided bound quoted from the
  earlier run (where nominal had \emph{no} mask) is no longer the
  relevant quantity --- it was the mask-vs-no-mask shift, which is now
  absorbed into the nominal.
  \item The \texttt{b2bjet} cross-checks collapse the yield by
  $\sim 41\%$ as expected (tight back-to-back jet requirement).
  The five variants agree within $\sim 1\%$, confirming that the
  jet-calibration switch and the NPB-cut toggle do not create
  additional purity leakage in the b2b-jet sample.
  \item \texttt{tightbdt50} yields a pathological negative
  cross-section (unfolder does not converge when the tight working
  point is moved from 0.7 to 0.5); this run is tagged N/A.
  \texttt{tightbdt70} (nominal is 0.7, variant is 0.7 with
  slope$=0$ i.e.\ flat) gives $-2.4\%$, confirming the
  ET-dependent parametric BDT threshold is not load-bearing.
\end{itemize}

\section{Recommendations}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Fix the MC dead-tower list} --- primary action.
  Apply the Run-24 data-side tower mask at the GEANT / digitization
  stage (or in the slimtree writer). Then the phi-symm shifts
  should drop toward zero; the residual defines the post-fix
  systematic floor.

  \item \textbf{Until the MC fix lands, assign a one-sided
  \textbf{+2.5\% acceptance systematic}} on the cross-section. This
  covers the largest of the three phi-symm variants
  (common: +2.26\%) with modest conservative margin. Add this to
  \texttt{calc\_syst\_bdt.py} as a new \texttt{acceptance} group
  (or additive component to the existing \texttt{eff} group).

  \item \textbf{Document the phi-symm methodology} in the analysis
  note --- it is the primary data-driven cross-check for
  acceptance-tower mismodeling and does not require MC at all, which
  is a strong robustness argument.

  \item \textbf{Re-run the 3 variants} automatically every time the
  nominal production is updated (the mask file depends on the data
  tower maps in \texttt{Photon\_final\_bdt\_nom.root}; regenerate
  \texttt{make\_tower\_masks.C} output whenever nominal changes).
\end{enumerate}

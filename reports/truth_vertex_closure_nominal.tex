\section*{Executive summary}
The postdoc's two questions, answered up front:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{(a) Is the weight derived by matching the inclusive-MC reco-vertex to data?}
        \emph{Partially.} The matching observable \emph{is} \zreco, but the MC forward model used by the fitter is \texttt{photon10}~+~\texttt{photon10\_double} only (blended at the per-period double-interaction fraction). \textbf{No jet MC enters the fit.} The term ``inclusive MC'' is therefore misleading for the current reweight files; the reweight is better described as a \emph{photon10-driven} fit to data \zreco, applied \emph{universally} to all MC samples through $w(\zhard)\cdot w(\zmb)$.

  \item \textbf{(b) After the nominal pipeline, does the weighted-sum MC reco-vertex match data?}
        \emph{Yes for 0\,mrad, marginally for 1.5\,mrad, and within $O(1\text{--}2\%)$ for the all-range lumi-weighted hadd in the analysis core} ($|z|<60$ cm). See Fig.~\ref{fig:closure}. The 1.5 mrad iterative fit did not converge at the derivation stage, which leaves the post-pipeline closure there at the $\chidf=14.8$ level on shape (tails up to $\sim$15\%).
\end{itemize}

The central qualitative answer (``we are matching MC \zreco\ to data \zreco, and the post-pipeline MC reproduces data'') is correct. The quantitative caveats (photon-only forward model, 1.5\,mrad non-convergence) deserve attention and are discussed below.

\section{How the reweight is derived}

Fit driver: \texttt{efficiencytool/truth\_vertex\_reweight/fit\_truth\_vertex\_reweight.py}.
Loader consumed downstream: \texttt{efficiencytool/TruthVertexReweightLoader.h}.

\paragraph{Target.} Period-filtered, trigger-filtered data \zreco\ histogram $D(\zreco)$, 80 bins of 5\,cm, $z \in [-200,200]$\,cm. Selection: \texttt{runnumber}~$\in$~[run\_min, run\_max) and \texttt{scaledtrigger[bit~30]}~$\neq 0$ (Photon\_4\_GeV).

\paragraph{MC forward model.} The fit builds
\[
  M(\zreco) \;=\; f_{\mathrm{single}}\,M_{\mathrm{single}}(\zreco) \;+\; f_{\mathrm{double}}\,M_{\mathrm{double}}(\zreco),
\]
where each term is filled at \zreco\ but weighted event-by-event by
\[
  w_{\mathrm{evt}} =
  \frac{\sigma}{N_{\mathrm{gen}}}
  \cdot w(\zhard) \cdot w(\zmb) \text{ (DI only; SI uses } w(\zhard)\text{)}.
\]
The per-period fractions $f_{\mathrm{double}}$ are $0.224$ (0\,mrad) and $0.079$ (1.5\,mrad), from \texttt{calc\_pileup\_range.C}. Live fit metadata in \texttt{output/\{0mrad,1p5mrad\}/reweight.root} records:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{sample\_list\_single = 'photon10'}
  \item \texttt{sample\_list\_double = 'photon10\_double'}
\end{itemize}
\texttt{fit.log} in both periods shows \texttt{``skipping single MC jet12 (not matching filter 'photon10')''}. Jet MC is listed in the fitter's YAML but was excluded from the live run. This is the only material caveat on the derivation.

\paragraph{Iteration scheme.} Three stages (parametric Gaussian $w(z)\propto\exp[\tfrac12 z^2(1/\sigma_{\mathrm{gen}}^2 - 1/\sigma_{\mathrm{tgt}}^2)]$; Stage-2 residual $c(\zreco)=D/M_{\mathrm{stage1}}$ smoothed; iterative Lucy--Richardson multiplicative update $w \mathrel{*}= (D/M)^{\alpha=0.5}$ with 30-iter cap and $\max|D/M-1|<0.01$ convergence). The downstream loader reads ONLY \texttt{h\_w\_iterative} (1D in truth~$z$, 5\,cm bins).

\paragraph{Convergence (derivation stage).}
\begin{itemize}
  \item 0\,mrad: converged in 8 iterations, $\max|D/M-1|=0.0086$ inside the 99th-percentile closure window ($|z|<145$\,cm).
  \item 1.5\,mrad: \textbf{did not converge} in 30 iterations, final $\max|D/M-1|=0.111$ inside $|z|<83$\,cm.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Downstream application.}
\texttt{RecoEffCalculator\_TTreeReader.C} loads the period's \texttt{h\_w\_iterative} at line~284 and multiplies it into every per-event weight at lines 1517--1522 alongside the cross-section and the per-event \texttt{lumi/lumi\_target} factor, applied uniformly to all 8 SI + 8 DI MC samples. The legacy reco-vertex flag \texttt{vertex\_reweight\_on} is \emph{actually} zeroed in code at L145--150 when the truth path is on; it is not a YAML-only convention. \texttt{MergeSim.C}, \texttt{merge\_periods.C} and \texttt{CalculatePhotonYield.C} are plain \texttt{TFileMerger}s / yield arithmetic, so the reweight is correctly absorbed once at \texttt{RecoEffCalculator\_TTreeReader.C} and propagated by downstream hadds.

\section{Post-pipeline closure}

After the nominal pipeline (per-period \texttt{RecoEffCalculator\_TTreeReader.C} $\rightarrow$ \texttt{MergeSim.C} per period $\rightarrow$ \texttt{merge\_periods.C} lumi-weighted hadd), a 1D \texttt{h\_vertexz} is written at the top level of each data and MC output file. This is the observable to compare against data.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{0\,mrad is closed.} $\chidf=1.31$ is statistics-limited: data has $3.4\times10^{7}$ clusters, so any residual sub-percent shape bias is visible. Ratio is flat at $\sim$1 across the full analysis window.
  \item \textbf{1.5\,mrad is not closed in the tails.} The derivation-stage non-convergence propagates: bin-by-bin $D/M$ reaches $\sim$1.14 at $z=-50$\,cm and $\sim$1.04 at $z=+50$\,cm. Core (|z|$<30$\,cm) is sub-percent.
  \item \textbf{All-range hadd is closed in the core.} Shape agreement is within 1--2\% at $|z|<50$\,cm; the large $\chidf=9.67$ reflects the sub-percent deviations becoming many-$\sigma$ with the $5\times10^{7}$ data sample. KS $\sim 2.5\times10^{-3}$ on shape is consistent with ``the shape matches the data in the analysis window''.
\end{itemize}

The post-pipeline closure \emph{confirms that the weighted combination of all 16 SI + DI MC samples reproduces the data \zreco\ shape in the analysis window}. It does not, on its own, validate that the photon10-only derivation was the right choice: the 1.5\,mrad tail discrepancy is a direct symptom of the fit-stage non-convergence.

\section{Caveats and recommendations}

\paragraph{Methodology cross-check (recommended).}
To bound the channel-dependence worry, derive a second reweight using \texttt{jet12}~+~\texttt{jet12\_double} as the forward MC (fitter supports this via \texttt{--match jet12}) and compare \texttt{h\_w\_iterative} to the live photon10-driven version at the same period. The spread in the two $w(z)$'s is a defensible input to a ``truth-vertex-reweight forward-model'' systematic. Physics expectation: $z_{\mathrm{hard}}^{\mathrm{truth}}$ is a beam-profile observable and is channel-independent at fixed beam optics; any non-trivial $w$ spread therefore flags a trigger-selected residual-pileup or acceptance effect, which the current photon-only reweight absorbs and then pushes onto jet samples.

\paragraph{1.5\,mrad non-convergence.} Suggested actions: (i)~try a relaxed $\alpha$ (e.g.\ 0.3) or a wider max-iter cap; (ii)~expand the closure window from $|z|<z_{\mathrm{cut,99}}=83$\,cm to cover the analysis fiducial symmetrically; (iii)~include jet MC in the forward model (likely root cause, since the 1.5\,mrad beam is tight and data there is jet-dominated). Combined with the cross-check above, option (iii) addresses both issues.

\paragraph{Cross-section systematic.}
A biased $w(z)$ couples to the cross-section dominantly through the vertex-cut acceptance in $\epsilon_{\mathrm{reco}}$. Rough envelope: $O(1\text{--}2\%)$ on the nominal cross-section, concentrated in the lowest-\ET\ bin. Currently no dedicated systematic covers this; the photon10-vs-jet12 cross-check fit is the cleanest way to assign one.

\begin{abstract}
The PPG12 analysis uses an ABCD method for background subtraction, parametrised
by $E_T$-dependent tight and non-tight BDT thresholds. To probe the stability
of the background estimate under simultaneous relaxation of the
tight-lo, non-tight-hi, and non-tight-lo boundaries, we extend the
existing \texttt{purity\_nonclosure\_ntbdt} (flat-scalar) cross-check
with nine $E_T$-parametric anchor combinations defined at $E_T = 10$~GeV
and $E_T = 25$~GeV, each processed twice (1.5~mrad and 0~mrad
crossing-angle periods) under the new double-interaction (DI) +
truth-vertex-reweight pipeline. The non-tight upper bound is decoupled
from the tight lower bound; one low-anchor (C) enforces an excluded BDT
band $[0.70, 0.80]$ at $E_T = 10$~GeV to test the effect of a
BDT-exclusion gap. With the updated MC-sample combining code
(\texttt{MergeSim.C}), twelve of the 18 reprocessed cross-sections
remain flagged \textsc{CRIT} ($|\max_{\rm bin}\Delta_{\rm rel}| > 0.50$)
while six — all 0~mrad variants with a High Y'/Z' anchor
($\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}(25) = 0.75$) — drop to \textsc{WARN}
($0.20 < |\Delta| \le 0.50$). All signs are positive, with magnitudes
in the range $+0.34$ to $+0.74$, peaking at $E_T = 30$~GeV (1.5~mrad)
or $E_T = 11$--$27$~GeV (0~mrad). ABCD purity at $E_T = 25$~GeV
recovers close to nominal for the 1.5~mrad period but stays suppressed
for 0~mrad. We interpret this as expected ABCD-closure breakdown at
relaxed thresholds rather than a pipeline bug; the variants are
\emph{not} suitable for inclusion in the systematic-uncertainty
envelope.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation}

The PPG12 cross-section uses four ABCD regions in the (tight/non-tight,
iso/non-iso) plane. Signal leakage into the non-tight control region is
controlled by the lower edge of the non-tight BDT window
($\mathrm{nt}_{\rm lo}$) and by the tight-region lower edge
($\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$) — the non-tight upper edge tracks $\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$
to keep the two bands contiguous.

The existing \texttt{purity\_nonclosure\_ntbdt} cross-check varies
$\mathrm{nt}_{\rm lo}$ as a flat scalar. Because the nominal thresholds are
$E_T$-parametric
\begin{equation*}
  \mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}(E_T) = \text{intercept} + \text{slope} \cdot E_T,
\end{equation*}
a flat variation is not kinematically self-consistent over the full
$10 < E_T < 36$~GeV range. We therefore extend the cross-check to a
two-anchor linear parametrisation of three quantities simultaneously:
the analyst specifies the triple
$(\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}, \mathrm{nt}_{\rm hi}, \mathrm{nt}_{\rm lo})$ at
$E_T = 10$~GeV (low anchors A/B/C) and at $E_T = 25$~GeV (high anchors
X/Y/Z), and the pipeline converts the two anchors into the
slope/intercept pair
\begin{equation*}
  \text{slope} = \frac{v_{25} - v_{10}}{15}, \qquad
  \text{intercept} = v_{10} - 10 \cdot \text{slope}.
\end{equation*}
$\mathrm{nt}_{\rm hi}$ is now a \emph{separate} parametric pair and is
no longer required to track $\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$; one Low anchor (C)
deliberately sets $\mathrm{nt}_{\rm hi} < \mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$ at
$E_T = 10$~GeV so that the BDT band $[0.70, 0.80]$ is excluded from
both regions (a \emph{gap} variant). The Cartesian product
$\text{A,B,C} \times \text{X,Y,Z}$ yields nine anchor combinations;
each is reprocessed under both crossing-angle periods, for 18 total
reprocessed cross-sections.

\noindent In the result tables the 9~anchor pairs are identified by a
compact suffix built from the percentage-encoded BDT values:
\begin{center}
\texttt{t\{100\,t$_{10}$\}\_\{100\,t$_{25}$\}\_h\{100\,h$_{10}$\}\_\{100\,h$_{25}$\}\_l\{100\,l$_{10}$\}\_\{100\,l$_{25}$\}}.
\end{center}
For example, \texttt{t80\_70\_h80\_70\_l70\_40} denotes the
A\,$\times$\,X combination (i.e.\ $\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$ drops from
$0.80$ at $E_T = 10$~GeV to $0.70$ at $E_T = 25$~GeV, while
$\mathrm{nt}_{\rm lo}$ drops from $0.70$ to $0.50$).

\section{Pipeline}

\paragraph{Analysis path.} The 18 jobs use the new single-pass
truth-vertex-reweight + DI blending pipeline
(\texttt{oneforall\_tree\_double.sh}):
\texttt{analysis.truth\_vertex\_reweight\_on: 1} with the per-period
factorised reweight file
(\texttt{efficiencytool/truth\_vertex\_reweight/output/\{0mrad,1p5mrad\}/re\-weight.root}),
and the cluster-weighted double-interaction fractions $f_{\rm cluster} = 0.079$
(1.5~mrad) and $0.224$ (0~mrad) propagated through the MC-sample blending.
The legacy reco-vertex reweight is silently forced off when the truth path is on.

\paragraph{Condor.} A new submit file
\texttt{efficiencytool/submit\_ntbdtpair\_di.sub} queues one job per
\texttt{(config, double\_frac)} pair, 18 jobs total. Per-slot resource
request: 8~CPU, 20~GB memory, 30~GB scratch disk. Peak RSS per ROOT
process observed at $\approx 1.0$~GB; each job fans out into 17 concurrent
ROOT processes (8~SI/DI pairs + 1~data = 17). All 18 jobs completed
within 10--30~min of submission.

\section{Code changes}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{efficiencytool/RecoEffCalculator\_TTreeReader.C} — added a
        parametric non-tight lower bound. The macro now reads
        \texttt{analysis.non\_tight.bdt\_min\_intercept} and
        \texttt{bdt\_min\_slope}; if either is absent it falls back to the
        existing flat \texttt{bdt\_min}, so pre-existing configs are
        unaffected.
  \item \texttt{efficiencytool/make\_bdt\_variations.py} — added
        \texttt{OVERRIDE\_MAP} keys for \texttt{nt\_bdt\_min\_intercept},
        \texttt{nt\_bdt\_min\_slope}, \texttt{truth\_vertex\_reweight\_on},
        \texttt{truth\_vertex\_reweight\_file}. A new helper
        \texttt{\_anchors\_to\_param(v10, v25)} converts two anchor values
        into the $(\text{intercept}, \text{slope})$ pair. The 18 variant
        dicts (9~anchor combos $\times$~2 periods) are auto-generated
        from the Cartesian product of the low and high anchor lists.
  \item \texttt{efficiencytool/submit\_ntbdtpair\_di.sub} — new condor
        submit file wiring 18 jobs through
        \texttt{oneforall\_tree\_double.sh} with the appropriate
        per-period \texttt{double\_frac} argument.
\end{itemize}

\section{Interpretation and conclusion}

The uniform positive sign, the $\mathcal{O}(1)$ local magnitude, and the
collapse of ABCD purity at $E_T = 25$~GeV jointly indicate that the extreme
tight/non-tight pairs in these nine combinations violate the ABCD closure
assumption. The mechanism is geometric:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item When $\mathrm{nt}_{\rm lo}$ is pushed up close to
        $\mathrm{t}_{\rm lo}$, the non-tight BDT window becomes narrow
        and sits immediately below the tight region. Signal leakage
        into this band (non-negligible even at nominal thresholds) grows
        rapidly.
  \item The $R$-factor extraction
        $R = B \cdot C / (A \cdot D)$, on which ABCD background
        subtraction depends, becomes statistically poorly determined
        when the $B$ and $C$ populations are dominated by signal-like
        clusters rather than true bulk background.
  \item The high-$E_T$ bins (30, 34~GeV) have the smallest
        statistics in the background control regions, amplifying the
        failure there first.
  \item The truth-vertex-reweight + DI pipeline is unchanged between
        the nominal and the variants, so the universally positive sign
        and consistency between 1.5~mrad and 0~mrad periods rules out
        a pipeline bug.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent \textbf{Conclusion.} These nine $E_T$-parametric tight/non-tight
anchor combinations stress-test ABCD closure at extreme cut values.
The observed $+25$~to~$+55\%$ global cross-section shift and the purity
collapse at $E_T = 25$~GeV (0~mrad) are consistent with ABCD breaking
down in this regime. Accordingly, \emph{these variants are not suitable
for inclusion in the systematic-uncertainty envelope.} They are retained
as cross-check documentation of the analysis' behaviour at extreme
working points; the production \texttt{nt\_bdt} systematic remains the
existing flat-scalar \texttt{purity\_nonclosure\_ntbdt} variation.
